🌸 Medical Ultrasound

🌸 Medical Ultrasound

Definition. Medical ultrasound exploits high-frequency mechanical waves—typically in the range $1\text{ MHz}$ to $20\text{ MHz}$—to image internal body structures. When a wave traveling through a medium of acoustic impedance $Z_1 = \rho_1 v_1$ encounters an interface with a second medium of impedance $Z_2 = \rho_2 v_2$, a portion of the incident intensity is reflected. The intensity reflection coefficient is

$$ R = \left(\frac{Z_2 - Z_1}{Z_2 + Z_1}\right)^2, $$

and the transmission coefficient satisfies $T = 1 - R$. The time elapsed between pulse emission and echo reception, $\Delta t$, determines the depth of the reflecting interface:

$$ d = \frac{v \,\Delta t}{2}, $$

where $v$ is the speed of sound in tissue (approximately $1540\text{ m/s}$ in soft tissue) and the factor of two accounts for the round trip.

Worked Example. A diagnostic transducer emits a pulse that returns an echo $\Delta t = 1.30 \times 10^{-4}\text{ s}$ after emission. Using $v = 1540\text{ m/s}$:

$$ d = \frac{(1540\text{ m/s})(1.30 \times 10^{-4}\text{ s})}{2} = \frac{0.2002\text{ m}}{2} \approx 0.100\text{ m} = 10.0\text{ cm}. $$

The reflecting structure lies $10.0\text{ cm}$ beneath the transducer surface. If that interface separates muscle ($Z_1 = 1.65 \times 10^6\text{ kg m}^{-2}\text{s}^{-1}$) from bone ($Z_2 = 7.80 \times 10^6\text{ kg m}^{-2}\text{s}^{-1}$), then

$$ R = \left(\frac{7.80 - 1.65}{7.80 + 1.65}\right)^2 = \left(\frac{6.15}{9.45}\right)^2 \approx 0.423, $$

meaning approximately $42\%$ of incident intensity reflects at the muscle-bone boundary—sufficient for a strong echo signal.

Key Theorem (Rayleigh Resolution Limit). Two structures separated by distance $\delta$ are spatially resolved only when $\delta \gtrsim \lambda/2$, where $\lambda = v/f$ is the wavelength. Higher frequencies yield shorter wavelengths and therefore finer spatial resolution, but suffer greater attenuation in tissue (attenuation coefficient $\alpha \propto f$), limiting penetration depth. Clinical transducer selection balances these competing demands.

Lab Exercise. Using a water tank and a calibrated ultrasound transducer operating at $5\text{ MHz}$, measure the round-trip travel time to a submerged reflector at three known depths. Plot $d$ versus $\Delta t/2$ and extract $v$ from the slope. Compare your measured value to $1482\text{ m/s}$ (water at $20°\text{C}$) and calculate the percentage error, discussing sources of systematic uncertainty.
