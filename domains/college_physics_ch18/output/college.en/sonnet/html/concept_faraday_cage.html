🌸 Faraday Cage

🌸 Faraday Cage

Definition. A Faraday cage is a closed conducting shell (or sufficiently dense conducting mesh) that shields its interior from external electrostatic fields. The shielding arises from the fundamental property of conductors in electrostatic equilibrium: the electric field inside a conductor is identically zero, and any net charge resides entirely on the outer surface. When an external field $\mathbf{E}_{\text{ext}}$ is applied, free electrons in the conducting shell redistribute until the induced surface charge density $\sigma$ produces an internal field that exactly cancels the external field, yielding $\mathbf{E}_{\text{interior}} = \mathbf{0}$.

By Gauss's law applied to a Gaussian surface entirely within the conductor,

$$ \oint_S \mathbf{E} \cdot d\mathbf{A} = \frac{Q_{\text{enc}}}{\varepsilon_0} = 0, $$

which implies no net charge exists on any interior surface in the absence of interior charges. Consequently, the potential throughout the interior is constant and equal to the potential of the shell.

Worked Example. A spherical copper shell of inner radius $r_1 = 0.10\ \text{m}$ and outer radius $r_2 = 0.12\ \text{m}$ carries a net charge $Q = +5.0\ \mu\text{C}$ and encloses a point charge $q = -2.0\ \mu\text{C}$ at its center. Determine the surface charge densities on the inner and outer surfaces.

By Gauss's law, the inner surface must carry $-q = +2.0\ \mu\text{C}$ so that the field inside the conductor is zero. The remaining charge distributes on the outer surface:

$$ Q_{\text{outer}} = Q - (-q) = 5.0 - 2.0 = +3.0\ \mu\text{C}. $$

The surface charge densities are:

$$ \sigma_{\text{inner}} = \frac{+2.0\times10^{-6}}{4\pi(0.10)^2} \approx 1.59\times10^{-4}\ \text{C/m}^2, \qquad \sigma_{\text{outer}} = \frac{3.0\times10^{-6}}{4\pi(0.12)^2} \approx 1.66\times10^{-4}\ \text{C/m}^2. $$

Key Theorem (Uniqueness of the Interior Field). Given fixed charges inside a grounded conducting shell, the electrostatic potential in the interior region is uniquely determined by the interior charge distribution alone, independent of any external field configuration. This follows directly from the uniqueness theorem for Laplace's equation with Dirichlet boundary conditions.

Lab Exercise. Construct a Faraday cage from aluminum mesh and place a charged electroscope inside. Connect an external Van de Graaff generator and observe that the electroscope deflection remains zero regardless of the generator's operating voltage. Record the mesh wire spacing and estimate the minimum ratio of wire spacing to cage dimension required to maintain effective shielding at the electrostatic level.
