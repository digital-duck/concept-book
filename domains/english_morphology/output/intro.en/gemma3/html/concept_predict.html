🍃 Predict

🍃 Predict

Let's start with something familiar: weather forecasts. You’ve probably seen a forecast saying it will be sunny tomorrow. That’s predicting – making an educated guess about what will happen in the future based on information we have now.

Everyday Example: Imagine you’re baking cookies. You know that if you add more sugar, the cookies will likely taste sweeter. This is a simple prediction!

Picture this: You're watching a baseball game. Based on the batter's recent performance and the pitcher's strategy, you might predict he'll hit another home run. It’s not a certainty, but your guess is informed by what you’ve seen.

Simple Rule: Predicting means using past information to figure out what’s likely to happen next. It’s about finding patterns and making reasonable assumptions. We use words like “likely,” "probably," or "might" because predictions aren't always 100% accurate.

Try it yourself: Think about your morning routine. You probably predict you'll have breakfast, get dressed, and leave for work. Write down three things you predict will happen today – these are simple predictions! Let’s say you predict it will rain later. That prediction is based on the current cloudy sky.
