🍃 Pre-

🍃 Pre-

Everyday example. Think about the word preview. Before you watch a movie, you see a short clip of what's coming — a preview. The tiny piece "pre-" just means before. It sneaks onto the front of a word and shifts its meaning in time.

Picture this. Imagine a timeline stretching left to right. You place an event somewhere on it. Now you stick "pre-" in front of the event's name, and suddenly you've moved to the left — to whatever happened earlier.

$$ \underbrace{\text{pre-}}_{\text{before}} + \underbrace{\text{view}}_{\text{look}} = \underbrace{\text{preview}}_{\text{look before}} $$

Simple rule. Pre- is a prefix — a word-part attached to the beginning of a base word. The formula is:

$$ \text{pre-} + \text{base word} \rightarrow \text{new word meaning "before [base word]"} $$

A few quick examples:

Base word With pre- Meaning
school preschool before regular school
heat preheat heat before using
pay prepay pay before the service

Notice that the base word keeps its original spelling; pre- simply clips onto the front.
