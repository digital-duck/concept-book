🌱 Electric Current

Definition. Electric current is the net rate of flow of electric charge through a cross-sectional area of a conductor. If a charge $\Delta Q$ passes through a surface in a time interval $\Delta t$, the average current is

$$ I_{\text{avg}} = \frac{\Delta Q}{\Delta t} $$

The instantaneous current is defined as the limit

$$ I = \frac{dQ}{dt} $$

The SI unit of current is the ampere (A), where $1\ \text{A} = 1\ \text{C/s}$. By convention, the direction of positive current flow is taken as the direction in which positive charges move, even though in metallic conductors the actual charge carriers are electrons moving in the opposite direction.

Worked Example. A copper wire carries a steady current of $2.50\ \text{A}$. Determine the total charge that passes through a cross-section of the wire in $4.00\ \text{min}$.

Given $I = 2.50\ \text{A}$ and $\Delta t = 4.00\ \text{min} = 240\ \text{s}$, rearranging the definition yields

$$ \Delta Q = I\,\Delta t = (2.50\ \text{A})(240\ \text{s}) = 600\ \text{C} $$

This corresponds to the passage of

$$ N = \frac{\Delta Q}{e} = \frac{600\ \text{C}}{1.602\times10^{-19}\ \text{C}} \approx 3.75\times10^{21}\ \text{electrons} $$

highlighting that even modest macroscopic currents involve an enormous number of charge carriers.

Key Theorem (Continuity of Current). For any steady-state (DC) circuit, the current entering any node equals the current leaving that node. Formally, this is Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL):

$$ \sum_{k} I_k = 0 $$

where currents directed into the node are positive and those directed away are negative. KCL is a direct consequence of conservation of charge: in steady state, charge cannot accumulate at any point in a conductor.

Lab Exercise. Connect a $10\ \Omega$ resistor to a 9 V battery in series with a digital multimeter set to measure current. Record $I$ and compute the predicted value using Ohm's Law ($I = V/R$). Next, insert a second identical resistor in series and repeat. Verify that the measured current decreases to half its original value, confirming that current is identical at every cross-section of a series circuit, consistent with KCL.
