🍃 Chord Construction

When designing anything from a suspension bridge to complex mechanical joints, engineers must accurately determine the straight-line connections—the chords—that span between fixed points. These structural elements are fundamental: they define tension, transfer load, and establish geometric integrity. In mathematics and geometry, chord construction refers to the systematic process of defining or deriving the parameters (coordinates, length, or equation) of a linear segment connecting two given points on a defined plane or curve. It is not merely drawing a line; it is establishing proof that the resulting connection satisfies specific criteria based on the initial input data.

Formally, given two distinct points $A = (x_1, y_1)$ and $B = (x_2, y_2)$ in a Cartesian plane $\mathbb{R}^2$, the chord construction involves defining the line segment $\overline{AB}$ and its associated properties. The core challenge is moving from discrete points to a continuous functional description of the connection.
