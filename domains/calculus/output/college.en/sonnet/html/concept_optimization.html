🍃 Optimization

Definition. Optimization is the process of finding the value(s) of an independent variable at which a given function attains its maximum or minimum value, either over an entire domain (global extremum) or within an open neighborhood (local extremum). Formally, a function $f : I \to \mathbb{R}$ defined on an interval $I$ has a local maximum at $c \in I$ if there exists $\delta > 0$ such that $f(c) \geq f(x)$ for all $x \in (c - \delta, c + \delta) \cap I$; a global maximum requires $f(c) \geq f(x)$ for every $x \in I$. Local minima are defined analogously with the inequality reversed. Points at which $f'(c) = 0$ or $f'(c)$ does not exist are called critical points and are the primary candidates for extrema on open intervals.

Worked Example. Maximize the area of a rectangle inscribed in the ellipse $\dfrac{x^2}{9} + \dfrac{y^2}{4} = 1$, with sides parallel to the coordinate axes.

A vertex in the first quadrant has coordinates $(x, y)$ with $y = \frac{2}{3}\sqrt{9 - x^2}$, so the area is

$$ A(x) = 4xy = \frac{8x}{3}\sqrt{9 - x^2}, \quad x \in (0, 3). $$

Setting $A'(x) = 0$:

$$ A'(x) = \frac{8}{3} \cdot \frac{9 - 2x^2}{\sqrt{9 - x^2}} = 0 \implies x = \frac{3}{\sqrt{2}}. $$

The corresponding $y = \frac{2}{\sqrt{2}} = \sqrt{2}$, giving maximum area $A = 4 \cdot \frac{3}{\sqrt{2}} \cdot \sqrt{2} = 12$.

Key Theorem (Extreme Value Theorem). If $f$ is continuous on a closed bounded interval $[a, b]$, then $f$ attains both a global maximum and a global minimum on $[a, b]$. Consequently, these extrema occur either at interior critical points or at the endpoints $a$ and $b$. To locate global extrema, one evaluates $f$ at all critical points in $(a, b)$ and compares with $f(a)$ and $f(b)$, selecting the largest and smallest values.

Lab Exercise. A farmer has $200\,\text{m}$ of fencing to enclose a rectangular pasture against a straight river (no fence needed along the river). Let $x$ denote the side perpendicular to the river.

  1. Express the enclosed area $A(x)$ in terms of $x$ and state the domain.
  2. Find the critical point of $A$ and verify it yields a maximum using the Second Derivative Test.
  3. State the dimensions that maximize the area and compute that maximum area.
