🌸 Physics Kinematics

Kinematics is the branch of classical mechanics that describes the motion of points and objects without reference to the forces causing that motion. It focuses exclusively on describing position, displacement, velocity, and acceleration over time. Given a particle's instantaneous position $\vec{r}(t)$ in an inertial frame of reference, its state can be fully characterized by differentiation with respect to time $t$. The instantaneous velocity $\vec{v}(t)$ is defined as the first derivative of position:

$$ \vec{v}(t) = \frac{d\vec{r}}{dt} $$

The acceleration $\vec{a}(t)$, which describes how quickly the velocity changes, is the second derivative of position (or the first derivative of velocity):

$$ \vec{a}(t) = \frac{d\vec{v}}{dt} = \frac{d^2\vec{r}}{dt^2} $$

Crucially, kinematics utilizes vector calculus to maintain directional accuracy. Displacement ($\Delta \vec{r}$) is the net change in position, while distance traveled is a scalar quantity representing the total path length covered.
