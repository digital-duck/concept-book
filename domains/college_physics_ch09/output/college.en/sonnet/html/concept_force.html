🌱 Force

🌱 Force

Definition. In classical mechanics, a force is a vector quantity that describes an interaction capable of changing the momentum of an object. Formally, Newton's second law defines net force as

$$ \vec{F}_{\text{net}} = \frac{d\vec{p}}{dt} = m\vec{a} $$

where $\vec{p} = m\vec{v}$ is the linear momentum, $m$ is the inertial mass (assumed constant in non-relativistic contexts), and $\vec{a}$ is the acceleration of the object. The SI unit of force is the newton: $1\,\text{N} = 1\,\text{kg}\cdot\text{m/s}^2$. Force is a free vector applied at a point of contact or acting at a distance (e.g., gravity, electrostatic force), and multiple forces superpose by vector addition: $\vec{F}_{\text{net}} = \sum_i \vec{F}_i$.

Worked Example. A block of mass $m = 5.0\,\text{kg}$ rests on a frictionless horizontal surface. Two horizontal forces act on it: $\vec{F}_1 = 12\,\hat{x}\,\text{N}$ and $\vec{F}_2 = -4\,\hat{x} + 6\,\hat{y}\,\text{N}$. Find the acceleration.

Solution. Compute the net force component-wise:

$$ F_x = 12 + (-4) = 8\,\text{N}, \qquad F_y = 0 + 6 = 6\,\text{N} $$ $$ \vec{F}_{\text{net}} = 8\,\hat{x} + 6\,\hat{y}\,\text{N}, \qquad |\vec{F}_{\text{net}}| = \sqrt{8^2 + 6^2} = 10\,\text{N} $$

Applying Newton's second law:

$$ \vec{a} = \frac{\vec{F}_{\text{net}}}{m} = \frac{8\,\hat{x} + 6\,\hat{y}}{5.0} = 1.6\,\hat{x} + 1.2\,\hat{y}\;\text{m/s}^2 $$

The magnitude of acceleration is $|\vec{a}| = 2.0\,\text{m/s}^2$ directed at $\theta = \arctan(6/8) \approx 36.9°$ above the $x$-axis.

Key Theorem (Newton's Third Law). For every force $\vec{F}_{A \to B}$ exerted by object $A$ on object $B$, there exists a reaction force $\vec{F}_{B \to A} = -\vec{F}_{A \to B}$. These forces are equal in magnitude, opposite in direction, and act on different objects, so they do not cancel when analyzing a single body's free-body diagram.

Identify sources of systematic error, including string mass and pulley friction, and estimate their contribution to the slope deviation from unity.
