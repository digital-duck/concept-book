🌸 Free Body Diagram

🌸 Free Body Diagram

Definition. A free body diagram (FBD) is a schematic representation of a single object isolated from its environment, showing all external forces acting upon it. Each force is drawn as a vector arrow originating at the point of application (or at the center of mass for translational analysis), labeled with its symbol and, where known, its magnitude and direction. The object itself is typically idealized as a point mass or a simple geometric shape. The FBD is the foundational tool for applying Newton's second law:

$$ \sum \vec{F} = m\vec{a}. $$

Only forces exerted on the object by external agents appear in the diagram; internal forces between parts of the object cancel by Newton's third law and are excluded.

Worked Example. Consider a block of mass $m = 5.0\,\text{kg}$ resting on a rough inclined plane inclined at $\theta = 30°$ above the horizontal. To construct the FBD, isolate the block and identify every external force: (1) the gravitational force $\vec{W} = mg\,\hat{j}$ directed vertically downward with magnitude $mg = 49\,\text{N}$; (2) the normal force $\vec{N}$ perpendicular to the incline surface; and (3) the static friction force $\vec{f}_s$ directed up the incline, opposing impending motion. Decomposing along axes parallel ($x'$) and perpendicular ($y'$) to the incline:

$$ \sum F_{x'} = f_s - mg\sin\theta = 0 \implies f_s = (49)\sin 30° = 24.5\,\text{N}, $$ $$ \sum F_{y'} = N - mg\cos\theta = 0 \implies N = (49)\cos 30° \approx 42.4\,\text{N}. $$

Key Theorem. Newton's second law applied to the FBD is both necessary and sufficient for determining the motion of the isolated body, provided the force inventory is complete. Newton's third law guarantees that internal forces — action-reaction pairs between parts of the same body — appear in equal and opposite pairs and cancel identically, so they need not appear in the FBD. This cancellation is what makes the isolation step valid: every force crossing the system boundary is external and must be included; every force confined within is internal and must be excluded.

Lab Exercise. Hang two masses $m_1 = 2.0\,\text{kg}$ and $m_2 = 3.0\,\text{kg}$ over a frictionless pulley (Atwood machine). Draw complete FBDs for each mass separately, define a consistent positive direction, and apply Newton's second law to each FBD to derive expressions for the acceleration $a$ of the system and the tension $T$ in the string. Verify that $T = \frac{2m_1 m_2}{m_1+m_2}g$ and interpret why $T$ differs from both $m_1 g$ and $m_2 g$.
