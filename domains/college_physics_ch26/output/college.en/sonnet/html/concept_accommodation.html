🍃 Accommodation

🍃 Accommodation

Definition. Accommodation is the physiological process by which the crystalline lens of the human eye changes its focal length to maintain a sharp image on the retina as the distance to the viewed object changes. The lens is an elastic, biconvex structure whose curvature is controlled by the ciliary muscles. When these muscles contract, tension on the zonular fibers decreases, allowing the lens to become more convex; this increases its refractive power. Conversely, when the muscles relax, the lens flattens, decreasing its power. The refractive power of a lens is quantified in diopters (D), defined as

$$ P = \frac{1}{f} $$

where $f$ is the focal length in meters. A healthy adult eye adjusts its total refractive power over a range of approximately $4\,\text{D}$ (from roughly $59\,\text{D}$ at rest to $63\,\text{D}$ when fully accommodated), enabling clear vision across object distances from infinity down to the near point, typically about $25\,\text{cm}$.

Worked Example. Suppose an eye at rest has a total power of $P_0 = 60.0\,\text{D}$, corresponding to an image distance (lens-to-retina) of $d_i = 2.00\,\text{cm}$. Determine the required power to view an object at $d_o = 25.0\,\text{cm}$.

Using the thin-lens equation in power form,

$$ P = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{d_i} = \frac{1}{0.250\,\text{m}} + \frac{1}{0.0200\,\text{m}} = 4.00\,\text{D} + 50.0\,\text{D} = 54.0\,\text{D}. $$

Wait — the image distance is fixed by the eyeball's axial length, so $d_i$ remains $2.00\,\text{cm}$, giving $P = 50.0 + 4.0 = 54.0\,\text{D}$. The required increase in power is $\Delta P = 54.0 - 50.0 = 4.0\,\text{D}$, consistent with the known accommodative range.

Key Theorem. The amplitude of accommodation $A$ is defined as

$$ A = P_{\text{near}} - P_{\text{far}} = \frac{1}{d_{\text{near}}} - \frac{1}{d_{\text{far}}} $$

where distances are measured from the lens. $A$ decreases with age due to lens hardening (presbyopia), reaching near zero beyond age 60.

Lab Exercise. Using an optical bench with a variable-power converging lens (to model accommodation), a fixed screen separated by $2.0\,\text{cm}$ from the lens, and object cards at $25\,\text{cm}$, $50\,\text{cm}$, and $100\,\text{cm}$, measure the lens power required at each distance. Plot $P$ versus $1/d_o$ and verify that the relationship is linear with slope 1, confirming the thin-lens power equation.
