🍃 Far Point

🍃 Far Point

Definition. The far point of an eye is the most distant object location that the eye can bring into sharp focus while the ciliary muscles are fully relaxed. For an emmetropic (normal) eye, the far point lies at optical infinity ($d_o \to \infty$), meaning parallel rays entering the eye are focused precisely on the retina without any accommodative effort. The condition is described by the thin-lens equation applied to the eye:

$$ \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{d_i} = \frac{1}{f} $$

where $d_o$ is the object distance, $d_i$ is the image distance (approximately the fixed anterior-to-retina length, $\approx 2.00\ \text{cm}$ for an adult eye), and $f$ is the focal length of the eye's optical system. When $d_o \to \infty$, the equation reduces to $f = d_i$, so the relaxed focal length equals the eye length.

In a myopic (nearsighted) eye, the eyeball is too long or the cornea too curved, causing the relaxed focal length to be shorter than the eye's axial length. The far point falls at a finite distance in front of the eye rather than at infinity.

Worked Example. A myopic patient's far point is measured at $25.0\ \text{cm}$ from the eye. Determine the power of the corrective lens needed (assume the lens sits directly at the eye).

For distant objects ($d_o = \infty$), the corrective lens must produce a virtual image at the far point, so $d_i = -25.0\ \text{cm}$ (negative by sign convention, virtual and on the same side as the object):

$$ P = \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{d_i} = 0 + \frac{1}{-0.250\ \text{m}} = -4.00\ \text{D} $$

A diverging lens of power $-4.00\ \text{diopters}$ is prescribed.

Key Theorem. The corrective lens power for myopia is $P = -1/|d_{fp}|$, where $d_{fp}$ is the far-point distance in meters. This result follows directly from requiring the lens to produce a virtual image at the patient's far point for an infinitely distant object.

Lab Exercise. Using an optical bench, a converging lens to model the eye, and an adjustable screen representing the retina, experimentally locate the far point of a simulated myopic eye by sliding the screen until it is $3\ \text{mm}$ beyond the lens's natural focal length. Record the corrective lens power required to restore focus for a distant object and compare with the theoretical prediction.
