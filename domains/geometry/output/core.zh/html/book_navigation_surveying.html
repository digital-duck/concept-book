🌱 Distance

Imagine you’re planning a road trip. You need to know how far it is between cities – that's where distance comes in. In everyday life, we use distance intuitively: “It’s about 10 miles to the store,” or “The mountain range is hundreds of kilometers away.” But what *is* distance precisely? Let’s unpack this fundamental concept, building a solid understanding from the ground up.

Definition:

In mathematics, particularly in geometry and physics, ‘distance’ represents the length of the path between two points. It’s a scalar quantity – meaning it only has magnitude (a numerical value) and no direction. We denote the distance between two points $A$ and $B$ as $d(A, B)$.

Worked Example:

Let's say we have a point $A$ located at coordinates $(2, 3)$ in a Cartesian plane, and another point $B$ located at coordinates $(5, 7)$. We can use the distance formula to calculate the distance between these two points. The distance formula is derived from the Pythagorean theorem:

$d(A, B) = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$

Plugging in our coordinates:

$d(A, B) = \sqrt{(5 - 2)^2 + (7 - 3)^2} = \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = \sqrt{9 + 16} = \sqrt{25} = 5$

Therefore, the distance between points $A(2, 3)$ and $B(5, 7)$ is 5 units. This result aligns with our intuitive understanding of a straight-line separation.

Why it’s true:

The distance formula relies on the Pythagorean theorem, which states that in a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle) is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides. This fundamental geometric principle ensures the accuracy of our distance calculations when dealing with straight line segments.

🌱 Angle

🌱 角 (Jiǎo)

想象一下，你正在设计一个房屋。你需要精确地测量墙壁之间的角度，以确保房间的布局合理、美观。或者，你是一名导航员，需要根据指南针和地图确定航向。这些场景都需要我们理解并能够使用“角”的概念。

定义 (Dìngyì) – 定义

在几何学中，一个角是由两条直线或射线组成的形状。它通常由两个终点定义——这两个端点是线段的顶点（顶点）。 我们用符号 $\angle$ 表示一个角，并用三个字母表示它，这三个字母之间用逗号隔开，第一个字母代表顶点，第二个字母代表与该顶点相邻的一条直线或射线，第三个字母代表与该顶点相对的一条直线或射线。例如，我们可以将这个角命名为 $\angle ABC$，其中 B 是顶点，AB 和 BC 是两条组成角的直线或射线。角度的度数是一个用于衡量角的尺寸的重要单位。

例子 (Lìzi): – 举例说明

假设你有一张纸，你可以用直尺在纸上画一条线段。然后，你可以从该点（例如，线的端点）画另一条线段，形成一个角。 在这个例子中，如果两条线段的夹角是 30 度，那么这个角就叫做 $\angle BAC$，其中 A 是顶点，AB 和 AC 是两条组成角的射线。

为什么它是真的 (Wèi shénme shì zhēn de) – Why it's true

我们使用“角”来描述两个线段或射线之间的夹角。 角度的度数是基于圆周的概念。 一个完整的圆形有 360 度。 将一个角分配到圆周上，可以形成扇形。 角的大小与扇形所占圆周的比例成正比。 这种几何直觉为我们测量和理解角度提供了基础。

练习 (Liànxí) – Practice problem

在 $\triangle ABC$ 中，$\angle A = 60^\circ$ 和 $\angle B = 80^\circ$，求 $\angle C$ 的度数。 (a) 30 度 (b) 100 度 (c) 200 度 (d) 40 度

🌱 Point

🌱 点 (Diǎn) – Point

Imagine you’re a cartographer, meticulously drawing a map. You need to precisely mark a specific location – let's say, the corner where two roads meet. You wouldn’t just draw a vague blob; you’d use a point, a symbol representing a single, definite position in space. This fundamental idea is at the heart of geometry and forms the basis for much more complex concepts. In Chinese mathematics, we call this “点” (diǎn), meaning ‘point’.

Definition:

In Euclidean geometry, a point is defined as a location in space that has no dimension. It’s represented by a symbol, typically a dot (•). Crucially, a point doesn't *occupy* any area or volume; it simply exists as a reference for defining other geometric objects like lines and shapes. Think of it as an infinitely small marker – the closest analogy is a single pixel on a screen.

Worked Example:

Consider two lines intersecting at a specific location. We represent this intersection with two points: $A$ and $B$. The point $B$ is the unique location where these two lines cross each other. We can accurately describe their relative positions using the point $B$.

Why it's true:

The concept of a point arises from our need to precisely locate things. Without a way to distinguish *one* specific position from another, we couldn’t build up more complex shapes or perform calculations involving distance and angles. The assumption that a point has no dimension is a foundational one; it allows us to rigorously define geometric relationships. It's an abstraction essential for building a logical framework.

🍃 Angle Measure

🍃 角测量 (Jiǎ Jù Cèliǎo)

Imagine you’re a surveyor, tasked with accurately mapping out a new parcel of land. You need to know exactly how much space is covered by different angles – this is where angle measurement comes in. It's a fundamental concept in geometry and trigonometry, allowing us to quantify turns and rotations. Let’s break down what it means precisely.

Definition

An *angle* is formed when two rays (线段, xiànduàn) share a common endpoint called the vertex (顶点, diàojī). We measure the size of an angle using degrees (°). A full circle has 360°; therefore, a straight line forms an angle of 180°, and a half-circle forms an angle of 90°. Angles are typically measured as the smaller of the two acute angles formed at the vertex.

Worked Example

Consider a triangle. Let’s say one of its angles measures 60°. We can use this to determine the other angles. The sum of the interior angles in any triangle is always 180°. If angle A = 60° and angle B = x°, then:

$60° + x° + C° = 180°$, where C represents the third angle.

Solving for *x*, we get: $x° = 180° - 60° - C°$. For example, if C° = 45°, then x° = 75°.

为什么它是真的 (Wèi shénme shì zhèng de – Why it's true)

This rule – the sum of angles in a triangle is 180° – stems from our understanding of parallel lines and transversals. When a straight line intersects two parallel lines, alternate interior angles are equal. This geometric framework allows us to calculate unknown angles within triangles with precision.

练习题 (Liànxí tí – Practice problem)

A rectangle has four right angles. If one of the acute angles measures 30°, what is the measure of the other acute angle? (a) 30° (b) 60° (c) 90° (d) 150°

🌱 Line

🌱 线 (Xiàn – Line)

Imagine you’re an architect designing a building. You need to draw straight lines across blueprints, ensuring walls are perfectly aligned and windows precisely positioned. Or consider a surveyor mapping out a field; they rely on lines to define boundaries with incredible accuracy. These seemingly simple shapes—lines—are fundamental to geometry and underpin countless aspects of our world. Let's explore what exactly constitutes a line.

Definition: In geometry, a line is defined as a set of points that are all equidistant from a fixed point called the *center* or *midpoint*. We represent this line using the symbol $l$. It’s crucial to understand that a line isn’t a physical object; it's an abstract concept—a collection of infinitely many points arranged in a specific manner.

Worked Example: Let’s say point *A* is the midpoint of line $l$. Points *B*, *C*, and *D* lie on line $l$ such that $AB = AC = AD = 2$ units. The length of the line segment $BD$ is 4 units.

Why it's true: This definition relies on the concept of *distance*. Distance is inherently defined as the shortest path between two points. Since every point on line $l$ is equidistant from *A*, *A* effectively acts as a balancing point, ensuring that the distances remain constant regardless of where we measure along the line. The resulting length of BD follows directly from properties of segment lengths and ratios.

Practice Problem: Point *M* is the midpoint of line $m$. Points *N*, *O*, and *P* lie on line $m$ such that $MN = MO = MP = 3$. What is the length of $NO$?

🍃 Line Segment

🍃 线段 (Xiànduàn)

Let’s consider a scenario: You're an architect designing a building, needing to ensure a support beam spans precisely the right length across a room. Or perhaps you’re a surveyor mapping out a property boundary – accurately measuring the distance between two points on the ground. These situations all involve understanding and working with *line segments*.

Definition: A line segment is a part of a straight line that has two distinct endpoints. We denote a line segment as $\overline{AB}$, where $A$ and $B$ are the endpoints. The distance between the endpoints, often denoted as $|AB|$, represents the length of the line segment. Importantly, the order of the endpoints matters – $\overline{AB}$ is not the same as $\overline{BA}$.

Worked Example: Let’s say you have a line segment with endpoint $A$ at coordinates $(2, 1)$ and endpoint $B$ at coordinates $(6, 5)$. To find the length of $\overline{AB}$, we use the distance formula:

$|AB| = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$

Substituting our coordinates:

$|AB| = \sqrt{(6 - 2)^2 + (5 - 1)^2} = \sqrt{4^2 + 4^2} = \sqrt{16 + 16} = \sqrt{32} = 4\sqrt{2}$

Therefore, the length of line segment $\overline{AB}$ is $4\sqrt{2}$.

Why it’s True: The distance formula is derived from the Pythagorean theorem. The x and y differences represent the legs of a right triangle formed by the line segment, and the square root calculates the hypotenuse – which represents the straight-line distance between the endpoints. This application of the Pythagorean theorem guarantees that we’re calculating the shortest possible distance between two points in a plane.

Practice Problem: Consider a line segment with endpoints $C$ at $( -1, 3)$ and $D$ at $(3, -2)$. Calculate the length of $\overline{CD}$. Show your work!

🍃 Polygon

🍃 多边形 (Duōbiānxíng – Polygon)

Let’s start with a familiar shape: a fence. Perhaps you've seen one enclosing a field, or even just a simple square made of wooden panels. All fences, in their essence, are constructed from polygons. Polygons are fundamental shapes in geometry and appear everywhere – from the patterns on fabrics to the design of buildings. Understanding them is crucial for navigating more complex geometric concepts.

定义 (Dìngyì – Definition)

A polygon is a closed two-dimensional shape formed by straight line segments connected end-to-end. “Closed” means the last line segment connects back to the first, creating a complete loop. “Two-dimensional” signifies it exists on a flat surface. "Straight line segments" are key; curves aren’t allowed. More formally, a polygon is a finite set of $n$ sides, where each side is a straight line segment. The vertices (corners) of the polygon are the points where these line segments meet.

例子 (Lìzi – Worked Example)

Consider a pentagon. A pentagon has five sides and five vertices. We can represent it with the following vertices in a Cartesian plane: $A(1, 1)$, $B(4, 1)$, $C(4, 4)$, $D(1, 4)$, and $E(1, 1)$. Notice that connecting these points creates a closed shape – you can trace your finger along the line segments and return to your starting point.

为什么成立 (Wèishénme chélìng – Why it’s true)

The definition hinges on the requirement of “straight line segments.” By definition, a straight line segment is a line with no curves. A closed shape formed from these segments *must* form a complete loop. Without this constraint, you could create an open figure with curved lines that wouldn't qualify as a polygon. The “closed” aspect ensures the shape occupies a defined area and allows for geometric calculations – like calculating its perimeter or area.

🍃 Rigid Motion

🍃 刚性运动

想象一下你正在搭建一个复杂的木结构，比如一座精美的模型船。如果你的手在搭建过程中突然移动了，船的形状可能会发生微妙的变化，导致最终的模型与你最初的设计大相径庭。这正是“刚性运动”的核心概念：确保在力的作用下，物体的形状保持不变。

定义 (Definition)

刚性运动（Rigid Motion）指的是在施加力或扭矩时，一个物体能够保持其形状和尺寸不变的运动状态。换句话说，我们假设物体是坚硬且不易变形的，因此它的各个部分之间保持着严格的连接，并且它们会以相同的方式响应外力的作用。 实际上，刚性运动是一个理想化的模型，可以帮助我们分析和理解复杂物体的行为。

示例 (Worked Example)

考虑一个正方形木块。如果我们用一个垂直于正方形面的力来施加压力，那么正方形的每个角都将受到相同的压力。 由于正方形的形状是固定的，因此它不会发生任何形变。 这就是刚性运动的一个简单例子。 想象一下你用手按压一个硬纸板的正方形，它不会弯曲或变形。

为什么它是真的 (Why it's true)

刚性运动的概念建立在微积分和力学的基础之上。 当物体受到外力作用时，力的产生会引起物体的内部位移。 假设物体是刚性的，我们就可以用以下公式来描述： $F = k \Delta x$, 其中 $F$ 是施加的力，$k$ 是材料的刚性系数，$\Delta x$ 是物体在变形下的位移。 如果 $\Delta x$ 很小，那么我们可以近似认为 $F \approx k \Delta x$， 证明了在微小的变形下，力的产生与变形成正比。

练习 (Practice Problem)

一个质量为 $m$ 的均匀球体，静止在一个水平桌面上。如果用一个力 $F$ 推动球体，那么球体的重心会发生怎样的位移？ (提示：考虑重力和推力之间的平衡)

🍃 Triangle

🍃 三角形

我们每天都能看到三角形，从建筑的屋顶到植物的叶子，再到我们的身体。它是一种非常基础且重要的几何形状。理解三角形的概念是学习更高级的几何知识的基础。

定义 (Definition)

在几何学中，三角形是由线段组成的三条线的封闭图形。这些线段必须两两相交，形成三个角。 三角形的关键特征在于其边和角之间的关系。根据边的不同，三角形可以分为以下几种类型：

  • 等边三角形 (Equilateral Triangle): 三条边都相等。
  • 等腰三角形 (Isosceles Triangle): 两条或多条边相等。
  • 直角三角形 (Right-angled Triangle): 包含一个90°的角。

    • 实例 (Worked Example)

    假设我们有一个三角形，其中两条边的长度分别为 $a = 5$ cm 和 $b = 7$ cm，且这两条边的夹角为 $\theta = 60^\circ$。我们可以使用余弦定理（Law of Cosines）来计算第三条边 $c$ 的长度：

    $$c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab \cos(\theta)$$

    将数值代入：

    $$c^2 = 5^2 + 7^2 - 2(5)(7) \cos(60^\circ)$$ $$c^2 = 25 + 49 - 70 (\frac{1}{2})$$ $$c^2 = 74 - 35$$ $$c^2 = 39$$

    因此，$c = \sqrt{39} \approx 6.24$ cm。

    为什么成立 (Why it's true)

    余弦定理是三角学中一个重要的结果，它是基于几何直觉和三角形面积公式推导出来的。 它提供了一种计算任意三角形边长的有效方法，特别是当角度已知时。 三角形的面积可以表示为 $\frac{1}{2}ab\sin(\theta)$ ，将这个公式结合到余弦定理中，我们可以得到一个更全面的理解。

    练习 (Practice Problem)

    一个直角三角形的两条直角边分别为3cm和4cm, 求斜边的长度.

    🍃 Congruence

    🍃 Congruence

    Imagine you have two identical puzzles – let’s say jigsaw puzzles of a landscape scene. You carefully assemble one puzzle, then find another exactly the same. You could simply state they are “similar,” but that doesn't fully capture what makes them so alike. Congruence is the precise mathematical statement that says these puzzles are *identical* in shape and size. It’s a fundamental concept in geometry, describing when two shapes are exactly the same – not just similar, but perfectly overlapping if you were to place them on top of each other.

    Definition: Two geometric figures (polygons, lines, angles, etc.) are said to be congruent if and only if one can be superimposed onto the other so that their corresponding vertices coincide. In simpler terms, they have the same shape and size. This means all their corresponding sides and angles must be equal.

    Worked Example: Consider two triangles, $\triangle ABC$ and $\triangle DEF$. We are given that $AB = DE$, $BC = EF$, and $CA = FD. $ Since all corresponding sides are equal, we can conclude that $\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DEF$. This notation ($\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DEF$) is the standard way to express congruence – it means “triangle ABC is congruent to triangle DEF.”

    Why it’s true: The key here is superposition. Because the shapes have identical dimensions, we can physically slide one around until its vertices align perfectly with the other. This isn't possible if they were just similar; a slight change in size would prevent this perfect overlap. Using the properties of rigid transformations (translations, rotations, and reflections) allows us to demonstrate that congruent figures are fundamentally indistinguishable.

    Practice Problem: Given rectangle $ABCD$ with $AB = 5$, $BC = 3$, and rectangle $EFGH$ with $EH = 10$, $FG = 6$, determine if $\triangle ABC \cong \triangle EHG$. Explain your reasoning. (Hint: Carefully consider the corresponding sides and angles).

    🍃 Area

    🍃 Area

    Imagine you're a landscape architect designing a beautiful garden. You need to know how much grass to plant, or how much stone to lay for a path. Calculating the amount of material needed is fundamentally about finding *area* – the measure of a two-dimensional surface. Area tells us how much space something occupies. It’s a concept we use constantly in everyday life and forms a cornerstone of many mathematical and scientific disciplines. Let's explore this vital idea.

    Definition

    Mathematically, area is defined as the amount of two-dimensional space enclosed by a closed curve or surface. It’s typically measured in square units – such as square meters ($m^2$), square feet ($ft^2$), or square centimeters ($cm^2$). We represent the area of a shape with the symbol $A$.

    Worked Example

    Let's consider a simple rectangle. A rectangle has two pairs of equal sides: length ($l$) and width ($w$). The area of this rectangle is given by the formula:

    $A = l \times w$

    Imagine a rectangle with a length of 5 meters and a width of 3 meters. To find its area, we substitute these values into the formula:

    $A = 5 \, m \times 3 \, m = 15 \, m^2$

    Therefore, the area of this rectangle is 15 square meters.

    Why it's True

    This formula arises from a basic understanding of multiplication. Imagine dividing the rectangle into smaller squares. The number of squares along the length multiplied by the number of squares along the width gives you the total number of small squares that make up the rectangle – which is precisely the area. This concept extends to more complex shapes through integration, but the underlying principle of repeated multiplication remains central.

    Practice Problem

    A square has a side length of 4 cm. Calculate its area. Express your answer in square centimeters ($cm^2$).

    🍃 Triangle Congruence Criteria

    🍃 三角形全等判定标准

    想象一下，你是一名建筑师，需要设计一座桥梁。为了确保桥梁的结构安全和美观，你需要精确地测量和构建它的各个部分。但仅仅知道三角形的边长是不够的，因为可能存在一些看似相同的三角形实际上并不相同的情况。这就是“三角形全等”的概念出现的原因。

    定义： 两个三角形被称为全等的，如果它们具有以下一种或多种情况：

  • SSS (边边边) 全等: 三条边的长度完全相等。
  • SAS (两边角对应) 全等: 两条边的长度以及这两条边所对应的角的度数完全相等。
  • ASA (两角对应) 全等: 两角的大小以及这两个角所对的边长完全相等。
  • AAS (两角和一边) 全等: 两个角的面积以及这两个角所对的边长完全相等。
  • RHS (直角-斜边-夹角) 全等 （仅适用于直角三角形）：如果两个直角三角形有两条直角边长度相等，且一个三角形有一个角是直角（即另一个三角形也必须有一个角为直角），那么这两个三角形全等。

    • 例子： 假设我们有两个三角形 $ABC$ 和 $DEF$，其中 $AB = DE = 5$, $BC = EF = 7$, $AC = DF = 10$ 并且 $\angle B = \angle E$, $\angle C = \angle F$. 那么根据SSS全等判定标准，我们知道三角形 $ABC$ 和 $DEF$ 是全等的。

    为什么它成立： 这基于尺规作图的原理。我们可以使用两条边和它们的夹角来精确地构造出另一个完全相同的三角形。这证明了在满足给定条件时，两个三角形必然是全等的。换句话说，这些条件定义了一个独特的形状，任何符合这些条件的形状都必须是全等形的。

    练习题： 已知三角形 $ABC$ 中，$AB = 4$, $BC = 5$, $\angle B = 60^\circ$，和三角形 $DEF$ 中，$DE = 4$, $EF = 5$, $\angle E = 60^\circ$。请判断这两个三角形是否全等，并说明理由。

    🍃 Pythagorean Theorem

    🍃 피타고라스 정리

    想象一下，你是一位建筑师，正在设计一座三角形的建筑物。你需要计算斜坡（即屋顶）的长度，这不仅仅是测量两边的长度就能完成的。 这时候，我们引入一个至关重要的定理——피타고라스 정리 (Pythagorean Theorem)。

    정의: 피타고라스 정리는 직각삼각형에서 두 변의 길이와 가장 긴 변（직각을 이루는 변） 사이에는特定的关系存在。具体来说，如果一个直角三角形的两个边长分别为 *a* 和 *b*，那么它们斜边的长度为 *c*，则满足以下公式：

    $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$

    换句话说，直角三角形两条直角边的平方和等于斜边的平方。

    例题: 假设一个直角三角形的两条直角边长分别为 3 米和 4 米。那么它的斜边长是多少？

    使用피타고라스 정리:

  • $a = 3$
  • $b = 4$
  • $c^2 = a^2 + b^2 = 3^2 + 4^2 = 9 + 16 = 25$
  • 所以，$c = \sqrt{25} = 5$ 米。

    • 因此，斜边长为 5 米。

    为什么它是真的？ 这个定理的证明有很多种方法，其中一种比较直观的方式是通过面积计算。我们可以将直角三角形分割成两个更小的直角三角形（或等腰直角三角形），然后通过计算它们的面积来推导出 $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$ 这个关系。 这种方法虽然涉及一些几何图形的理解，但能帮助我们直观地认识到这个定理的内在逻辑。

    练习题: 一个直角三角形的两条直角边长分别为 6 厘米和 8 厘米，那么它的斜边长是多少？ 请使用피타고라스 정리计算。

    🍃 Similarity

    🍃 相似性 (Xiāngsìng – Similarity)

    Imagine you’re a detective examining two crime scenes. You notice that a muddy footprint at one location bears an uncanny resemblance to another footprint found miles away. Or perhaps a skilled architect is reviewing blueprints for a building and comparing them to historical designs, searching for echoes of past styles. These scenarios illustrate the core idea behind similarity: determining how alike two objects or sets are. It’s about more than just looking identical; it's about capturing the *essential* relationships between things.

    定义 (Dìngyì – Definition)

    Formally, we say that two shapes or sets are similar if they are proportional to each other. This means that corresponding lengths are in the same ratio. Let’s consider two triangles, $\triangle ABC$ and $\triangle A'B'C'$, where $A'$ is the reflection of $A$ across a line through $B'$. If $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle A'B'C'$, then this signifies their similarity. The notation $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle A'B'C'$ reads “triangle ABC is similar to triangle A’B’C’.”

    示例 (Lìziyì – Worked Example)

    Let’s consider two rectangles, Rectangle 1 with dimensions $AB = 4$ and $BC = 6$, and Rectangle 2 with dimensions $A'B' = 8$ and $B'C' = 12$. Note that $\angle ABC = \angle A'B'C'$ (they are right angles). Since $\frac{AB}{A'B'} = \frac{4}{8} = \frac{1}{2}$ and $\frac{BC}{B'C'} = \frac{6}{12} = \frac{1}{2}$, we have $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle A'B'C'$ because their corresponding sides are proportional.

    为什么是真的 (Wèishénme shì zhēn de – Why it’s true)

    The concept of similarity is rooted in the idea of scaling. If two shapes are similar, one can be obtained from the other by uniform scaling—stretching or shrinking without distortion. This means that their angles remain equal, and their side lengths are related by a constant factor. This proportionality ensures that the fundamental geometric relationships between these shapes are preserved.

    练习 (Liànxí – Practice Problem)

    Consider two circles with radii $r_1 = 5$ and $r_2 = 10$. A chord of length $l_1$ in the first circle forms a chord of length $l_2$ in the second circle. Prove that the two circles are similar. (Hint: Consider the angles subtended by the chords).

    🍃 Coordinate Plane

    🍃 坐标系

    Imagine you’re trying to give someone very specific directions – not just “turn left,” but precisely how many blocks to turn, and in what direction. Without a way to pinpoint locations relative to a known reference point, it would be incredibly difficult. This is where the coordinate plane comes in; it provides that precise framework for locating points in space.

    Definition: The coordinate plane, also known as the Cartesian plane, is a two-dimensional system used to represent and analyze spatial relationships. It’s formed by two perpendicular lines called the x-axis (horizontal) and the y-axis (vertical). The point where these axes intersect is called the origin, denoted by $(0, 0)$. Each point on the coordinate plane is identified by an ordered pair of numbers – $(x, y)$ – representing its horizontal distance ($x$) from the origin and its vertical distance ($y$) from the origin. The $x$-axis increases to the right, while the y-axis increases upwards.

    Worked Example: Let’s locate the point ‘A’ on the coordinate plane. We are given that A is 3 units to the right of the origin along the x-axis and 4 units above the origin along the y-axis. Therefore, the coordinates of point A are $(3, 4)$.

    Why it's True: This system works because it’s based on a fundamental principle: we can divide space into equal intervals in both horizontal and vertical directions. The number of units we choose for each interval (commonly 1, but can be any positive value) determines the scale of the coordinate plane. By consistently using these intervals, we create a reliable and repeatable method for describing location, regardless of where we are on the plane.

    Practice Problem: A point is located at the coordinates $(-2, 1)$ on the coordinate plane. Describe its location relative to the origin: it’s how many units to the left/right and how many units up/down from the origin.

    🍃 Triangle Similarity Criteria

    🍃 三角形相似性准则 (Triangular Similarity Criteria)

    Imagine you’re an architect designing a bridge. You need to ensure that different sections of the bridge maintain proper proportions and angles – even as the overall size changes. This is where triangle similarity comes in handy. It allows us to determine if two triangles are similar, which means they have the same shape but can be scaled (made larger or smaller) without changing their form. Understanding this concept is crucial not just for architecture but also for fields like navigation and even computer graphics.

    定义 (Definition): 两个三角形 $ABC$ 和 $A'B'C'$ 是相似的，如果它们之间存在以下对应关系：

  • $\angle A \cong \angle A'$, $\angle B \cong \angle B'$, 以及 $\angle C \cong \angle C'$.

    • 这意味着这两个三角形具有相同的内角。 此外，对应边也成比例，即 $\frac{AB}{A'B'} = \frac{BC}{B'C'} = \frac{AC}{A'C'}$。

    示例 (Worked Example):

    Consider triangles $ABC$ and $DEF$. We are given that $\angle A = \angle D$, $\angle B = \angle E$, and $\angle C = \angle F$. Let $AB = 3$, $BC = 4$, and $AC = 5$. Also, let $DE = 6$, $EF = 8$, and $DF = 10$.

    Since the corresponding angles are equal, we can conclude that triangles $ABC$ and $DEF$ are similar. We can calculate the ratio of corresponding sides: $\frac{AB}{DE} = \frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{2}$ $\frac{BC}{EF} = \frac{4}{8} = \frac{1}{2}$ $\frac{AC}{DF} = \frac{5}{10} = \frac{1}{2}$

    Since the ratios are equal, $ABC \sim DEF$.

    为什么它是真的 (Why It's True): 相似性源于角度的保持不变。如果两个三角形共享一个角，并且其余角也相等，那么它们的形状必然相同。 这种保证基于几何中的基本原理，即对应角相等的三角形是相似的。这与平行线的性质相关联，它允许我们推导出相似三角形的关系。

    练习 (Practice Problem):

    Given that $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle XYZ$, with $AB = 5$, $BC = 8$, $CA = 6$ and $XY = 10$, $YZ = 16$, $XZ = 12$. Determine if the triangles are similar. Explain your reasoning.

    🍃 Distance Formula

    🍃 Distance Formula

    Imagine you’re a surveyor tasked with measuring the length of a parcel of land. You don't have a tape measure that can span across the entire property at once. Instead, you can strategically place stakes and use triangles to calculate distances – this is where the distance formula comes in handy! It provides a powerful way to find the straight-line distance between any two points in a coordinate plane.

    Definition: The distance formula calculates the straight-line distance between two points, let’s call them $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$, using the Pythagorean theorem. Specifically, it’s defined as:

    $d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$

    Where *d* represents the distance between the two points. This formula breaks down the straight line into horizontal and vertical components, squares those components, adds them together, and then takes the square root – mirroring how the Pythagorean theorem works for right-angled triangles.

    Worked Example: Let’s say we have two points: A(1, 2) and B(4, 6). We want to find the distance between A and B.

    Using the formula: $d = \sqrt{(4 - 1)^2 + (6 - 2)^2}$ $d = \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2}$ $d = \sqrt{9 + 16}$ $d = \sqrt{25}$ $d = 5$

    Therefore, the distance between points A(1, 2) and B(4, 6) is 5 units.

    Why it’s True: This formula is rooted in the Pythagorean theorem ($a^2 + b^2 = c^2$). Each point can be viewed as a vertex of a right triangle where the x-distance between points is one leg (*a*), the y-distance is another leg (*b*), and the distance between the points themselves is the hypotenuse (*c*) of that triangle. Squaring each side and adding them accurately applies this theorem to find the overall length.

    Practice Problem: Find the distance between the points C(-2, 3) and D(1, -5). Show your work.

    🍃 Trigonometric Ratios

    🍃 三角函数比

    我们已经学习了直角三角形的基本概念，包括斜边、直角和两个锐角。现在，我们将探索一种强大的工具——三角函数比，它允许我们在直角三角形中测量角度并计算未知边长。这种能力在导航、工程以及物理学等众多领域都至关重要。

    定义

    三角函数比是使用直角三角形的两个特定角的正弦、余弦和正切。 它们分别是：

  • 正弦 (sine): sin(θ) = \(\frac{相对边}{斜边}\)
  • 余弦 (cosine): cos(θ) = \(\frac{邻近边}{斜边}\)
  • 正切 (tangent): tan(θ) = \(\frac{相对边}{邻近边}\)

    • 其中，θ 代表直角三角形的那个角度。

    例子

    假设我们有一个直角三角形，其中一个锐角（让我们称之为 θ）等于 30°，斜边长度为 10。 我们需要计算出对边 (相对边) 的长度。

    根据定义，cos(30°) = \(\frac{邻近边}{斜边}\)。 我们知道斜边是10，所以：

    cos(30°) = \(\frac{邻近边}{10}\)。

    我们知道 cos(30°) = \(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\)，因此：

    \(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} = \frac{邻近边}{10}\)

    求解邻近边: 邻近边 = \(10 * \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} = 5\sqrt{3}\)。

    为什么有效？

    这些比率之所以有效，是因为它们基于直角三角形的定义。 三角函数比是角度与直角三角形之间关系的一致表示。 通过使用这些比率，我们可以建立一个简单的方程并求解未知量。

    练习题

    在直角三角形 ABC 中，∠A = 60°，AB = 8。 计算 BC 的长度（BC 是斜边）。 (a) 利用 cos(60°) = \(\frac{邻近边}{斜边}\)。 (b) 求解方程并计算 BC 的值。

    🌸 Navigation Surveying

    🌸 导航测量 (Navigation Surveying)

    Imagine you’re a captain charting a course across the vast ocean, or an engineer surveying a remote construction site – both rely on understanding and utilizing precise location information. Navigation surveying is fundamentally about determining position and direction using celestial observations, terrestrial measurements, and increasingly, electronic instruments. It's not just about knowing *where* something is; it’s about knowing *how to get there*, and accurately representing that spatial relationship.

    定义 (Definition)

    Navigation surveying encompasses a suite of techniques designed to ascertain geographic coordinates – typically latitude and longitude – of a specific point on the Earth’s surface. Traditionally, this involved using celestial bodies like the sun, moon, and stars as reference points. Modern navigation surveying also incorporates terrestrial measurements such as angles and distances, combined with sophisticated electronic instruments like GPS receivers. The goal is always to establish a precise spatial reference frame, allowing for accurate mapping, positioning, and route planning.

    示例 (Worked Example)

    Let’s consider a simplified scenario. A surveyor wants to determine the latitude of a point on Earth. Using a sextant, they measure the angle from Polaris – the North Star – to the horizon at a specific time. Knowing the local apparent position of Polaris – which is highly dependent on the observer's location and the time of year – allows the surveyor to calculate the latitude using spherical trigonometry. The precise formula involves the Earth’s radius, the observed angle, and the Greenwich Mean Time (GMT).

    为什么成立 (Why it’s true)

    This method fundamentally relies on the geometry of a sphere (the Earth) and the principles of celestial navigation. The angles measured with the sextant are directly related to the angular distance between the observer's position and the celestial body. Because the Earth is approximately spherical, these angles can be mathematically converted into latitude – the angular distance north or south of the equator – using established formulas derived from trigonometry.

    练习问题 (Practice Problem)

    A surveyor measures an angle of 60 degrees to the North Star with a sextant. The local time is 14:00 GMT. Assuming a standard Earth radius of $6371 \text{ km}$, calculate the approximate latitude of the observation point in degrees. （提示：需要使用天文历表数据来确定Polaris的观测角度。）

    Payoff 🎯

    Navigation surveying represents the culmination of our journey into understanding spatial relationships and measurement. It’s not simply about taking angles and distances; it's about transforming that raw data into a precise, actionable representation of an environment – a navigable map or a detailed record for informed decision-making. We’ve built towards this by meticulously defining key concepts like coordinate systems, triangulation, resectioning, and error analysis – each step refining our ability to accurately locate and describe points in space.

    Why is navigation surveying the natural endpoint? Because it takes all the foundational knowledge we've acquired and applies it directly to a critical real-world problem: determining location and creating reliable maps. Prior to this, we’ve been laying the groundwork for understanding how observations relate to spatial positions. Now, we’re synthesizing that understanding into tangible results.

    Connecting to Applications: Navigation surveying is intrinsically linked to all the applications explored throughout this concept book. Consider land development – accurate property boundaries and site layouts rely heavily on resectioning techniques derived from our foundational principles. In environmental monitoring, precise location data gathered using navigation surveying allows us to track changes in ecosystems and identify areas of concern. Similarly, within construction, it ensures correct placement of infrastructure elements, utilizing coordinate systems for efficient planning and execution. Even in archaeology, the careful triangulation and resectioning methods are fundamental for mapping ancient sites with accuracy.

The angle $\angle ABC$ is 60 degrees, and the distance BC is 100 meters. Assuming a horizontal datum, calculate the approximate coordinates of Point A.
