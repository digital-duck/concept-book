🍃 字

Everyday example. Think about how families grow — a child is born inside a home, and that home now holds new meaning. The Chinese character 字 (zì) captures exactly this image: it means written character or word, and its shape tells a little story about creation.

Picture this. Look at 字 and notice its two parts. On top sits 宀 (mián), a small roof — picture a cozy house. Underneath sits 子 (zǐ), which means child. A child sheltered under a roof. The ancient idea is beautiful: just as a family gives birth to a child inside a home, a writer gives birth to meaning by combining simple pieces into a character. The word 字 itself demonstrates what it describes.

Simple rule. Many Chinese characters are built by stacking or combining smaller pieces, each carrying its own meaning. When you see an unfamiliar character, look for parts you already recognize — they are clues. Here, knowing 宀 (roof) and 子 (child) lets you read the character like a tiny picture: something born under a roof → a created symbol → a written character.

Try it yourself. Cover the top of 字 with your finger — you see 子 (child). Now cover the bottom — you see 宀 (roof). Practice writing 字 stroke by stroke: first the roof (three strokes), then the child below (three strokes). You have just written the word for word itself!
