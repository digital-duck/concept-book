🍃 Acceleration

🍃 Acceleration

Definition. Let a particle move along a path in $\mathbb{R}^3$, with velocity $\mathbf{v}(t)$ at time $t$ (as defined in the preceding section). The acceleration is the rate of change of velocity with respect to time:

$$ \mathbf{a}(t) = \frac{d\mathbf{v}}{dt} = \frac{d^2\mathbf{r}}{dt^2}. $$

Acceleration is a vector quantity carrying SI units of $\mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}$. It is zero when the velocity is constant—whether at rest or in uniform rectilinear motion—and nonzero whenever the speed or direction of motion changes.

Worked Example. A projectile is launched from the origin with initial velocity $\mathbf{v}_0 = (20\,\hat{x} + 15\,\hat{y})\,\mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}$ near Earth's surface. The only force is gravity, giving $\mathbf{a} = -g\,\hat{y}$ with $g = 9.8\,\mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}$. Integrating once:

$$ \mathbf{v}(t) = 20\,\hat{x} + (15 - 9.8\,t)\,\hat{y}\;\mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}. $$

Integrating again:

$$ \mathbf{r}(t) = 20t\,\hat{x} + \!\left(15t - \tfrac{1}{2}(9.8)t^2\right)\hat{y}\;\mathrm{m}. $$

At $t = 1\,\mathrm{s}$, the acceleration is still exactly $-9.8\,\hat{y}\,\mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}$, confirming that $\mathbf{a}$ is independent of the current velocity under a constant force.

Key Theorem (Newton's Second Law). For a particle of constant mass $m$ subjected to a net force $\mathbf{F}$,

$$ \mathbf{F} = m\,\mathbf{a}. $$

This relation elevates acceleration from a kinematic descriptor to a dynamic quantity: knowing $\mathbf{F}$ as a function of position and time yields a second-order ordinary differential equation whose solution determines the complete trajectory $\mathbf{r}(t)$ given initial conditions $\mathbf{r}(0)$ and $\mathbf{v}(0)$.

Lab Exercise. A cart of mass $m = 0.50\,\mathrm{kg}$ is released from rest on a frictionless inclined plane tilted at angle $\theta = 30°$.

  1. Show that the acceleration along the slope is $a = g\sin\theta$.
  2. Calculate the speed of the cart after it has traveled $s = 2.0\,\mathrm{m}$ down the slope, using $v^2 = 2as$.
  3. How does your result change if $\theta = 45°$? Interpret the dependence on $\theta$ physically.
