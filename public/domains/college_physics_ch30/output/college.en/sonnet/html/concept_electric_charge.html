🌱 Electric Charge

Definition. Electric charge is a fundamental intrinsic property of matter that determines how a particle participates in electromagnetic interactions. Charge is quantized: every observable free charge is an integer multiple of the elementary charge $e = 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\ \text{C}$. By convention, the proton carries charge $+e$ and the electron carries charge $-e$. The SI unit of charge is the coulomb (C). The total electric charge of an isolated system is conserved — a principle called conservation of charge — meaning charge can neither be created nor destroyed, only transferred between objects.

The electrostatic force between two point charges $q_1$ and $q_2$ separated by distance $r$ in vacuum is given by Coulomb's Law:

$$ \vec{F} = k_e \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} \hat{r} $$

where $k_e = 8.988 \times 10^9\ \text{N·m}^2\text{/C}^2$ is the Coulomb constant, related to the permittivity of free space by $k_e = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}$.

Worked Example. Two small spheres carry charges $q_1 = +3.0\ \mu\text{C}$ and $q_2 = -1.5\ \mu\text{C}$ and are placed $0.20\ \text{m}$ apart. Find the magnitude and character of the force.

$$ |\vec{F}| = k_e \frac{|q_1||q_2|}{r^2} = (8.988 \times 10^9) \frac{(3.0 \times 10^{-6})(1.5 \times 10^{-6})}{(0.20)^2} $$ $$ |\vec{F}| = (8.988 \times 10^9) \frac{4.5 \times 10^{-12}}{0.040} \approx 1.01\ \text{N} $$

Because the charges are opposite in sign, the force is attractive.

Key Theorem (Conservation of Charge). For any closed system, $\sum q_i = \text{constant}$. In pair production, for example, a photon creates an electron–positron pair $(q = -e,\ q = +e)$; the net charge remains zero, consistent with the photon's zero charge.

Lab Exercise. Charge a rubber rod by friction with fur and bring it near a suspended aluminum-foil strip. Record the deflection angle as a function of separation distance $r$ at five measured positions. Plot $F$ versus $1/r^2$ and determine whether the slope is consistent with the predicted value $k_e \cdot q_1 q_2$. Estimate $q_1$ using the slope and a known test charge $q_2$ measured with a calibrated electrometer.
