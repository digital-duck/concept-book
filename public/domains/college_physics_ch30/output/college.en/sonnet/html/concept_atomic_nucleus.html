🍃 Atomic Nucleus

🍃 Atomic Nucleus

Definition. The atomic nucleus is the dense, positively charged core of an atom, composed of protons and neutrons (collectively called nucleons) bound together by the strong nuclear force. For a nucleus with atomic number $Z$ (proton count) and mass number $A$ (total nucleon count), the neutron number is $N = A - Z$. The nucleus is conventionally denoted $^A_Z\text{X}$, where X is the chemical symbol. Nuclear radii follow the empirical relation

$$ r = r_0 A^{1/3}, \quad r_0 \approx 1.2 \text{ fm}, $$

where $1 \text{ fm} = 10^{-15} \text{ m}$. This scaling implies that nuclear volume is proportional to $A$, reflecting an approximately constant nucleon density of roughly $2.3 \times 10^{17} \text{ kg/m}^3$.

Worked Example. Estimate the radius and density of the iron-56 nucleus, $^{56}_{26}\text{Fe}$.

Using the radius formula:

$$ r = (1.2 \text{ fm})(56)^{1/3} = (1.2 \text{ fm})(3.83) \approx 4.6 \text{ fm}. $$

The nuclear mass is approximately $56 \times 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg} = 9.35 \times 10^{-26} \text{ kg}$. The volume is:

$$ V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 = \frac{4}{3}\pi (4.6 \times 10^{-15})^3 \approx 4.1 \times 10^{-43} \text{ m}^3. $$

The density is therefore $\rho \approx 2.3 \times 10^{17} \text{ kg/m}^3$, confirming that all nuclei share nearly the same density regardless of $A$.

Key Theorem: Nuclear Binding Energy. The total mass of a nucleus is less than the sum of its constituent nucleon masses. This mass defect $\Delta m$ corresponds to the binding energy $B$ via Einstein's relation:

$$ B = \Delta m \, c^2 = \bigl[Z m_p + N m_n - m_\text{nuc}\bigr]c^2. $$

The binding energy per nucleon, $B/A$, peaks near $A = 56$ (iron), explaining why both fission of heavy nuclei and fusion of light nuclei release energy.

Lab Exercise. Using a table of atomic masses, calculate the binding energy per nucleon for $^4_2\text{He}$ (helium-4) and $^{235}_{92}\text{U}$ (uranium-235). Compare your results to the semi-empirical mass formula prediction and identify which nucleus is more tightly bound per nucleon. Explain the physical significance of the difference in terms of the competition between the strong force and electrostatic repulsion.
