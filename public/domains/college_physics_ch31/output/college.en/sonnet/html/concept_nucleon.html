🍃 Nucleon

🍃 Nucleon

Definition. A nucleon is any particle that resides in the atomic nucleus; the term encompasses both the proton and the neutron. Protons carry electric charge $+e = +1.602 \times 10^{-19}\ \text{C}$, while neutrons are electrically neutral. Both particles have nearly equal rest masses:

$$ m_p = 1.007276\ \text{u}, \qquad m_n = 1.008665\ \text{u}, $$

where $1\ \text{u} = 1.66054 \times 10^{-27}\ \text{kg}$ is the unified atomic mass unit. The total number of nucleons in a nucleus is the mass number $A$, which equals the sum of the atomic number $Z$ (proton count) and the neutron number $N$:

$$ A = Z + N. $$

Nucleons are bound together by the strong nuclear force, a short-range ($\sim 1\ \text{fm} = 10^{-15}\ \text{m}$) attractive interaction that overwhelms electrostatic repulsion at sub-femtometer separations.

Worked Example. Consider the nuclide $^{56}_{26}\text{Fe}$, ordinary iron. Here $A = 56$ and $Z = 26$, so the number of neutrons is

$$ N = A - Z = 56 - 26 = 30. $$

The nucleus therefore contains 26 protons and 30 neutrons, for a total of 56 nucleons. The nuclear binding energy per nucleon—a measure of stability—is approximately $8.8\ \text{MeV}$, among the highest of any nuclide, which is why iron represents the endpoint of stellar nucleosynthesis.

Key Theorem (Semi-Empirical Mass Formula). The Bethe–Weizsäcker formula approximates the nuclear binding energy $B$ as

$$ B = a_v A - a_s A^{2/3} - a_c \frac{Z(Z-1)}{A^{1/3}} - a_a \frac{(A - 2Z)^2}{A} + \delta(A,Z), $$

where the terms represent, respectively, volume, surface, Coulomb, asymmetry, and pairing contributions. This formula encodes the fact that binding energy scales extensively with nucleon number $A$ in the nuclear interior, reflecting the short-range saturation property of the strong force.

Lab Exercise. Using a chart of nuclides, identify the stable isotopes of calcium ($Z = 20$). For each, compute $N$ and the mass number $A$. Plot $N$ versus $Z$ for these isotopes and describe how the neutron excess $N - Z$ changes as $A$ increases. Explain this trend in terms of the Coulomb and asymmetry terms in the semi-empirical mass formula.
