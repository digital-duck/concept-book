🍃 Nuclear Decay

🍃 Nuclear Decay

Definition. Nuclear decay is the spontaneous transformation of an unstable atomic nucleus into a more stable configuration through the emission of particles or electromagnetic radiation. The process is governed by conservation laws (charge, baryon number, lepton number, energy-momentum) and is inherently probabilistic: the decay rate of a sample of $N$ identical nuclei is proportional to $N$ itself,

$$ \frac{dN}{dt} = -\lambda N, $$

where $\lambda$ is the decay constant (units: s$^{-1}$). Integrating yields the fundamental law of radioactive decay:

$$ N(t) = N_0\, e^{-\lambda t}. $$

The half-life $t_{1/2} = \ln 2\, /\, \lambda$ is the time required for half of any initial population to decay. The activity $A = \lambda N$ (measured in becquerels, 1 Bq = 1 decay s$^{-1}$) quantifies the instantaneous decay rate.

Worked Example. Carbon-14 ($^{14}_{6}\text{C}$) undergoes $\beta^-$ decay with $t_{1/2} = 5730\,\text{yr}$, so $\lambda = \ln 2 / 5730\,\text{yr} \approx 1.21 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{yr}^{-1}$. Suppose an archaeological sample initially contains $N_0 = 6.0\times10^{10}$ atoms of $^{14}$C. After $t = 11{,}460\,\text{yr}$ (exactly two half-lives), the remaining population is

$$ N = N_0\left(\tfrac{1}{2}\right)^2 = 6.0\times10^{10} \times 0.25 = 1.5\times10^{10}\,\text{atoms}. $$

The activity at that time is $A = \lambda N = (1.21\times10^{-4}\,\text{yr}^{-1})(1.5\times10^{10}) \approx 1.82\times10^6\,\text{yr}^{-1} \approx 0.058\,\text{Bq}$.

Key Theorem (Bateman Equations). For a decay chain $A \to B \to C$ with constants $\lambda_A$ and $\lambda_B$, the population of daughter nuclide $B$ evolves as

$$ N_B(t) = \frac{\lambda_A N_{A,0}}{\lambda_B - \lambda_A}\!\left(e^{-\lambda_A t} - e^{-\lambda_B t}\right) + N_{B,0}\,e^{-\lambda_B t}. $$

When $\lambda_B \operatorname{gg} \lambda_A$, secular equilibrium is reached: $\lambda_A N_A = \lambda_B N_B$, meaning daughter activity matches parent activity.

Lab Exercise. Using a Geiger–Müller counter, record the count rate of a $^{137}$Ba sample (produced from $^{137}$Cs) every 30 seconds for 15 minutes. Plot $\ln A$ versus $t$; the slope equals $-\lambda$. Extract $t_{1/2}$ and compare to the accepted value of 2.55 min. Assess sources of systematic error, including background radiation and detector dead time.
