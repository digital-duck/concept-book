🌸 Stability Analysis

Stability analysis is a fundamental branch of dynamical systems theory concerned with determining whether an equilibrium point of a system remains stable when subjected to small perturbations. Formally, a system $\dot{\mathbf{x}} = \mathbf{f}(\mathbf{x})$ is considered stable at an operating point $\mathbf{x}_0$ if any initial deviation from $\mathbf{x}_0$ results in the trajectory remaining close to $\mathbf{x}_0$ indefinitely, or returning asymptotically to it. For nonlinear systems, this often requires invoking Lyapunov's Direct Method; however, for foundational understanding, we typically employ linearization around the equilibrium point $\mathbf{x}_0$. We analyze the behavior of the system by approximating $\mathbf{f}(\mathbf{x})$ with its Taylor expansion up to the first order, yielding a local linear model:

$$ \dot{\mathbf{y}} = J(\mathbf{x}_0)\mathbf{y} $$

where $J$ is the Jacobian matrix evaluated at $\mathbf{x}_0$, and $\mathbf{y}$ represents the perturbation vector.
