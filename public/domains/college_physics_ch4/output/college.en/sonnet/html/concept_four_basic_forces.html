Definition

Modern physics recognizes four fundamental interactions that govern all physical phenomena in the universe. These are the gravitational force, the electromagnetic force, the strong nuclear force, and the weak nuclear force. Each force is characterized by its relative strength, range, and the particles (mediators) through which it acts.

Force Relative Strength Range Mediator
Strong nuclear $1$ $\sim 10^{-15}$ m Gluons
Electromagnetic $10^{-2}$ $\infty$ Photons
Weak nuclear $10^{-13}$ $\sim 10^{-18}$ m $W^\pm$, $Z^0$ bosons
Gravitational $10^{-38}$ $\infty$ Gravitons (theoretical)

The gravitational force between two masses $m_1$ and $m_2$ separated by distance $r$ is given by Newton's law:

$$ F_g = G\frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} $$

where $G = 6.674 \times 10^{-11}\ \text{N┬Ęm}^2/\text{kg}^2$. The electromagnetic force between two charges $q_1$ and $q_2$ follows Coulomb's law:

$$ F_e = k_e\frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} $$

where $k_e = 8.99 \times 10^9\ \text{N┬Ęm}^2/\text{C}^2$.

Worked Example

Compare the gravitational and electromagnetic forces between a proton and an electron separated by $r = 5.29 \times 10^{-11}\ \text{m}$ (the Bohr radius).

Using $m_p = 1.673 \times 10^{-27}\ \text{kg}$, $m_e = 9.109 \times 10^{-31}\ \text{kg}$, and $|q| = 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\ \text{C}$:

$$ F_g = \frac{(6.674\times10^{-11})(1.673\times10^{-27})(9.109\times10^{-31})}{(5.29\times10^{-11})^2} \approx 3.63 \times 10^{-47}\ \text{N} $$ $$ F_e = \frac{(8.99\times10^9)(1.602\times10^{-19})^2}{(5.29\times10^{-11})^2} \approx 8.23 \times 10^{-8}\ \text{N} $$

The ratio $F_e/F_g \approx 2.27 \times 10^{39}$ confirms that electromagnetism dominates gravity at the atomic scale by roughly 39 orders of magnitude.

Key Theorem

The unification principle holds that at sufficiently high energies, the electromagnetic and weak forces merge into a single electroweak force ŌĆö a result confirmed experimentally at CERN through detection of the $W^\pm$ and $Z^0$ bosons. Theorists seek a Grand Unified Theory (GUT) encompassing the strong force and, ultimately, a Theory of Everything incorporating gravity.
