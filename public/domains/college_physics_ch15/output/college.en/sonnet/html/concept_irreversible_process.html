🍃 Irreversible Process

🍃 Irreversible Process

Definition. An irreversible process is a thermodynamic process that cannot be reversed by an infinitesimal change in an external parameter, and whose spontaneous reversal would violate the second law of thermodynamics. Formally, a process is irreversible if the total entropy of the universe (system plus surroundings) increases:

$$ \Delta S_{\text{universe}} = \Delta S_{\text{system}} + \Delta S_{\text{surroundings}} > 0 $$

Common sources of irreversibility include friction, unrestrained expansion, heat transfer across a finite temperature difference, and mixing of distinct substances. Unlike a quasi-static (reversible) process, an irreversible process drives the system through non-equilibrium states, making it impossible to retrace the exact path in both the system and surroundings simultaneously.

Worked Example. Consider $n = 1.00\,\text{mol}$ of an ideal gas at $T = 300\,\text{K}$ confined to one side of an insulated container of total volume $V_f = 2V_i$. When a partition is removed, the gas undergoes a free expansion into the vacuum.

Since no work is done ($W = 0$) and the walls are adiabatic ($Q = 0$), the internal energy and hence the temperature remain unchanged. The entropy change of the system is calculated using the equivalent reversible isothermal path:

$$ \Delta S_{\text{system}} = nR\ln\!\left(\frac{V_f}{V_i}\right) = (1.00)(8.314)\ln(2) \approx +5.76\,\text{J/K} $$

The surroundings receive no heat, so $\Delta S_{\text{surroundings}} = 0$. Therefore:

$$ \Delta S_{\text{universe}} = 5.76\,\text{J/K} > 0 $$

confirming the process is irreversible.

Key Theorem (Clausius Inequality). For any cyclic process, the Clausius inequality states:

$$ \oint \frac{\delta Q}{T} \leq 0 $$

with equality holding only for a reversible cycle. For any process between states 1 and 2, this implies $\Delta S \geq \int_1^2 \frac{\delta Q}{T}$, where the inequality corresponds to an irreversible path. This theorem provides the quantitative criterion distinguishing irreversible from reversible processes.

Lab Exercise. Place a thermally insulated copper block at $T_H = 373\,\text{K}$ in thermal contact with a water reservoir at $T_C = 293\,\text{K}$. Measure the heat $Q$ transferred until equilibrium is reached. Calculate $\Delta S_{\text{block}}$, $\Delta S_{\text{reservoir}}$, and $\Delta S_{\text{universe}}$. Verify that $\Delta S_{\text{universe}} > 0$ and discuss which physical mechanism makes this process irreversible.
