🌸 Van De Graaff Generator

🌸 Van De Graaff Generator

Definition

A Van de Graaff generator is an electrostatic device that accumulates large amounts of electric charge on a hollow metallic sphere by exploiting the principle that charge resides on the outer surface of conductors. The mechanism consists of a non-conducting conveyor belt driven between two rollers—one near the base and one inside the dome—with a pair of metallic combs positioned at each roller. The lower comb acquires charge from an external high-voltage source (or through triboelectric contact) and sprays charge onto the moving belt. The upper comb, located inside the grounded interior of the sphere, strips this charge off the belt and deposits it onto the sphere's outer surface. Because the interior of a conductor in electrostatic equilibrium has zero net charge and zero electric field, charge continuously migrates to the exterior regardless of how much has already accumulated, allowing the potential to build up without theoretical limit (in practice limited by corona discharge and breakdown of surrounding air).

Worked Example

Suppose a spherical dome of radius $R = 0.15\ \text{m}$ has accumulated a charge $Q = 1.0\ \mu\text{C}$. The electric potential at the surface is:

$$ V = \frac{kQ}{R} = \frac{(8.99 \times 10^9\ \text{N·m}^2/\text{C}^2)(1.0 \times 10^{-6}\ \text{C})}{0.15\ \text{m}} \approx 6.0 \times 10^4\ \text{V} $$

The surface electric field is $E = V/R \approx 4.0 \times 10^5\ \text{V/m}$. Since air begins to ionize at roughly $3 \times 10^6\ \text{V/m}$, this dome is well below breakdown, but a dome of the same charge at $R = 0.02\ \text{m}$ would exceed the threshold.

Key Theorem

By Gauss's law applied to a closed surface just inside the conducting shell:

$$ \oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A} = \frac{Q_{\text{enc}}}{\varepsilon_0} = 0 $$

since $Q_{\text{enc}} = 0$ in the interior. Consequently, all charge distributes on the outer surface, and the potential everywhere inside equals the surface potential—a result central to the generator's operation and to electrostatic shielding (Faraday cage).

Lab Exercise

Set up a Van de Graaff generator in a dry environment and bring a grounded sphere of known radius $r$ near the dome. Record the gap distance $d$ at which a spark first jumps. Use $E_{\text{breakdown}} \approx 3 \times 10^6\ \text{V/m}$ and the approximation $V_{\text{dome}} \approx E_{\text{breakdown}} \cdot d$ to estimate the dome potential. Compare this with the theoretical value calculated from the dome radius and an assumed charge. Discuss sources of discrepancy including humidity, electrode geometry, and charge leakage through the belt.
