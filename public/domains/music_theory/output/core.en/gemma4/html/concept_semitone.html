🌱 Semitone

Have you ever noticed how certain keys on a piano seem physically closer together than others? This perception points to the fundamental unit of musical distance: the semitone. For any musician or theorist, understanding this smallest interval is key because it dictates the structure of scales and chords, allowing for precise control over pitch movement.

Definition: A semitone is defined as half the size of a whole tone. It represents the minimum measurable interval on the standard chromatic scale in Western music theory. While octave relationships define frequency ratios (like $2:1$), the semitone defines the smallest quantized step between adjacent notes, such as C to $\text{C}\sharp$.

Worked Example: Consider the interval starting at Middle C ($C_4$) and moving up to D ($D_4$). We can break this movement down into successive steps: $C \rightarrow \text{C}\sharp$ (1 semitone); $\text{C}\sharp \rightarrow D\flat$ (1 semitone); and finally, $D\flat \rightarrow D$ (1 semitone). Therefore, the total interval from $C_4$ to $D_4$ spans exactly 3 half-steps, or 3 semitones.
