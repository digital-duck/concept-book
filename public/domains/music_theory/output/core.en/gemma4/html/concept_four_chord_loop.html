🍃 Four Chord Loop

Have you ever noticed how certain musical progressions feel instantly familiar and satisfying? Many of the most enduring song structures rely on repeating patterns that cycle back to their origin—a perfect example being a four-chord loop, where the sequence feels complete yet continually refreshing. Understanding this mechanism is key not just in music theory, but in any field dealing with cyclical dependencies, from computer programming algorithms to chemical reaction cycles.

A Four Chord Loop describes a closed sequential system involving exactly four distinct elements. Formally, it is an ordered set of states $\{C_1, C_2, C_3, C_4\}$ where the transition rule dictates that after visiting $C_4$, the process must return directly to $C_1$. Mathematically, this sequence exhibits a period ($P$) of 4. The loop can be represented by the transition map:

$$ C_{i+1} = f(C_i) \text{ for } i=1, 2, 3, 4 $$

with the critical constraint that $f(C_4) = C_1$.
