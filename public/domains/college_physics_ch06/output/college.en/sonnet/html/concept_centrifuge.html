🌸 Centrifuge

🌸 Centrifuge

Definition. A centrifuge is a device that rotates a sample at high angular velocity $\omega$ to exploit the effective separation force arising from circular motion. When an object of mass $m$ moves in a circle of radius $r$, it requires a centripetal force $F_c = m\omega^2 r$ directed inward. Inside the rotating frame, this manifests as an apparent outward (centrifugal) force of equal magnitude. Denser components of a mixture experience a greater net outward force per unit volume and therefore migrate radially outward faster than less dense components, achieving separation that gravity alone would require prohibitively long timescales to accomplish.

The effectiveness of a centrifuge is characterized by its relative centrifugal force (RCF), expressed as a multiple of gravitational acceleration $g$:

$$ \text{RCF} = \frac{\omega^2 r}{g} $$

where $\omega$ is in rad/s and $r$ is the radial distance of the sample from the rotation axis. RCF is also reported in units of $\times g$.

Worked Example. A microcentrifuge spins at $12{,}000\ \text{rpm}$ with samples held at $r = 0.080\ \text{m}$ from the axis. Find the RCF.

Convert rpm to rad/s:

$$ \omega = 12{,}000\ \frac{\text{rev}}{\text{min}} \times \frac{2\pi\ \text{rad}}{\text{rev}} \times \frac{1\ \text{min}}{60\ \text{s}} = 1257\ \text{rad/s} $$

Compute RCF:

$$ \text{RCF} = \frac{(1257)^2 (0.080)}{9.81} \approx \frac{1.580 \times 10^6 \times 0.080}{9.81} \approx 1.29 \times 10^4 $$

The sample experiences approximately $12{,}900\,g$, compressing sedimentation time from hours (under gravity alone) to seconds.

Key Theorem. The sedimentation velocity $v_s$ of a spherical particle is given by Stokes' law modified for centrifugal acceleration:

$$ v_s = \frac{2r_p^2 (\rho_p - \rho_f)\,\omega^2 r}{9\eta} $$

where $r_p$ is the particle radius, $\rho_p$ and $\rho_f$ are particle and fluid densities, and $\eta$ is fluid viscosity. Separation efficiency scales as $\omega^2$, making rotational speed the dominant design parameter.

Lab Exercise. Set up a centrifuge at two speeds, $3{,}000\ \text{rpm}$ and $6{,}000\ \text{rpm}$, using the same rotor radius. Calculate the RCF at each setting, determine the ratio of sedimentation velocities, and verify experimentally that doubling $\omega$ increases $v_s$ by a factor of four.
