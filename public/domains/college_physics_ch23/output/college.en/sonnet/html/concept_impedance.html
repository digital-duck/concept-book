🍃 Impedance

🍃 Impedance

Definition. In an AC circuit, impedance $Z$ is the complex-valued generalization of resistance that accounts for the combined opposition to alternating current from resistors, inductors, and capacitors. For a series RLC circuit driven at angular frequency $\omega$, the impedance is defined as

$$ Z = R + j\left(\omega L - \frac{1}{\omega C}\right), $$

where $R$ is resistance (in ohms), $L$ is inductance (in henries), $C$ is capacitance (in farads), and $j = \sqrt{-1}$. The real part $R$ represents dissipative opposition; the imaginary part $X = \omega L - 1/(\omega C)$ is the reactance. The magnitude of impedance is

$$ |Z| = \sqrt{R^2 + X^2} = \sqrt{R^2 + \left(\omega L - \frac{1}{\omega C}\right)^2}, $$

and the phase angle $\phi$ between voltage and current satisfies $\tan\phi = X/R$. By Ohm's law generalized to AC, the peak voltage and peak current are related by $V_0 = |Z|\, I_0$.

Worked Example. A series RLC circuit has $R = 50\,\Omega$, $L = 0.20\,\text{H}$, and $C = 10\,\mu\text{F}$, driven at $f = 200\,\text{Hz}$ (so $\omega = 2\pi \times 200 \approx 1257\,\text{rad/s}$). Compute:

$$ \omega L = 1257 \times 0.20 = 251.3\,\Omega, \qquad \frac{1}{\omega C} = \frac{1}{1257 \times 10^{-5}} \approx 79.6\,\Omega. $$

Thus $X = 251.3 - 79.6 = 171.7\,\Omega$, and

$$ |Z| = \sqrt{50^2 + 171.7^2} = \sqrt{2500 + 29\,481} \approx 178.5\,\Omega. $$

If the source peak voltage is $V_0 = 120\,\text{V}$, then $I_0 = 120/178.5 \approx 0.672\,\text{A}$.

Key Theorem (Resonance Condition). The impedance magnitude $|Z|$ is minimized — and current is maximized — when $X = 0$, i.e., when

$$ \omega_0 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{LC}}. $$

At resonance, $|Z| = R$, the circuit behaves as a purely resistive load, and voltage and current are in phase ($\phi = 0$).

Lab Exercise. Assemble a series RLC circuit with $R = 100\,\Omega$, $L = 50\,\text{mH}$, and $C = 1\,\mu\text{F}$. Sweep the driving frequency from $100\,\text{Hz}$ to $5\,\text{kHz}$ and record $I_0$ at each step. Plot $|Z|$ versus $f$, identify the resonant frequency experimentally, and compare to the theoretical prediction $f_0 = 1/(2\pi\sqrt{LC})$. Discuss how varying $R$ affects the sharpness (quality factor $Q = \omega_0 L / R$) of the resonance curve.
