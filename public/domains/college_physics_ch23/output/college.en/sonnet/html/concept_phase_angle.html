🍃 Phase Angle

🍃 Phase Angle

Definition. In the analysis of alternating-current (AC) circuits, the phase angle $\phi$ is the angular difference between the voltage phasor and the current phasor at a given frequency. For a series RLC circuit driven by a sinusoidal source $v(t) = V_0 \cos(\omega t)$, the steady-state current takes the form $i(t) = I_0 \cos(\omega t - \phi)$, where

$$ \tan \phi = \frac{X_L - X_C}{R} = \frac{\omega L - \frac{1}{\omega C}}{R}. $$

Here $X_L = \omega L$ is the inductive reactance, $X_C = 1/(\omega C)$ is the capacitive reactance, and $R$ is the resistance. The phase angle lies in the range $-\pi/2 \leq \phi \leq \pi/2$; a positive $\phi$ indicates an inductive circuit (current lags voltage), while a negative $\phi$ indicates a capacitive circuit (current leads voltage).

Worked Example. A series RLC circuit has $R = 50\,\Omega$, $L = 0.20\,\text{H}$, and $C = 10\,\mu\text{F}$, driven at $f = 200\,\text{Hz}$.

Compute the reactances:

$$ X_L = 2\pi (200)(0.20) \approx 251.3\,\Omega, \qquad X_C = \frac{1}{2\pi (200)(10 \times 10^{-6})} \approx 79.6\,\Omega. $$

Then:

$$ \tan \phi = \frac{251.3 - 79.6}{50} = \frac{171.7}{50} \approx 3.43 \implies \phi \approx \arctan(3.43) \approx 73.7°. $$

The circuit is strongly inductive; the current lags the voltage by nearly $74°$.

Key Theorem. The average power delivered to the circuit is

$$ \langle P \rangle = \frac{1}{2} V_0 I_0 \cos \phi = I_{\text{rms}}^2 R, $$

where $\cos \phi$ is called the power factor. This follows from expanding $v(t)\,i(t)$ and time-averaging over one period; only the resistive term survives. At resonance ($X_L = X_C$), $\phi = 0$ and the power factor is unity, so maximum power is transferred.

Lab Exercise. Connect a series RLC circuit ($R = 100\,\Omega$, $L = 50\,\text{mH}$, $C = 1\,\mu\text{F}$) to a function generator swept from $100\,\text{Hz}$ to $5\,\text{kHz}$. Using an oscilloscope, measure the time delay $\Delta t$ between the voltage and current waveforms, then compute $\phi = \omega \Delta t$. Plot $\phi$ versus frequency and identify the resonant frequency where $\phi = 0$.
