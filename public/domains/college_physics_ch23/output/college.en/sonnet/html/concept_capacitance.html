🌱 Capacitance

🌱 Capacitance

Definition. A capacitor is a device that stores electric charge and, consequently, electrical energy. When a potential difference $V$ is established between two conducting surfaces separated by an insulating medium, charge accumulates: positive charge $+Q$ on one conductor and negative charge $-Q$ on the other. The capacitance $C$ of the device is defined as the ratio of the magnitude of charge stored on either conductor to the magnitude of the potential difference between them:

$$ C = \frac{Q}{V} $$

Capacitance is a purely geometric property — it depends on the shape, size, and separation of the conductors, as well as the permittivity of the intervening medium, but not on $Q$ or $V$ individually. The SI unit of capacitance is the farad (F), where $1\,\text{F} = 1\,\text{C/V}$. Practical devices range from picofarads ($\text{pF}$) to millifarads ($\text{mF}$).

Worked Example. Consider a parallel-plate capacitor with plate area $A = 0.020\,\text{m}^2$ and plate separation $d = 1.5\,\text{mm} = 1.5 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{m}$ in vacuum. The capacitance is given by:

$$ C = \varepsilon_0 \frac{A}{d} $$

where $\varepsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12}\,\text{F/m}$ is the permittivity of free space. Substituting:

$$ C = \frac{(8.85 \times 10^{-12})(0.020)}{1.5 \times 10^{-3}} = 1.18 \times 10^{-10}\,\text{F} \approx 118\,\text{pF} $$

If this capacitor is connected to a $9.0\,\text{V}$ battery, the stored charge is $Q = CV = (1.18 \times 10^{-10})(9.0) \approx 1.06\,\text{nC}$.

Key Theorem. For a parallel-plate capacitor, $C = \varepsilon_0 A/d$ follows directly from applying Gauss's law to a pillbox surface enclosing one plate, yielding $E = \sigma/\varepsilon_0$ between the plates, and integrating: $V = Ed$. If the gap is filled with a dielectric of dielectric constant $\kappa$, the capacitance increases to $C = \kappa\varepsilon_0 A/d$, because the dielectric reduces the effective electric field, allowing more charge to be stored at the same potential.

Lab Exercise. Connect a parallel-plate capacitor with variable plate separation to a charged electrometer. Record the electrometer reading (proportional to $V$) as you increase $d$ in measured increments while keeping $Q$ fixed. Plot $V$ versus $d$ and verify the linear relationship $V = Qd/(\varepsilon_0 A)$, confirming that $C \propto 1/d$. From the slope, extract an experimental value of $\varepsilon_0$ and compare it to the accepted value.
