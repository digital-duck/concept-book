🌱 Magnetic Field

🌱 Magnetic Field

Definition. A magnetic field is a vector field $\mathbf{B}$ that permeates space in the vicinity of moving electric charges or magnetic dipoles, exerting forces on other moving charges placed within it. The SI unit of magnetic field strength is the tesla (T), where $1\,\text{T} = 1\,\text{kg}\cdot\text{s}^{-2}\cdot\text{A}^{-1}$. The magnetic force on a point charge $q$ moving with velocity $\mathbf{v}$ in a magnetic field $\mathbf{B}$ is given by the Lorentz force law:

$$ \mathbf{F} = q\mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{B} $$

This force is always perpendicular to both $\mathbf{v}$ and $\mathbf{B}$, so a magnetic field does no work on a moving charge and cannot alter its kinetic energy—only its direction of motion.

Worked Example. A proton ($q = 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\,\text{C}$, $m = 1.673 \times 10^{-27}\,\text{kg}$) moves with speed $v = 2.0 \times 10^6\,\text{m/s}$ in the $+x$-direction through a uniform magnetic field $\mathbf{B} = 0.50\,\text{T}\,\hat{z}$.

The magnetic force is:

$$ \mathbf{F} = q\mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{B} = q(v\hat{x}) \times (B\hat{z}) = qvB(\hat{x} \times \hat{z}) = -qvB\,\hat{y} $$ $$ |\mathbf{F}| = (1.602\times10^{-19})(2.0\times10^6)(0.50) \approx 1.60 \times 10^{-13}\,\text{N} $$

The force acts in the $-y$-direction, deflecting the proton into circular motion in the $xy$-plane.

Key Theorem — Radius of Circular Motion. When a charged particle moves perpendicular to a uniform magnetic field, the magnetic force provides the centripetal acceleration. Setting $|\mathbf{F}| = qvB$ equal to $mv^2/r$:

$$ r = \frac{mv}{|q|B} $$

This result, known as the cyclotron radius or Larmor radius, shows that the orbital radius is proportional to momentum and inversely proportional to field strength. For the proton above: $r = (1.673\times10^{-27})(2.0\times10^6)/[(1.602\times10^{-19})(0.50)] \approx 0.042\,\text{m} = 4.2\,\text{cm}$.

Lab Exercise. Using a cathode-ray tube in a transverse magnetic field, measure the deflection radius $r$ of an electron beam for three different field strengths $B$. Plot $r$ versus $1/B$ and extract the electron's charge-to-mass ratio $|q|/m$ from the slope. Compare your result to the accepted value of $1.759 \times 10^{11}\,\text{C/kg}$ and estimate systematic uncertainties.
