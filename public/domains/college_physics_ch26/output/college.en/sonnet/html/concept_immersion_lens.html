🌸 Immersion Lens

🌸 Immersion Lens

Definition. An immersion lens is an optical system in which the space between the objective lens and the specimen is filled with a medium of refractive index $n > 1$ — typically immersion oil with $n \approx 1.515$ — rather than air ($n = 1$). This technique exploits the dependence of numerical aperture on the medium's refractive index. The numerical aperture of an objective is defined as

$$ \text{NA} = n \sin\theta, $$

where $\theta$ is the half-angle of the maximum cone of light that can enter or exit the lens. Because immersion oil closely matches the refractive index of glass, light passes from the coverslip into the oil and into the objective with minimal refraction, allowing larger effective cone angles and thus a higher NA than an equivalent air objective.

Worked Example. Consider an oil-immersion objective with $\theta = 67°$ used in immersion oil ($n = 1.515$). Compute the numerical aperture and compare it with an identical dry objective ($n_\text{air} = 1.00$).

$$ \text{NA}_\text{oil} = 1.515 \times \sin 67° = 1.515 \times 0.921 \approx 1.40 $$ $$ \text{NA}_\text{dry} = 1.00 \times \sin 67° \approx 0.921 $$

The immersion objective achieves $\text{NA} \approx 1.40$, a factor of $1.515$ improvement. Since the minimum resolvable separation is

$$ d = \frac{0.61\,\lambda}{\text{NA}}, $$

for $\lambda = 550\,\text{nm}$:

$$ d_\text{oil} = \frac{0.61 \times 550\,\text{nm}}{1.40} \approx 240\,\text{nm}, \qquad d_\text{dry} \approx 364\,\text{nm}. $$

The immersion lens resolves structures roughly 35% finer.

Key Theorem (Abbe Diffraction Limit). Ernst Abbe showed that the resolution limit of a far-field optical microscope is bounded below by

$$ d_\min = \frac{\lambda}{2\,\text{NA}}. $$

This result is fundamental: no conventional refractive optic can surpass it. The immersion lens maximizes NA — and therefore minimizes $d_\min$ — within the constraints of classical wave optics.

Lab Exercise. Prepare two slides of a diffraction grating (600 lines/mm): examine one with a $40\times$ dry objective (NA $= 0.65$) and the other with a $100\times$ oil-immersion objective (NA $= 1.25$). Illuminate with $\lambda = 546\,\text{nm}$ green light. Calculate the theoretical resolution limit for each objective, record the finest resolved grating lines, and determine the percentage improvement in resolving power attributable to oil immersion. Discuss any discrepancy between theoretical and observed resolution, considering factors such as aberration and sample preparation quality.
