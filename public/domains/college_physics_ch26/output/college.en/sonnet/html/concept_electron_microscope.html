🌸 Electron Microscope

🌸 Electron Microscope

Definition. An electron microscope is an imaging instrument that uses a beam of accelerated electrons, rather than visible light, to form magnified images of specimens. Because the resolving power of any microscope is fundamentally limited by the wavelength of the illuminating radiation, replacing photons (wavelengths $\lambda \approx 400$–$700\ \text{nm}$) with electrons (whose de Broglie wavelength can be made smaller than $0.01\ \text{nm}$) allows resolution at the atomic scale. The de Broglie wavelength of an electron accelerated through a potential difference $V$ is

$$ \lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2m_e eV}}, $$

where $h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34}\ \text{J·s}$ is Planck's constant, $m_e = 9.109 \times 10^{-31}\ \text{kg}$ is the electron rest mass, and $e = 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\ \text{C}$ is the elementary charge. Magnetic or electrostatic lenses focus the beam, and the Rayleigh criterion gives the minimum resolvable feature size as approximately $d_{\min} \approx 0.61\lambda / \text{NA}$, where NA is the numerical aperture of the objective lens.

Worked Example. An electron is accelerated through $V = 100\ \text{kV}$.

$$ \lambda = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{\sqrt{2(9.109 \times 10^{-31})(1.602 \times 10^{-19})(1.00 \times 10^{5})}} $$

Computing the denominator: $2m_e eV = 2(9.109\times10^{-31})(1.602\times10^{-19})(10^5) = 2.919\times10^{-44}\ \text{kg}^2\text{m}^2\text{s}^{-2}$, so $\sqrt{2m_e eV} = 1.709\times10^{-22}\ \text{kg·m·s}^{-1}$. Therefore

$$ \lambda = \frac{6.626\times10^{-34}}{1.709\times10^{-22}} \approx 3.88\times10^{-12}\ \text{m} = 3.88\ \text{pm}. $$

This wavelength is roughly $10^5$ times smaller than visible light, permitting sub-nanometer resolution.

Key Theorem (Abbe Resolution Limit). The minimum resolvable separation between two point objects is

$$ d_{\min} = \frac{0.61\,\lambda}{\text{NA}}. $$

For a transmission electron microscope operating at $100\ \text{kV}$ with NA $\approx 0.01$, this yields $d_{\min} \approx 0.24\ \text{nm}$, sufficient to resolve individual atomic columns in crystalline materials.

Lab Exercise. Using a transmission electron microscope operating at $200\ \text{kV}$, calculate the electron wavelength (applying the relativistic correction $\lambda = h/\sqrt{2m_e eV(1 + eV/2m_e c^2)}$) and estimate the point resolution if the objective lens has NA $= 0.012$. Compare your result to the lattice spacing of gold ($d = 0.204\ \text{nm}$) and determine whether individual lattice planes would be resolvable.
