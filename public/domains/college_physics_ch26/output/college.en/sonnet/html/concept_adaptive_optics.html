🌸 Adaptive Optics

🌸 Adaptive Optics

Definition. Adaptive optics (AO) is a technology that measures and corrects wavefront distortions in real time, restoring the diffraction-limited resolution of an optical system that would otherwise be degraded by aberrations—most commonly atmospheric turbulence in ground-based astronomy. A planar wavefront emitted by a distant point source becomes corrugated as it traverses layers of air with spatially and temporally varying refractive index $n(\mathbf{r}, t)$. The accumulated optical path difference (OPD) across an aperture of diameter $D$ is characterized by the Fried parameter $r_0$, defined such that the atmospheric coherence length satisfies

$$ r_0 = 0.185 \left(\frac{\lambda^2}{\int C_n^2(h)\,dh}\right)^{3/5}, $$

where $C_n^2(h)$ is the refractive-index structure constant at altitude $h$ and $\lambda$ is the wavelength. When $D \operatorname{gg} r_0$, angular resolution is limited to $\lambda/r_0$ rather than the diffraction limit $\lambda/D$.

Worked Example. A telescope with aperture $D = 8\,\text{m}$ operates at $\lambda = 500\,\text{nm}$. Under typical seeing conditions, $r_0 \approx 10\,\text{cm}$. Without AO, the angular resolution is $\lambda/r_0 = (500 \times 10^{-9})/(0.10) = 5\,\mu\text{rad} \approx 1\,\text{arcsec}$. With AO correction restoring diffraction-limited performance, the resolution becomes $\lambda/D = (500 \times 10^{-9})/(8.0) = 62.5\,\text{nrad} \approx 0.013\,\text{arcsec}$—an improvement factor of $D/r_0 = 80$.

Key Theorem (Marechal Approximation). For small residual phase errors, the Strehl ratio $S$—the ratio of the on-axis intensity to the diffraction-limited peak—satisfies

$$ S \approx e^{-\sigma_\phi^2}, $$

where $\sigma_\phi^2$ is the variance of the residual wavefront phase in radians squared. AO systems aim to reduce $\sigma_\phi^2$ below $1\,\text{rad}^2$ (corresponding to $S > 0.37$); high-performance systems achieve $\sigma_\phi^2 \lesssim 0.1\,\text{rad}^2$, yielding $S > 0.90$.

Lab Exercise. Using a spatial light modulator to simulate atmospheric turbulence, introduce a random phase screen with a specified $r_0 = 5\,\text{cm}$ at $\lambda = 633\,\text{nm}$. Record the point-spread function (PSF) of a HeNe laser focused through the screen. Then activate a Shack–Hartmann wavefront sensor and deformable mirror in closed-loop correction. Measure the Strehl ratio before and after AO correction, and compare the ratio $S_\text{corrected}/S_\text{uncorrected}$ to the prediction from the Marechal approximation using the sensor-reported residual phase variance.
