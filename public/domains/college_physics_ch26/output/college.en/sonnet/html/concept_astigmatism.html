🍃 Astigmatism

🍃 Astigmatism

Definition. Astigmatism is an optical aberration in which a lens or mirror fails to bring light rays originating from a single point object to a single focal point. Instead, rays lying in two orthogonal planes — the sagittal and tangential (meridional) planes — focus at different axial positions, producing two separate line foci rather than one sharp point. In the human eye, astigmatism typically arises from a cornea whose curvature varies with meridian: rather than being spherically symmetric, the corneal surface approximates a torus, with principal radii of curvature $R_1$ and $R_2$ satisfying $R_1 \neq R_2$. The corresponding refractive powers in the two principal planes are

$$ P_1 = \frac{n' - n}{R_1}, \qquad P_2 = \frac{n' - n}{R_2}, $$

where $n$ and $n'$ are the refractive indices on either side of the surface. The difference $\Delta P = P_1 - P_2$ quantifies the degree of astigmatism, measured clinically in diopters (D).

Worked Example. A patient's cornea has radii of curvature $R_1 = 7.8\ \text{mm}$ in the horizontal meridian and $R_2 = 7.2\ \text{mm}$ in the vertical meridian. Taking $n = 1.000$ (air) and $n' = 1.376$ (corneal tissue), compute the astigmatic difference:

$$ P_1 = \frac{1.376 - 1.000}{0.0078\ \text{m}} \approx 48.2\ \text{D}, \qquad P_2 = \frac{0.376}{0.0072} \approx 52.2\ \text{D}. $$

Thus $\Delta P \approx 4.0\ \text{D}$, a clinically significant astigmatism requiring correction.

Key Theorem (Sturm's Conoid). For a thin astigmatic lens with principal powers $P_1$ and $P_2$, a point source at object distance $u$ produces two line foci at image distances $v_1$ and $v_2$ given by $\frac{1}{v_i} = P_i + \frac{1}{u}$. Between these foci lies the circle of least confusion at position $v_c = \frac{2v_1 v_2}{v_1 + v_2}$, where image blur is minimized. The interval $|v_1 - v_2|$ is called the interval of Sturm.

Lab Exercise. Using a cylindrical lens of known power and a point-source laser, locate both line foci on an optical bench by translating a screen axially until each line image achieves minimum width. Record $v_1$ and $v_2$, compute the interval of Sturm, and verify the predicted position of the circle of least confusion. Compare your measured $\Delta P$ to the lens specification.
