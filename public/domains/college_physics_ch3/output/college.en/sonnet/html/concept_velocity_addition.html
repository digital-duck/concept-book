🍃 Velocity Addition

🍃 Velocity Addition

Definition. When an object moves within a reference frame that is itself in motion relative to a second, stationary observer, the velocity of the object as measured by the stationary observer is obtained by vector addition of the two velocities. For one-dimensional motion, if frame $S'$ moves with velocity $\vec{v}_{S'}$ relative to frame $S$, and an object moves with velocity $\vec{v}'$ relative to $S'$, then the object's velocity relative to $S$ is

$$ \vec{v} = \vec{v}' + \vec{v}_{S'}. $$

In two dimensions, the same principle applies component-wise:

$$ v_x = v'_x + v_{S',x}, \qquad v_y = v'_y + v_{S',y}. $$

This is the Galilean velocity addition formula, valid when all speeds are negligible compared to the speed of light.

Worked Example. A riverboat heads directly across a river of width $d = 120\ \mathrm{m}$ with speed $v'_x = 4.0\ \mathrm{m/s}$ relative to the water. The river current flows downstream at $v_{S',y} = 3.0\ \mathrm{m/s}$. Find the boat's speed and direction as seen by an observer on the bank.

Applying the addition formula:

$$ v_x = v'_x = 4.0\ \mathrm{m/s}, \qquad v_y = v_{S',y} = 3.0\ \mathrm{m/s}. $$

The resultant speed is

$$ v = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2} = \sqrt{(4.0)^2 + (3.0)^2} = 5.0\ \mathrm{m/s}. $$

The angle downstream from the perpendicular is

$$ \theta = \arctan\!\left(\frac{v_y}{v_x}\right) = \arctan(0.75) \approx 36.9°. $$

Key Theorem (Frame Independence of Acceleration). Under Galilean relativity, if $\vec{v}_{S'}$ is constant, differentiating $\vec{v} = \vec{v}' + \vec{v}_{S'}$ with respect to time gives $\vec{a} = \vec{a}'$. Acceleration—and therefore the net force via Newton's second law—is identical in all inertial frames. This invariance underpins the classical principle of relativity: the laws of mechanics take the same form in every inertial reference frame, with no experiment capable of distinguishing one from another by purely mechanical means.

Lab Exercise. Set up a low-friction cart on a rolling platform. Measure the cart's velocity $v'$ relative to the platform using a motion sensor, and the platform's velocity $v_{S'}$ relative to the floor using a second sensor. Verify that the cart's velocity relative to the floor satisfies $v = v' + v_{S'}$ for at least three different platform speeds, and compute the percent deviation from the predicted sum.
