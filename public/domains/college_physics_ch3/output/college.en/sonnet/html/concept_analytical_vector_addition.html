🍃 Analytical Vector Addition

🍃 Analytical Vector Addition

Definition. Analytical vector addition decomposes each vector into orthogonal scalar components along a chosen coordinate system, performs ordinary arithmetic on like components, and reconstructs the resultant vector from the summed components. For a vector $\vec{A}$ of magnitude $A$ oriented at angle $\theta$ measured counterclockwise from the positive $x$-axis, the components are

$$ A_x = A\cos\theta, \qquad A_y = A\sin\theta. $$

Given $n$ vectors $\vec{A}_1, \vec{A}_2, \ldots, \vec{A}_n$, the resultant $\vec{R}$ satisfies

$$ R_x = \sum_{i=1}^n A_{ix}, \qquad R_y = \sum_{i=1}^n A_{iy}. $$

The magnitude and direction of $\vec{R}$ are then recovered by

$$ R = \sqrt{R_x^2 + R_y^2}, \qquad \phi = \arctan\!\left(\frac{R_y}{R_x}\right), $$

where $\phi$ must be placed in the correct quadrant by inspecting the signs of $R_x$ and $R_y$.

Worked Example. A hiker walks $\vec{A} = 30\,\text{m}$ at $30°$ north of east, then $\vec{B} = 20\,\text{m}$ due north. Find the resultant displacement.

Decompose each vector:

$$ A_x = 30\cos 30° \approx 25.98\,\text{m}, \quad A_y = 30\sin 30° = 15.00\,\text{m}. $$ $$ B_x = 20\cos 90° = 0\,\text{m}, \quad B_y = 20\sin 90° = 20.00\,\text{m}. $$

Sum the components:

$$ R_x = 25.98 + 0 = 25.98\,\text{m}, \qquad R_y = 15.00 + 20.00 = 35.00\,\text{m}. $$

Reconstruct the resultant:

$$ R = \sqrt{(25.98)^2 + (35.00)^2} \approx \sqrt{675.0 + 1225.0} \approx 43.6\,\text{m}, $$ $$ \phi = \arctan\!\left(\frac{35.00}{25.98}\right) \approx 53.4°\text{ north of east.} $$

Key Theorem. Vector addition is commutative: $\vec{A}+\vec{B} = \vec{B}+\vec{A}$. This follows immediately from the component representation—swapping the order of summation in $R_x = A_x + B_x$ and $R_y = A_y + B_y$ leaves the sums unchanged, since real-number addition is commutative. No additional structure beyond component arithmetic is required; associativity follows by the same argument applied to three or more vectors.
