🍃 Relative Velocity

🍃 Relative Velocity

Definition. The velocity of an object depends on the reference frame from which it is observed. If frame $S'$ moves with constant velocity $\vec{v}_{S'S}$ relative to frame $S$, and an object $P$ has velocity $\vec{v}_{PS'}$ as measured in $S'$, then the velocity of $P$ as measured in $S$ is given by the Galilean velocity addition rule:

$$ \vec{v}_{PS} = \vec{v}_{PS'} + \vec{v}_{S'S} $$

This relation is a vector equation; directions matter. The subscript notation $\vec{v}_{AB}$ denotes the velocity of $A$ as observed from $B$. The mnemonic "inner subscripts cancel" follows directly: in $\vec{v}_{PS'} + \vec{v}_{S'S}$, the repeated index $S'$ cancels to yield $\vec{v}_{PS}$.

Worked Example. A river flows due east at $\vec{v}_{rg} = 3.0\,\hat{x}\ \text{m/s}$ relative to the ground. A boat heads due north at $\vec{v}_{br} = 4.0\,\hat{y}\ \text{m/s}$ relative to the river. Find the boat's velocity relative to the ground.

Applying the addition rule with $S'$ = river and $S$ = ground:

$$ \vec{v}_{bg} = \vec{v}_{br} + \vec{v}_{rg} = 4.0\,\hat{y} + 3.0\,\hat{x}\ \text{m/s} $$

The magnitude is:

$$ |\vec{v}_{bg}| = \sqrt{(3.0)^2 + (4.0)^2} = 5.0\ \text{m/s} $$

and the direction is:

$$ \theta = \arctan\!\left(\frac{3.0}{4.0}\right) \approx 36.9^\circ \text{ east of north} $$

Key Theorem. Galilean relativity asserts that the laws of classical mechanics are invariant under the transformation $\vec{v}_{PS} = \vec{v}_{PS'} + \vec{v}_{S'S}$, provided $\vec{v}_{S'S}$ is constant (inertial frames). Consequently, no mechanical experiment performed within an inertial frame can distinguish that frame from rest. This principle breaks down at speeds approaching $c$, where it is superseded by the Lorentz transformation of special relativity.

Lab Exercise. Two lab carts move along the same track. Cart $A$ travels at $2.0\ \text{m/s}$ east relative to the lab. Cart $B$ travels at $5.0\ \text{m/s}$ east relative to $A$. (a) Determine the velocity of $B$ relative to the lab. (b) Determine the velocity of $A$ relative to $B$. (c) If $B$ instead travels at $5.0\ \text{m/s}$ west relative to $A$, repeat both calculations and compare the magnitudes.
