🍃 Free Fall

🍃 Free Fall

Definition. Free fall is the motion of an object subject solely to gravitational acceleration, with all other forces (air resistance, normal force, etc.) neglected. Near Earth's surface, this acceleration is directed downward and has the constant magnitude $g = 9.80\ \text{m/s}^2$. Adopting the convention that upward is positive, the kinematic equations for free fall become:

$$ v = v_0 - gt $$ $$ y = y_0 + v_0 t - \tfrac{1}{2}g t^2 $$ $$ v^2 = v_0^2 - 2g(y - y_0) $$

These are the standard constant-acceleration equations with $a = -g$. The independence of free-fall motion from the object's mass follows directly from the equivalence of gravitational and inertial mass, a result confirmed experimentally to high precision.

Worked Example. A stone is thrown vertically upward from ground level ($y_0 = 0$) with initial velocity $v_0 = 15.0\ \text{m/s}$. Find (a) the maximum height reached and (b) the time to return to the ground.

(a) At maximum height, $v = 0$. Using $v^2 = v_0^2 - 2gy_{\max}$:

$$ y_{\max} = \frac{v_0^2}{2g} = \frac{(15.0)^2}{2(9.80)} = \frac{225}{19.6} \approx 11.5\ \text{m} $$

(b) Setting $y = 0$ in the displacement equation and factoring:

$$ 0 = t\!\left(v_0 - \tfrac{1}{2}g t\right) \implies t = 0\ \text{(launch)}\quad \text{or}\quad t = \frac{2v_0}{g} = \frac{2(15.0)}{9.80} \approx 3.06\ \text{s} $$

Key Theorem (Symmetry of Free Fall). For any object launched vertically and returning to its launch height, the time of ascent equals the time of descent, and the speed at any given height is the same on the way up as on the way down. This follows because the equations of motion under constant acceleration are time-reversible: substituting $t \to T - t$ (where $T$ is total flight time) maps the upward trajectory onto the downward trajectory exactly.

Lab Exercise. Using a motion sensor or high-speed camera, drop a dense ball from rest at a measured height $h$. Record the time of fall $t_f$ and compute $g_{\text{exp}} = 2h/t_f^2$. Repeat five times, compute the mean and standard deviation, and compare your value to $9.80\ \text{m/s}^2$. Investigate how the result changes if you use a crumpled sheet of paper instead, and identify which assumption of free fall is violated.
