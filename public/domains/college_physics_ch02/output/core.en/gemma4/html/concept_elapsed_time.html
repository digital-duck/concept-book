🍃 Elapsed Time

Have you ever been late because you underestimated how long a task would take? Calculating elapsed time is one of the most practical skills in mathematics, allowing us to accurately measure the duration between two points in time. It helps us manage schedules, estimate travel times, and even plan cooking meals! If we only know the start time and the end time, determining how long that process took requires understanding the concept of elapsed time.

Definition: Elapsed time is the total amount of time that passes between a specific starting point (the initial time) and an ending point (the final time). Mathematically, if $T_{start}$ is the start time and $T_{end}$ is the end time, the elapsed duration $\Delta T$ must be calculated by finding the difference:

$$ \Delta T = T_{end} - T_{start} $$

Worked Example: A movie started at 4:15 PM and finished at 6:00 PM. What was the elapsed time? We can break this down into segments. From 4:15 PM to 5:00 PM is $45$ minutes. From 5:00 PM to 6:00 PM is $1$ hour. Therefore, the total elapsed time is $1 \text{ hour} + 45 \text{ minutes}$, or $\mathbf{1 \text{ hour and } 45 \text{ minutes}}$.

Why It's True: While simple subtraction works for consistent units (e.g., finding the difference between 8:00 and 9:00 is $1$ hour), elapsed time often crosses boundaries, such as crossing an hour mark or a day boundary. To ensure accuracy, we must first convert all times into a standard unit (like minutes since midnight) before performing subtraction if standard clock arithmetic fails. The principle remains the same: duration is always measured by subtracting the initial value from the final value.

Practice Problem: A train departed at 7:30 AM and arrived at its destination at 11:50 AM. Calculate the total elapsed time in hours and minutes.
