🌸 High Voltage Power Transmission

🌸 High Voltage Power Transmission

Definition. High voltage power transmission is the practice of conveying electrical energy over long distances using alternating current (AC) at voltages significantly elevated above the generation or consumption levels—typically 115 kV to 765 kV in modern grids. The governing principle derives from the relationship between power, voltage, and current. For a transmission line carrying power $P$ at voltage $V$, the line current is

$$ I = \frac{P}{V} $$

(assuming unity power factor). The resistive power loss in a line of resistance $R$ is

$$ P_{\text{loss}} = I^2 R = \frac{P^2 R}{V^2} $$

Doubling the transmission voltage therefore reduces resistive losses by a factor of four, making high-voltage operation essential for economic and efficient energy delivery.

Worked Example. A generating station produces $P = 500\,\text{MW}$ of power. The transmission line has total resistance $R = 10\,\Omega$. Compare losses at $V_1 = 20\,\text{kV}$ versus $V_2 = 500\,\text{kV}$.

At $V_1 = 20\,\text{kV}$:

$$ I_1 = \frac{500 \times 10^6}{20 \times 10^3} = 25{,}000\,\text{A}, \quad P_{\text{loss},1} = (25{,}000)^2 \times 10 = 6{,}250\,\text{MW} $$

This exceeds the generated power—transmission at 20 kV is physically impossible at this scale.

At $V_2 = 500\,\text{kV}$:

$$ I_2 = \frac{500 \times 10^6}{500 \times 10^3} = 1{,}000\,\text{A}, \quad P_{\text{loss},2} = (1{,}000)^2 \times 10 = 10\,\text{MW} $$

This represents only 2% of the transmitted power—an operationally acceptable loss.

Key Theorem (Transformer Voltage-Current Reciprocity). An ideal transformer with turns ratio $n = N_s / N_p$ satisfies

$$ \frac{V_s}{V_p} = n, \qquad \frac{I_s}{I_p} = \frac{1}{n} $$

Conservation of power ($V_p I_p = V_s I_s$) is preserved exactly. Step-up transformers at the generation end raise voltage (and proportionally reduce current), while step-down transformers at the distribution end restore safe consumer voltages. This reciprocity is what makes the $V^{-2}$ loss reduction practically achievable.

Lab Exercise. Using a variable autotransformer and a resistive load of $R_L = 50\,\Omega$ to simulate a transmission line loss of $R = 2\,\Omega$, measure the delivered power and line loss at stepped output voltages of 50 V, 100 V, and 200 V. Plot $P_{\text{loss}}/P_{\text{total}}$ versus $V$ and verify that the ratio follows the predicted $V^{-2}$ dependence. Discuss at what voltage the efficiency exceeds 95%.
