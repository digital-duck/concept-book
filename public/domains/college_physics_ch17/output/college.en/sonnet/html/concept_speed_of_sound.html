🍃 Speed Of Sound

🍃 Speed Of Sound

Definition. The speed of sound is the rate at which a mechanical pressure wave propagates through a medium. For an ideal fluid or gas, it is determined by the medium's bulk modulus $B$ and equilibrium density $\rho_0$:

$$ v = \sqrt{\frac{B}{\rho_0}} $$

For an ideal gas undergoing adiabatic compression—the physically correct model for audible sound—this reduces to

$$ v = \sqrt{\frac{\gamma P_0}{\rho_0}} = \sqrt{\frac{\gamma R T}{M}} $$

where $\gamma = C_P/C_V$ is the adiabatic index, $R = 8.314\ \text{J mol}^{-1}\text{K}^{-1}$ is the universal gas constant, $T$ is the absolute temperature in kelvin, and $M$ is the molar mass of the gas in $\text{kg mol}^{-1}$. For dry air, $\gamma \approx 1.40$ and $M \approx 0.02896\ \text{kg mol}^{-1}$, yielding the familiar approximation $v \approx 331\sqrt{T/273}\ \text{m s}^{-1}$.

Worked Example. Find the speed of sound in dry air at $T = 20\ ^\circ\text{C} = 293\ \text{K}$.

$$ v = \sqrt{\frac{(1.40)(8.314)(293)}{0.02896}} = \sqrt{\frac{3413.5}{0.02896}} \approx \sqrt{117{,}869} \approx 343\ \text{m s}^{-1} $$

This agrees with the commonly cited value and confirms that sound travels approximately $1\ \text{km}$ every $2.9\ \text{s}$ at room temperature.

Key Theorem. Because $v \propto \sqrt{T}$, the speed of sound depends on temperature but is independent of static pressure at constant temperature. This follows directly from the ideal-gas relation $P_0/\rho_0 = RT/M$: when pressure rises, density rises proportionally, so the ratio $P_0/\rho_0$ remains fixed at constant $T$. Consequently, sound travels faster in warmer air and slower in cooler air, a dependence that governs how sound beams curve as they pass through air masses of varying temperature.

Lab Exercise. Using two microphones separated by a measured distance $d$ and a digital oscilloscope, record the time delay $\Delta t$ between the arrival of a sharp impulse (a hand clap). Compute $v = d/\Delta t$. Repeat the measurement at three different ambient temperatures (e.g., indoors, outdoors in shade, outdoors in sunlight) and compare your results to the theoretical prediction $v = 331\sqrt{T/273}\ \text{m s}^{-1}$. Quantify the percentage discrepancy and discuss possible sources of systematic error, including humidity and wind.
