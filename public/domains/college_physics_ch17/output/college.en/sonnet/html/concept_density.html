🌱 Density

🌱 Density

Definition. The density of a substance is defined as its mass per unit volume:

$$ \rho = \frac{m}{V} $$

where $\rho$ (rho) is the density in kilograms per cubic meter ($\text{kg/m}^3$), $m$ is the mass in kilograms, and $V$ is the volume in cubic meters. Density is an intensive property — it characterizes the material itself, independent of the sample size. For a non-uniform object, the local density at a point is defined as the limiting ratio $\rho = \lim_{\Delta V \to 0} \frac{\Delta m}{\Delta V}$, which reduces to the uniform definition when the material is homogeneous.

Common reference values: water at $4^\circ\text{C}$ has $\rho = 1000\ \text{kg/m}^3$, air at standard conditions has $\rho \approx 1.29\ \text{kg/m}^3$, and lead has $\rho \approx 11{,}300\ \text{kg/m}^3$.

Worked Example. A solid aluminum cylinder has a measured mass of $m = 0.540\ \text{kg}$ and dimensions: radius $r = 2.00\ \text{cm} = 0.0200\ \text{m}$, height $h = 15.0\ \text{cm} = 0.150\ \text{m}$. Find the density.

First, compute the volume:

$$ V = \pi r^2 h = \pi (0.0200)^2 (0.150) = \pi (4.00 \times 10^{-4})(0.150) \approx 1.885 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{m}^3 $$

Then:

$$ \rho = \frac{m}{V} = \frac{0.540}{1.885 \times 10^{-4}} \approx 2{,}870\ \text{kg/m}^3 $$

The accepted value for aluminum is $2{,}700\ \text{kg/m}^3$, placing this result within reasonable experimental agreement.

Key Theorem (Archimedes' Principle). When an object is submerged in a fluid of density $\rho_f$, the buoyant force equals the weight of displaced fluid:

$$ F_b = \rho_f V_{\text{submerged}}\, g $$

An object floats when $\rho_{\text{object}} \leq \rho_{\text{fluid}}$, and sinks when $\rho_{\text{object}} > \rho_{\text{fluid}}$. This follows directly from the definition of density combined with hydrostatic pressure analysis.

Lab Exercise. Using a digital balance and a graduated cylinder partially filled with water, determine the density of an irregular stone by the water-displacement method. Record the initial water volume $V_1$, submerge the stone, and record $V_2$. Compute $V = V_2 - V_1$ and measure the stone's mass $m$ directly. Calculate $\rho = m/V$ and compare with published values for granite ($\approx 2{,}700\ \text{kg/m}^3$). Estimate the percent uncertainty by propagating errors in $m$ and $V$.
