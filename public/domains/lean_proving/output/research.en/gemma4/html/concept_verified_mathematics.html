🌸 Verified Mathematics

Definition. Verified mathematics constitutes the systematic endeavor of formalizing entire mathematical theories within a machine-checkable framework, thereby transforming potentially fallible human arguments into finite sequences of logical deductions verifiable by automated theorem provers (ATPs) or proof assistants. Given a theory $\mathcal{T}$, it is formally verified if its axioms and all associated theorems are proven within a foundational type theory $\mathcal{F}$—such as Homotopy Type Theory (HoTT)—which employs dependent types. Specifically, the deduction system $\vdash_{\mathcal{F}}$ must guarantee consistency relative to standard mathematical logic models (e.g., ZFC or constructive set theory), ensuring that propositions are viewed as types and proofs are realized as terms inhabiting those types.

Theorem. If a theorem $A$ is provable ($\vdash$) within a formal proof assistant utilizing a type theory $\mathcal{F}$, then its truth value, $A$, is guaranteed by the computational soundness of the underlying system, provided that $\mathcal{F}$ itself satisfies the consistency condition of $\text{Con}(\mathcal{F})$. Formally, if $\vdash_{\text{formal}}(A)$, then $A$ holds in all standard models of arithmetic and set theory; this equivalence is only conditional on the consistency assumption $\text{Con}(\mathcal{F})$.

Proof. The foundation for this assurance rests critically upon the Curry-Howard correspondence, which establishes a fundamental isomorphism between types in constructive logic and computational terms. Mathematically, a proof of $A$ is computationally realized as a term $t$ inhabiting the type corresponding to $A$, denoted $\text{Type}(A)$. The soundness property of the formal system $\mathcal{F}$ ensures that if such a term $t$ exists—and can be successfully synthesized by the proof assistant—it corresponds uniquely to a valid deduction chain. Consequently, acceptance confirms both the syntactic validity (adherence to established inference rules) and the semantic truth (the proposition $A$ holds), provided the system's type checking mechanism validates that the term $t$ is well-formed with respect to $\mathcal{F}$'s type judgments.

Remark. The methodology represents a pivotal shift from meta-mathematical concerns, such as Gödelian incompleteness or consistency proofs for foundational systems like Peano Arithmetic (PA), toward mechanical verification of specific, highly complex mathematical structures. While early efforts focused on traditional arithmetic and set theory, modern verified mathematics increasingly leverages advanced type theories and dependent types to tackle areas like algebraic topology, where frameworks such as Homotopy Type Theory provide richer expressive power concerning higher-dimensional structures. This rigorous approach enables machine-checked validation mechanisms that function as a definitive alternative to conventional peer review in certifying mathematical results.
