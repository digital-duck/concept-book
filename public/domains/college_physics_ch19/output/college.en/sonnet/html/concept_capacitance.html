🍃 Capacitance

🍃 Capacitance

Definition. A capacitor is any pair of conductors separated by an insulating medium (or vacuum) that stores energy in an electric field. The capacitance $C$ of such a system is defined as the ratio of the magnitude of charge $Q$ stored on either conductor to the magnitude of the potential difference $V$ between them:

$$ C = \frac{Q}{V} $$

The SI unit of capacitance is the farad (F), where $1\,\text{F} = 1\,\text{C/V}$. Capacitance is a purely geometric property of the conductor configuration; it is independent of $Q$ and $V$ individually, depending only on the shape, size, and separation of the conductors and the permittivity of the intervening medium.

Worked Example. Consider a parallel-plate capacitor with plate area $A = 2.00 \times 10^{-2}\,\text{m}^2$ and plate separation $d = 1.00\,\text{mm} = 1.00 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{m}$, with vacuum between the plates. The electric field between infinite parallel plates is uniform, so the potential difference satisfies $V = Ed$. Using Gauss's law, the field is $E = \sigma/\varepsilon_0 = Q/(\varepsilon_0 A)$, giving:

$$ C = \frac{Q}{V} = \frac{Q}{Ed} = \frac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d} = \frac{(8.85 \times 10^{-12}\,\text{F/m})(2.00 \times 10^{-2}\,\text{m}^2)}{1.00 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{m}} = 1.77 \times 10^{-10}\,\text{F} \approx 177\,\text{pF} $$

If this capacitor is charged to $V = 12.0\,\text{V}$, the stored charge is $Q = CV = (1.77 \times 10^{-10}\,\text{F})(12.0\,\text{V}) = 2.12\,\text{nC}$.

Key Theorem. For a parallel-plate capacitor with a dielectric of permittivity $\varepsilon = \kappa\varepsilon_0$ filling the gap, the capacitance generalizes to:

$$ C = \frac{\kappa\varepsilon_0 A}{d} $$

where $\kappa \geq 1$ is the dielectric constant. Inserting a dielectric increases $C$ by the factor $\kappa$ because polarization of the dielectric reduces the effective electric field, lowering $V$ for the same stored charge.

Lab Exercise. Using a capacitance meter, measure the capacitance of a parallel-plate capacitor as you systematically vary the plate separation $d$ from 1 mm to 10 mm in 1 mm steps, keeping $A$ fixed. Plot $C$ versus $1/d$ and extract $\varepsilon_0 A$ from the slope. Compare your experimental value of $\varepsilon_0$ to the accepted value $8.85 \times 10^{-12}\,\text{F/m}$ and discuss sources of deviation.
