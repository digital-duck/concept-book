🌱 Coulomb Force

🌱 Coulomb Force

Definition. The Coulomb force is the electrostatic interaction between two point charges at rest. If charge $q_1$ is located at the origin and charge $q_2$ is separated from it by a distance $r$, the magnitude of the force each charge exerts on the other is

$$ F = k_e \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}, $$

where $k_e = 8.988 \times 10^9\ \mathrm{N \cdot m^2 \cdot C^{-2}}$ is Coulomb's constant, related to the permittivity of free space by $k_e = \dfrac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}$, with $\epsilon_0 = 8.854 \times 10^{-12}\ \mathrm{C^2 \cdot N^{-1} \cdot m^{-2}}$. The force is repulsive when the charges share the same sign and attractive when they are opposite in sign. In vector form,

$$ \vec{F}_{12} = k_e \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2}\hat{r}_{12}, $$

where $\hat{r}_{12}$ is the unit vector pointing from $q_1$ toward $q_2$.

Worked Example. Two point charges, $q_1 = +3.0\ \mu\mathrm{C}$ and $q_2 = -5.0\ \mu\mathrm{C}$, are separated by $r = 0.20\ \mathrm{m}$ in vacuum. Find the magnitude of the force between them.

$$ F = k_e \frac{|q_1||q_2|}{r^2} = (8.988 \times 10^9) \frac{(3.0 \times 10^{-6})(5.0 \times 10^{-6})}{(0.20)^2} $$ $$ F = (8.988 \times 10^9) \frac{1.5 \times 10^{-11}}{4.0 \times 10^{-2}} = (8.988 \times 10^9)(3.75 \times 10^{-10}) \approx 3.4\ \mathrm{N}. $$

Because the charges are opposite, this force is attractive, pulling each charge toward the other.

Key Theorem — Superposition Principle. For a system of $n$ point charges, the net Coulomb force on charge $q$ is the vector sum of the individual pairwise forces:

$$ \vec{F}_{\mathrm{net}} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \vec{F}_i = k_e q \sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{q_i}{r_i^2}\hat{r}_i. $$

This linearity means fields and forces from multiple sources add independently, a cornerstone of classical electrostatics.

Lab Exercise. Place three charges along the $x$-axis: $q_1 = +2.0\ \mu\mathrm{C}$ at $x = 0$, $q_2 = -2.0\ \mu\mathrm{C}$ at $x = 0.30\ \mathrm{m}$, and $q_3 = +4.0\ \mu\mathrm{C}$ at $x = 0.60\ \mathrm{m}$. Calculate the magnitude and direction of the net Coulomb force on $q_2$, specifying clearly whether each contributing force is attractive or repulsive.
