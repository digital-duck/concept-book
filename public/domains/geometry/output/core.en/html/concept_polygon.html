Polygon

Definition. A polygon is a closed plane figure formed by a finite sequence of line segments—called sides or edges—such that each segment shares exactly one endpoint with the next, and the last segment connects back to the first. Formally, a polygon $P$ with $n$ sides ($n \geq 3$) is determined by an ordered set of vertices $\{V_1, V_2, \ldots, V_n\} \subset \mathbb{R}^2$, with edges $\overline{V_1V_2},\, \overline{V_2V_3},\, \ldots,\, \overline{V_{n-1}V_n},\, \overline{V_nV_1}$. A polygon is called simple if no two non-adjacent edges intersect; all results in this section assume simplicity.

Worked Example. Consider the regular hexagon with center at the origin and circumradius $R = 2$. Its six vertices are given by

$$V_k = \left(2\cos\!\left(\frac{2\pi(k-1)}{6}\right),\; 2\sin\!\left(\frac{2\pi(k-1)}{6}\right)\right), \quad k = 1, 2, \ldots, 6.$$

Computing explicitly: $V_1 = (2, 0)$, $V_2 = (1, \sqrt{3})$, $V_3 = (-1, \sqrt{3})$, and so on. The side length equals $s = R = 2$, and the interior angle at each vertex is $\dfrac{(6-2)\cdot 180^\circ}{6} = 120^\circ$.

Key Theorem (Interior Angle Sum). For any simple polygon with $n$ vertices, the sum of interior angles is

$$\sum_{i=1}^{n} \theta_i = (n - 2)\cdot \pi \text{ radians} = (n-2)\cdot 180^\circ.$$

Proof sketch. Triangulate the polygon by drawing $n - 3$ non-crossing diagonals from a single vertex, producing exactly $n - 2$ triangles. Since each triangle's angles sum to $\pi$, and every triangle angle is an interior angle of $P$, the total is $(n-2)\pi$. $\square$

The area of a simple polygon whose vertices are listed in order is computed via the Shoelace Formula:

$$A = \frac{1}{2}\left|\sum_{i=1}^{n}\bigl(x_i\, y_{i+1} - x_{i+1}\, y_i\bigr)\right|,$$

where indices are taken modulo $n$.

Lab Cell (SymPy).

from sympy import cos, sin, pi, simplify, Polygon, Point

# Regular hexagon with circumradius R = 2
R, n = 2, 6
vertices = [Point(R*cos(2*pi*k/n), R*sin(2*pi*k/n)) for k in range(n)]
hex_poly = Polygon(*vertices)

print("Area:              ", simplify(hex_poly.area))       # 6√3
print("Perimeter:         ", simplify(hex_poly.perimeter))  # 12
print("Interior angle sum:", (n - 2) * 180, "degrees")      # 720

Running this confirms $A = 6\sqrt{3} \approx 10.39$ and perimeter $= 12$, consistent with the formula $A = \tfrac{3\sqrt{3}}{2}s^2$ for a regular hexagon of side $s = 2$.
