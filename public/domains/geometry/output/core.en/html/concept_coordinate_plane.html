Coordinate Plane

Definition. The Cartesian coordinate plane (also called the xy-plane) is the set $\mathbb{R}^2 = \{(x, y) \mid x, y \in \mathbb{R}\}$, equipped with a distinguished point called the origin $O = (0, 0)$ and two mutually perpendicular directed lines called the coordinate axes. The horizontal axis is the $x$-axis and the vertical axis is the $y$-axis. Every point $P$ in the plane is uniquely identified by an ordered pair $(a, b)$, where $a$ is the abscissa (signed horizontal distance from the $y$-axis) and $b$ is the ordinate (signed vertical distance from the $x$-axis).

The axes divide the plane into four quadrants, labeled $\mathrm{I}$–$\mathrm{IV}$ counterclockwise from the positive $x$-axis:

Worked Example. Plot the points $A = (3, 2)$, $B = (-1, 4)$, and $C = (0, -3)$. Compute the distance $|AB|$.

Using the distance formula derived from the Pythagorean theorem:

$$|AB| = \sqrt{(3 - (-1))^2 + (2 - 4)^2} = \sqrt{16 + 4} = \sqrt{20} = 2\sqrt{5} \approx 4.47.$$

Point $A$ lies in Quadrant I, $B$ in Quadrant II, and $C$ on the negative $y$-axis (not in any quadrant).

Key Theorem (Distance Formula). For any two points $P_1 = (x_1, y_1)$ and $P_2 = (x_2, y_2)$ in the coordinate plane:

$$d(P_1, P_2) = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}.$$

Proof sketch. Form a right triangle whose hypotenuse connects $P_1$ to $P_2$. The horizontal leg runs parallel to the $x$-axis and has length $|x_2 - x_1|$; the vertical leg runs parallel to the $y$-axis and has length $|y_2 - y_1|$. Because the coordinate axes are perpendicular by definition, these legs are themselves perpendicular. The Pythagorean theorem then gives $d^2 = (x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2$, and taking the positive square root yields the formula. $\square$

Lab Cell (SymPy).

from sympy import sqrt, Point
A, B = Point(3, 2), Point(-1, 4)
print(A.distance(B))   # 2*sqrt(5)
