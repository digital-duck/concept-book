Angle

Definition. Let $\mathbf{u}$ and $\mathbf{v}$ be two nonzero vectors in $\mathbb{R}^n$. The angle $\theta$ between them is the unique value in $[0, \pi]$ satisfying

$$\cos\theta = \frac{\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v}}{\|\mathbf{u}\|\,\|\mathbf{v}\|},$$

where $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} u_i v_i$ is the Euclidean inner product and $\|\mathbf{u}\| = \sqrt{\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{u}}$ is the Euclidean norm. This definition extends the familiar geometric notion of angle to arbitrary finite dimensions.

Worked Example. Let $\mathbf{u} = (1, 2, 2)$ and $\mathbf{v} = (4, 0, -3)$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$. Compute:

$$\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} = (1)(4) + (2)(0) + (2)(-3) = 4 + 0 - 6 = -2,$$ $$\|\mathbf{u}\| = \sqrt{1 + 4 + 4} = 3, \qquad \|\mathbf{v}\| = \sqrt{16 + 0 + 9} = 5.$$

Therefore,

$$\cos\theta = \frac{-2}{3 \cdot 5} = -\frac{2}{15}, \qquad \theta = \arccos\!\left(-\tfrac{2}{15}\right) \approx 97.7°.$$

Since $\cos\theta < 0$, the vectors form an obtuse angle, which is geometrically consistent with $\mathbf{v}$ having a component directly opposing $\mathbf{u}$.

Key Theorem (Cauchy–Schwarz Inequality). For all $\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v} \in \mathbb{R}^n$,

$$|\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v}| \leq \|\mathbf{u}\|\,\|\mathbf{v}\|,$$

with equality if and only if $\mathbf{u}$ and $\mathbf{v}$ are linearly dependent. This inequality guarantees that the ratio $\dfrac{\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v}}{\|\mathbf{u}\|\,\|\mathbf{v}\|}$ always lies in $[-1, 1]$, making the definition of $\theta$ via $\arccos$ well-posed in any dimension.

Proof sketch. Consider $f(t) = \|\mathbf{u} + t\mathbf{v}\|^2 \geq 0$ for all $t \in \mathbb{R}$. Expanding yields a quadratic in $t$ whose discriminant must be non-positive, giving $(\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v})^2 \leq \|\mathbf{u}\|^2\|\mathbf{v}\|^2$. $\square$

Lab Cell (SymPy).

import sympy as sp

u = sp.Matrix([1, 2, 2])
v = sp.Matrix([4, 0, -3])

cos_theta = u.dot(v) / (u.norm() * v.norm())
theta = sp.acos(cos_theta)
print(sp.simplify(theta))          # exact form
print(float(theta.evalf()) * 180 / float(sp.pi))  # degrees
