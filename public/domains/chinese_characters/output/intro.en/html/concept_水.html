🌱 水

🌱 水

Everyday example Turn on a tap and watch what happens. Water flows downward, splits around obstacles, and always finds the lowest path. The Chinese character 水 (shuǐ) captures exactly this image — a stream of liquid tumbling downhill.

Picture this Look at the character 水. The vertical stroke in the center is the main current of a river. The shorter strokes on either side are splashes and tributaries branching off as the water rushes past rocks. Imagine standing on a bridge, looking down at a creek — the central flow with little ripples curling away on both sides. That is what the ancient writers were sketching.

Simple rule 水 means water in all its forms — drinking water, rivers, rain, oceans. When 水 appears inside other characters, it often shrinks into a three-dot pattern on the left side, called the "water radical" (氵). Whenever you spot those three dots, the character probably relates to liquid or flowing: 河 (river), 湖 (lake), 泪 (tears). Think of 氵 as a shorthand signature that says, "water was here."

Try it yourself Write 水 stroke by stroke: start with the left-falling stroke, then the vertical center, then the remaining strokes on either side. Now scan these characters — 海, 洗, 汤 — and find the hidden 氵. What water-related meaning might each one carry?
