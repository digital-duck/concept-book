🍃 Torque On Current Loop

🍃 Torque On Current Loop

Definition. When a planar current loop is placed in a uniform magnetic field, the field exerts forces on each current-carrying segment. Although the net force on the loop is zero (since the loop is closed), the forces on opposite sides generally act in opposing directions along a common axis, producing a net torque. For a rectangular loop of area $A = ab$ carrying current $I$ in a uniform field $\mathbf{B}$, the torque is

$$ \boldsymbol{\tau} = \mathbf{m} \times \mathbf{B} $$

where $\mathbf{m} = NIA\hat{n}$ is the magnetic dipole moment, $N$ is the number of turns, and $\hat{n}$ is the unit normal to the loop determined by the right-hand rule. The magnitude of the torque is

$$ \tau = NIAB\sin\theta $$

where $\theta$ is the angle between $\mathbf{m}$ and $\mathbf{B}$. Torque is maximized at $\theta = 90°$ and vanishes at $\theta = 0°$ and $\theta = 180°$ (equilibrium positions).

Worked Example. A 50-turn rectangular coil with dimensions $4.0\ \text{cm} \times 6.0\ \text{cm}$ carries a current of $2.0\ \text{A}$ in a uniform magnetic field of magnitude $0.30\ \text{T}$. The plane of the coil makes an angle of $30°$ with $\mathbf{B}$, so $\theta = 90° - 30° = 60°$ (the angle between $\hat{n}$ and $\mathbf{B}$).

$$ A = (0.04)(0.06) = 2.4 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}^2 $$ $$ \tau = NIAB\sin\theta = (50)(2.0)(2.4 \times 10^{-3})(0.30)\sin 60° $$ $$ \tau = 50 \times 2.0 \times 7.2 \times 10^{-4} \times 0.866 \approx 6.2 \times 10^{-2}\ \text{N·m} $$

Key Theorem. The torque expression $\boldsymbol{\tau} = \mathbf{m} \times \mathbf{B}$ is independent of the loop's shape; it holds for any planar loop of area $A$. This follows from integrating $d\boldsymbol{\tau} = \mathbf{r} \times d\mathbf{F}$ over the loop using $d\mathbf{F} = I(d\boldsymbol{\ell} \times \mathbf{B})$, where the cross terms reduce identically to $IA(\hat{n} \times \mathbf{B})$ for any closed planar contour. This universality underlies the operating principle of electric motors.

Lab Exercise. Wind a 20-turn coil of known dimensions around a cardboard frame, connect it to a variable DC source, and suspend it on a torsion fiber in a measured field from a Helmholtz coil pair. Record the equilibrium deflection angle as a function of current. Plot $\tau$ versus $I$ and verify linearity; extract $NAB$ from the slope and compare it to the directly computed value.
