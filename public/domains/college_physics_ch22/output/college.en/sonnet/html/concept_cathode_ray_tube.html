🌸 Cathode Ray Tube

🌸 Cathode Ray Tube

Definition. A cathode ray tube (CRT) is a vacuum-sealed glass envelope containing an electron gun, deflection system, and phosphorescent screen. The electron gun comprises a heated cathode that emits electrons via thermionic emission, an anode maintained at a large positive potential $V_a$ relative to the cathode, and focusing electrodes. Electrons emitted from the cathode are accelerated through the potential difference and emerge as a narrow beam—historically called "cathode rays"—traveling toward the screen at high speed. Electrostatic or magnetic deflection plates redirect the beam to illuminate specific points on the phosphor coating, which emits visible light upon electron impact.

Worked Example. Electrons are accelerated from rest through a potential difference of $V_a = 5.00 \times 10^3\,\text{V}$. Find the speed of the electrons as they exit the electron gun. Using conservation of energy:

$$ eV_a = \frac{1}{2}m_e v^2 $$

Solving for $v$:

$$ v = \sqrt{\frac{2eV_a}{m_e}} = \sqrt{\frac{2(1.602 \times 10^{-19}\,\text{C})(5.00 \times 10^3\,\text{V})}{9.109 \times 10^{-31}\,\text{kg}}} $$ $$ v \approx 4.19 \times 10^7\,\text{m/s} $$

This is approximately $14\%$ of the speed of light, confirming that classical mechanics applies to good approximation.

Key Theorem — Electrostatic Deflection. Once inside the deflection region (length $L$, plate separation $d$, deflecting voltage $V_d$), an electron experiences a transverse electric field $E = V_d/d$, producing a constant transverse acceleration $a = eE/m_e$. The transverse displacement on the screen located a distance $D$ beyond the plates is:

$$ y = \frac{V_d L}{2V_a d}\left(\frac{L}{2} + D\right) $$

This linear relationship between $y$ and $V_d$ is the operating principle behind oscilloscope CRTs, where time-varying voltages are displayed as waveforms.

Lab Exercise. In a CRT oscilloscope experiment, set $V_a = 3.00\,\text{kV}$, $L = 5.00\,\text{cm}$, $d = 1.20\,\text{cm}$, and $D = 20.0\,\text{cm}$. Apply a deflecting voltage of $V_d = 150\,\text{V}$. Calculate (a) the electron speed at the anode, (b) the transverse electric field between the deflection plates, and (c) the vertical displacement of the beam spot on the screen. Verify that the deflection sensitivity $y/V_d$ is independent of the electron charge-to-mass ratio.
