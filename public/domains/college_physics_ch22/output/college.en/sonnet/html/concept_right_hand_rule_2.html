🍃 Right Hand Rule 2

🍃 Right Hand Rule 2

Definition

Right Hand Rule 2 (RHR-2) is a mnemonic procedure for determining the direction of the magnetic field produced by a current-carrying conductor. Given a long, straight wire carrying conventional current $I$ in a specified direction, the magnetic field lines form concentric circles around the wire. To apply RHR-2: point the right thumb in the direction of the conventional current; the curled fingers then indicate the direction in which the magnetic field $\vec{B}$ circulates around the wire. This rule is a direct consequence of the Biot–Savart Law and Ampère's Law, both of which establish that the magnetic field of a straight wire is azimuthal and obeys

$$ B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}, $$

where $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7}\ \text{T·m/A}$ is the permeability of free space and $r$ is the perpendicular distance from the wire.

Worked Example

A vertical wire carries a current of $5.0\ \text{A}$ directed upward. Determine the direction and magnitude of $\vec{B}$ at a point $P$ located $0.10\ \text{m}$ directly to the east of the wire.

Direction: Point the right thumb upward (direction of $I$). The curled fingers at point $P$ (to the east) curl from north, through east, toward south. At the eastern point specifically, the fingers point south. Therefore, $\vec{B}$ points southward at $P$.

Magnitude:

$$ B = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7})(5.0)}{2\pi (0.10)} = \frac{2.0 \times 10^{-6}}{0.628} \approx 1.0 \times 10^{-5}\ \text{T}. $$

Key Theorem (Ampère's Law)

For any closed Amperian loop encircling a net current $I_{\text{enc}}$:

$$ \oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{\ell} = \mu_0 I_{\text{enc}}. $$

For a circular path of radius $r$ centered on a long straight wire, symmetry demands $B$ is constant along the path, yielding $B(2\pi r) = \mu_0 I$, which recovers the formula above and confirms that RHR-2 correctly identifies the circulation sense.

Lab Exercise

Set up a long vertical wire connected to a DC power supply. Using a compass placed at several positions around the wire (north, south, east, west at $r = 5\ \text{cm}$), record the compass needle orientation at each location. Predict each orientation using RHR-2 before observing, then compare. Reverse the current direction and repeat. Quantify $B$ at each position using the formula and compare with a Hall probe measurement.
