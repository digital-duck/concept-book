🌸 Galvanometer

🌸 Galvanometer

Definition. A galvanometer is a sensitive electromagnetic instrument used to detect and measure small electric currents, typically in the microampere to milliampere range. Its operation is based on the torque exerted on a current-carrying coil placed within a magnetic field. In the classic D'Arsonval design, a rectangular coil of $N$ turns, each of area $A$, is suspended between the poles of a permanent magnet producing a radial field of magnitude $B$. When a current $I$ flows through the coil, it experiences a magnetic torque

$$ \tau_{\text{mag}} = NIAB $$

which is balanced at equilibrium by the restoring torque of a calibrated spiral spring, $\tau_{\text{spring}} = \kappa\phi$, where $\kappa$ is the torsion constant and $\phi$ is the angular deflection. At equilibrium, $NIAB = \kappa\phi$, yielding the linear response

$$ \phi = \frac{NIAB}{\kappa}. $$

Because $\phi$ is proportional to $I$, the deflection of a pointer attached to the coil serves as a direct measure of the current.

Worked Example. A galvanometer has $N = 50$ turns, coil area $A = 1.2 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}^2$, field strength $B = 0.15\ \text{T}$, and torsion constant $\kappa = 4.5 \times 10^{-5}\ \text{N·m/rad}$. Find the full-scale deflection angle when the full-scale current is $I = 100\ \mu\text{A} = 1.0 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{A}$.

$$ \phi = \frac{(50)(1.0\times10^{-4})(0.15)(1.2\times10^{-3})}{4.5\times10^{-5}} = \frac{9.0\times10^{-7}}{4.5\times10^{-5}} = 0.020\ \text{rad} \approx 1.15°. $$

This small angle is magnified mechanically by the pointer length to give a readable scale deflection.

Key Theorem. A galvanometer can be converted into an ammeter by placing a low-resistance shunt $R_s$ in parallel with the coil resistance $R_g$. If the galvanometer deflects full-scale for current $I_g$, the shunt required to extend the range to total current $I$ satisfies

$$ R_s = \frac{I_g\, R_g}{I - I_g}. $$

Conversely, connecting a large series resistance $R_{\text{mult}}$ converts it into a voltmeter, with $R_{\text{mult}} = V_{\text{fs}}/I_g - R_g$.

Lab Exercise. Using a galvanometer with $R_g = 50\ \Omega$ and $I_g = 200\ \mu\text{A}$, calculate the shunt resistance needed to measure currents up to $2.0\ \text{mA}$. Then determine the series resistance required to use the same galvanometer as a voltmeter with a $5.0\ \text{V}$ full-scale range. Verify both results by constructing the circuits on a breadboard and comparing measured deflections against a calibrated multimeter.
