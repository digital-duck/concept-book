🌸 Rolling Without Slipping

🌸 Rolling Without Slipping

Definition. A rigid body rolls without slipping on a surface when every point of contact between the body and the surface has zero instantaneous velocity relative to that surface. For a uniform cylinder or sphere of radius $R$ rolling along a flat surface, this constraint links the translational velocity of the center of mass $v_{\text{cm}}$ to the angular velocity $\omega$ about the center of mass by the relation

$$ v_{\text{cm}} = R\omega. $$

Differentiating with respect to time yields the analogous acceleration constraint $a_{\text{cm}} = R\alpha$, where $\alpha$ is the angular acceleration. The static friction force at the contact point enforces this constraint but does no work, since the contact point is instantaneously at rest.

Worked Example. A solid sphere of mass $m = 2.0\ \text{kg}$ and radius $R = 0.10\ \text{m}$ starts from rest and rolls without slipping down an incline of angle $\theta = 30°$ and length $L = 2.0\ \text{m}$. Find the speed of the center of mass at the bottom.

Apply conservation of energy. The moment of inertia of a solid sphere is $I = \frac{2}{5}mR^2$. Setting potential energy equal to the sum of translational and rotational kinetic energy:

$$ mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv_{\text{cm}}^2 + \frac{1}{2}I\omega^2 = \frac{1}{2}mv_{\text{cm}}^2 + \frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{2}{5}mR^2\cdot\frac{v_{\text{cm}}^2}{R^2} = \frac{7}{10}mv_{\text{cm}}^2. $$

With $h = L\sin\theta = (2.0)(0.5) = 1.0\ \text{m}$:

$$ v_{\text{cm}} = \sqrt{\frac{10gh}{7}} = \sqrt{\frac{10(9.8)(1.0)}{7}} \approx 3.74\ \text{m/s}. $$

Key Theorem. For any rotationally symmetric object of moment of inertia $I = \beta mR^2$ rolling without slipping down an incline of height $h$, the no-slip constraint reduces the speed at the bottom relative to a sliding object. The center-of-mass speed satisfies

$$ v_{\text{cm}} = \sqrt{\frac{2gh}{1+\beta}}. $$

A larger $\beta$ means a greater fraction of energy goes into rotation, so the object translates more slowly. For a solid sphere $\beta = 2/5$; a solid cylinder $\beta = 1/2$ arrives at the bottom more slowly, and a thin-walled hollow cylinder $\beta = 1$ more slowly still.

Lab Exercise. Release a solid cylinder and a hollow cylinder of equal mass and radius simultaneously from the top of a ramp. Measure their arrival times at the bottom and compute the ratio $t_{\text{hollow}}/t_{\text{solid}}$. Use the no-slip energy relation to predict this ratio theoretically, then assess the percent discrepancy and identify sources of error such as air resistance or surface deformation.
