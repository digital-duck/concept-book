🌱 Permittivity Of Free Space

🌱 Permittivity Of Free Space

Definition. The permittivity of free space, denoted $\varepsilon_0$, is a fundamental physical constant that quantifies the ability of a vacuum to permit (support) electric field lines. It appears in Coulomb's law and throughout electromagnetic theory as the proportionality constant relating electric fields to their source charges in the absence of any material medium. Its accepted value is

$$ \varepsilon_0 = 8.854 \times 10^{-12} \, \mathrm{C^2 \, N^{-1} \, m^{-2}} $$

or equivalently $8.854 \times 10^{-12} \, \mathrm{F \, m^{-1}}$ (farads per meter). It enters Coulomb's law as

$$ F = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} $$

where the factor $\frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \approx 8.988 \times 10^9 \, \mathrm{N \, m^2 \, C^{-2}}$ is often written as $k_e$, Coulomb's constant. The physical meaning of $\varepsilon_0$ is subtle: a larger permittivity would weaken the electric force between charges, since the vacuum itself would more readily "accommodate" the field.

Worked Example. Two point charges $q_1 = +3.0 \, \mu\mathrm{C}$ and $q_2 = -5.0 \, \mu\mathrm{C}$ are separated by $r = 0.20 \, \mathrm{m}$ in vacuum. Find the magnitude of the electrostatic force.

$$ F = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \frac{|q_1||q_2|}{r^2} = (8.988 \times 10^9) \frac{(3.0 \times 10^{-6})(5.0 \times 10^{-6})}{(0.20)^2} $$ $$ F = (8.988 \times 10^9) \frac{1.5 \times 10^{-11}}{0.04} = (8.988 \times 10^9)(3.75 \times 10^{-10}) \approx 3.4 \, \mathrm{N} $$

The force is attractive because the charges have opposite signs.

Key Theorem. Gauss's law, the cornerstone of electrostatics, is expressed directly in terms of $\varepsilon_0$:

$$ \oint_S \mathbf{E} \cdot d\mathbf{A} = \frac{Q_\mathrm{enc}}{\varepsilon_0} $$

This states that the total electric flux through any closed surface equals the net enclosed charge divided by $\varepsilon_0$. The constant thus sets the absolute scale of electric fields produced by a given charge distribution in vacuum.

Lab Exercise. Using a parallel-plate capacitor with plate area $A = 0.050 \, \mathrm{m^2}$ and separation $d = 1.0 \, \mathrm{mm}$, measure the capacitance $C$ with an LCR meter. Verify that $C = \varepsilon_0 A / d$ and extract an experimental value of $\varepsilon_0$. Compare your result to the accepted value and compute the percent error, discussing sources of systematic uncertainty such as fringe fields at plate edges.
