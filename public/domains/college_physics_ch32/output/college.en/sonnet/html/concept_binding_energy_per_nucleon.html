🌱 Binding Energy Per Nucleon

Definition. The binding energy per nucleon, denoted $\varepsilon$, is defined as the total nuclear binding energy of a nuclide divided by its mass number $A$:

$$ \varepsilon = \frac{BE}{A} $$

where the total binding energy is

$$ BE = \left(Z m_p + N m_n - m_{\text{nucleus}}\right)c^2 $$

with $Z$ protons of mass $m_p$, $N$ neutrons of mass $m_n$, and nuclear mass $m_{\text{nucleus}}$. In practice, atomic masses $m_{\text{atom}}$ are tabulated, so one uses

$$ BE = \left(Z m_H + N m_n - m_{\text{atom}}\right)c^2 $$

where $m_H$ is the atomic mass of hydrogen (electron masses cancel). The quantity $\varepsilon$ measures how tightly, on average, each nucleon is bound within the nucleus; a larger $\varepsilon$ indicates a more stable nuclide.

Worked Example. Consider $^{56}\text{Fe}$, which has $Z = 26$, $N = 30$, and atomic mass $m = 55.934939\ \text{u}$.

$$ BE = \bigl(26(1.007825\ \text{u}) + 30(1.008665\ \text{u}) - 55.934939\ \text{u}\bigr) \times 931.5\ \text{MeV/u} $$

Computing the mass defect:

$$ \Delta m = 26.20345 + 30.25995 - 55.934939 = 0.528461\ \text{u} $$ $$ BE = 0.528461 \times 931.5\ \text{MeV} \approx 492.3\ \text{MeV} $$ $$ \varepsilon = \frac{492.3\ \text{MeV}}{56} \approx 8.79\ \text{MeV/nucleon} $$

This places iron near the maximum of the binding energy per nucleon curve, explaining its role as the endpoint of stellar nucleosynthesis fusion chains.

Key Theorem — Peak Stability. The binding energy per nucleon curve exhibits a broad maximum near $A \approx 56$–62 (the iron-nickel peak), with $\varepsilon \approx 8.7$–$8.8\ \text{MeV/nucleon}$. Nuclides lighter than this peak release energy via fusion; nuclides heavier than the peak release energy via fission. Both processes drive the system toward the energy minimum at the peak.

Lab Exercise. Using tabulated atomic masses, calculate $\varepsilon$ for $^{4}\text{He}$, $^{12}\text{C}$, $^{238}\text{U}$, and $^{235}\text{U}$. Plot these four values alongside $^{56}\text{Fe}$ on a sketch of the $\varepsilon$ vs. $A$ curve, and identify which nuclides would be expected to undergo fission and which fusion to increase stability.
