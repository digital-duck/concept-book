🍃 Chain Reaction

🍃 Chain Reaction

Definition. A chain reaction is a self-sustaining sequence of nuclear fission events in which each fission produces, on average, at least one neutron that induces a subsequent fission. The critical parameter governing this behavior is the multiplication factor $k$, defined as

$$ k = \frac{\text{number of neutrons in one generation}}{\text{number of neutrons in preceding generation}}. $$

When $k < 1$ the reaction is subcritical and dies out; when $k = 1$ it is critical and self-sustaining at constant power; when $k > 1$ it is supercritical and the neutron population grows exponentially. In uranium-235, a single thermal-neutron-induced fission releases an average of $\bar{\nu} \approx 2.43$ neutrons and approximately $200\,\text{MeV}$ of energy per event.

Worked Example. Suppose a fissile assembly has a reproduction factor $\eta = 2.43$ (neutrons produced per neutron absorbed in fuel), a thermal utilization $f = 0.90$, a resonance escape probability $p = 0.85$, and a fast fission factor $\varepsilon = 1.03$. The four-factor formula for an infinite medium gives

$$ k_\infty = \eta \, f \, p \, \varepsilon = (2.43)(0.90)(0.85)(1.03) \approx 1.91. $$

A finite reactor additionally multiplies by leakage terms $P_{NL}$ (non-leakage probability), yielding $k_{\text{eff}} = k_\infty P_{NL}$. If $P_{NL} = 0.524$, then $k_{\text{eff}} \approx 1.00$, placing the reactor at criticality.

Key Theorem (Criticality Condition). For a bare spherical reactor of radius $R$ and material buckling $B^2 = k_\infty/M^2 - 1/M^2$, criticality requires

$$ B^2 R^2 = \pi^2, $$

where $M^2$ is the migration area. This geometric buckling condition defines the critical radius, below which leakage prevents a sustained chain reaction.

Lab Exercise. Using a reactor simulation tool (e.g., OpenMC or a provided spreadsheet), vary the enrichment of $^{235}\text{U}$ from $3\%$ to $20\%$ while holding geometry fixed. Record $k_{\text{eff}}$ at each enrichment level, identify the threshold enrichment at which criticality is first achieved, and plot $k_{\text{eff}}$ versus enrichment. Explain physically why increasing $^{235}\text{U}$ concentration raises $\eta$ and thus $k_\infty$.
