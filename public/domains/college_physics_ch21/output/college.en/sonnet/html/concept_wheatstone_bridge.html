🌸 Wheatstone Bridge

🌸 Wheatstone Bridge

Definition. A Wheatstone bridge is a circuit configuration used to measure an unknown electrical resistance with high precision. It consists of four resistors arranged in a diamond (rhombus) topology: three known resistors $R_1$, $R_2$, $R_3$, and one unknown resistor $R_x$. A voltage source $V$ is connected across one diagonal of the diamond, and a galvanometer $G$ is connected across the other diagonal. The bridge is said to be balanced when no current flows through the galvanometer, a condition achieved by adjusting the known resistances until the potential difference across the galvanometer is zero. At balance, the unknown resistance satisfies:

$$ R_x = \frac{R_2 \, R_3}{R_1} $$

This relation follows directly from Kirchhoff's voltage law applied to each loop of the balanced circuit.

Worked Example. Suppose $R_1 = 100\,\Omega$, $R_2 = 200\,\Omega$, and $R_3$ is adjusted to $150\,\Omega$ to achieve balance (zero galvanometer deflection). The unknown resistance is:

$$ R_x = \frac{R_2 \, R_3}{R_1} = \frac{(200)(150)}{100} = 300\,\Omega $$

To verify balance, note that the voltage divider ratios across each arm must be equal. Left arm: $\frac{R_1}{R_1 + R_x} = \frac{100}{400} = 0.25$. Right arm: $\frac{R_2}{R_2 + R_3} = \frac{200}{350} \approx 0.571$. Recheck: with $R_x = 300\,\Omega$, left arm $= \frac{100}{400} = 0.25$ and right arm $= \frac{200}{200+150} = \frac{200}{350}$. Correcting the setup: for balance, $\frac{R_1}{R_x} = \frac{R_2}{R_3}$, giving $R_x = R_1 \cdot \frac{R_3}{R_2} = 100 \cdot \frac{150}{200} = 75\,\Omega$.

Key Theorem. At balance, applying Kirchhoff's voltage law to both loops of the bridge yields two equations whose ratio eliminates the supply voltage $V$ entirely:

$$ \frac{R_1}{R_x} = \frac{R_2}{R_3} \implies R_x = \frac{R_1 \, R_3}{R_2} $$

This independence from $V$ makes the Wheatstone bridge intrinsically immune to supply fluctuations, which is why it remains a standard for precision resistance measurement.
