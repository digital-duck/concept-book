🌱 Capacitance

🌱 Capacitance

Definition. A capacitor is a device that stores electric charge and energy in an electric field between two conducting surfaces. The capacitance $C$ of a capacitor is defined as the ratio of the magnitude of charge $Q$ stored on either conductor to the magnitude of the potential difference $V$ between them:

$$ C = \frac{Q}{V} $$

The SI unit of capacitance is the farad (F), where $1\ \text{F} = 1\ \text{C/V}$. Capacitance is a geometric property—it depends on the shape, size, and separation of the conductors, as well as the permittivity of the medium between them, not on $Q$ or $V$ individually.

For a parallel-plate capacitor with plate area $A$, plate separation $d$, and vacuum (or air) between the plates, the electric field is uniform and given by $E = \sigma/\varepsilon_0$, where $\sigma = Q/A$ is the surface charge density. The potential difference is $V = Ed = Qd/(\varepsilon_0 A)$, which yields the standard result:

$$ C = \varepsilon_0 \frac{A}{d} $$

where $\varepsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12}\ \text{F/m}$ is the permittivity of free space.

Worked Example. A parallel-plate capacitor has square plates of side $0.10\ \text{m}$ separated by $d = 1.0\ \text{mm} = 1.0 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}$. Find the capacitance and the charge stored when connected to a $12\ \text{V}$ battery.

Solution. The plate area is $A = (0.10)^2 = 0.010\ \text{m}^2$. Then:

$$ C = \varepsilon_0 \frac{A}{d} = (8.85 \times 10^{-12})\frac{0.010}{1.0 \times 10^{-3}} = 88.5\ \text{pF} $$

The stored charge is:

$$ Q = CV = (88.5 \times 10^{-12})(12) = 1.06\ \text{nC} $$

Key Theorem. The energy $U$ stored in a capacitor is:

$$ U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 = \frac{Q^2}{2C} = \frac{QV}{2} $$

This result follows directly from integrating the work done to transfer incremental charge $dq$ against the growing potential $q/C$: $U = \int_0^Q (q/C)\,dq = Q^2/(2C)$. The three equivalent forms are useful depending on which pair of quantities—$C$, $Q$, $V$—is known.

Lab Exercise. Using a capacitance meter, measure the capacitance of two parallel metal plates as you vary the separation $d$ from $2\ \text{mm}$ to $10\ \text{mm}$. Plot $C$ versus $1/d$ and verify the linear relationship predicted by $C = \varepsilon_0 A/d$. Determine $\varepsilon_0$ from the slope and compare with the accepted value.
