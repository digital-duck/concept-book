🌸 Fiber Optics

🌸 Fiber Optics

Definition. Fiber optics exploits the principle of total internal reflection to transmit light signals along thin, flexible strands of glass or plastic with minimal loss. A fiber consists of a core of refractive index $n_1$ surrounded by a cladding of lower refractive index $n_2 < n_1$. When light strikes the core–cladding interface at an angle of incidence $\theta_i$ greater than the critical angle $\theta_c$, it undergoes total internal reflection and propagates along the fiber without escaping. The critical angle is given by

$$ \theta_c = \arcsin\!\left(\frac{n_2}{n_1}\right). $$

An important figure of merit is the numerical aperture (NA), which quantifies the acceptance cone of the fiber:

$$ \mathrm{NA} = n_0 \sin\theta_{\max} = \sqrt{n_1^2 - n_2^2}, $$

where $n_0 = 1$ for air and $\theta_{\max}$ is the maximum angle of incidence (measured from the fiber axis at the entrance face) for which light will be totally internally reflected within the core.

Worked Example. A step-index fiber has a core index $n_1 = 1.50$ and cladding index $n_2 = 1.46$. Find the critical angle and the numerical aperture.

Critical angle:

$$ \theta_c = \arcsin\!\left(\frac{1.46}{1.50}\right) = \arcsin(0.9733) \approx 76.7°. $$

Numerical aperture:

$$ \mathrm{NA} = \sqrt{(1.50)^2 - (1.46)^2} = \sqrt{2.25 - 2.1316} = \sqrt{0.1184} \approx 0.344. $$

The acceptance half-angle is $\theta_{\max} = \arcsin(0.344) \approx 20.1°$, meaning only light entering within a cone of half-angle $20.1°$ about the fiber axis will be guided.

Key Theorem (Total Internal Reflection Condition). For a ray traveling in a medium of index $n_1$ and incident on a boundary with a medium of index $n_2 < n_1$, total internal reflection occurs for all $\theta_i \geq \theta_c$. No refracted ray exists; all energy is reflected back into the denser medium.

Lab Exercise. Using a ray-optics simulation, set $n_1 = 1.55$ and vary $n_2$ from $1.30$ to $1.54$ in steps of $0.05$. For each value, compute $\theta_c$ and $\mathrm{NA}$, then record the maximum number of reflections a ray undergoes in a $10\,\mathrm{cm}$ fiber. Discuss how decreasing the index contrast affects light confinement and signal bandwidth.
