🍃 Lens Power

🍃 Lens Power

Definition. The power of a lens is a measure of its ability to converge or diverge light, defined as the reciprocal of the focal length:

$$ P = \frac{1}{f} $$

where $f$ is the focal length measured in meters. The SI unit of lens power is the diopter (D), where $1\,\text{D} = 1\,\text{m}^{-1}$. A converging (convex) lens has positive power; a diverging (concave) lens has negative power. When two thin lenses are placed in contact, their powers add algebraically:

$$ P_{\text{total}} = P_1 + P_2 $$

This additive property makes the diopter a practical unit in optics and clinical optometry, where prescriptions are written directly in diopters rather than in focal lengths.

Worked Example. A converging lens has focal length $f_1 = 20\,\text{cm}$ and a diverging lens has focal length $f_2 = -50\,\text{cm}$. Find the power of each lens and the combined power when they are placed in contact.

Converting to meters: $f_1 = 0.20\,\text{m}$ and $f_2 = -0.50\,\text{m}$.

$$ P_1 = \frac{1}{0.20} = +5.0\,\text{D}, \qquad P_2 = \frac{1}{-0.50} = -2.0\,\text{D} $$ $$ P_{\text{total}} = P_1 + P_2 = 5.0 + (-2.0) = +3.0\,\text{D} $$

The equivalent focal length of the combination is $f_{\text{eq}} = 1/3.0 \approx 0.333\,\text{m} = 33.3\,\text{cm}$, and the system acts as a net converging lens.

Key Theorem. For a thin lens in air, the lensmaker's equation relates power directly to the lens geometry:

$$ P = \frac{1}{f} = (n - 1)\left(\frac{1}{R_1} - \frac{1}{R_2}\right) $$

where $n$ is the index of refraction of the lens material and $R_1$, $R_2$ are the radii of curvature of the two surfaces (with sign determined by convention). This shows that power depends on both material choice and surface curvature, providing the theoretical basis for lens design.

Lab Exercise. Place a converging lens on an optical bench and project a sharp image of a distant window onto a screen. Measure the image distance, which approximates $f$ for a distant object. Compute $P = 1/f$ and compare with the value obtained by applying the thin-lens equation $\frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{d_i} = P$ for a nearby object at a measured distance $d_o$.
