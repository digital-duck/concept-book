🌸 Camera Lens

🌸 Camera Lens

Definition. A camera lens is an optical system comprising one or more refracting surfaces designed to form a real, inverted image of an object on a photographic sensor or film plane. The fundamental governing relationship is the thin-lens equation,

$$ \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{d_i} = \frac{1}{f}, $$

where $d_o$ is the object distance (measured from the lens to the object), $d_i$ is the image distance (measured from the lens to the image plane), and $f$ is the focal length of the lens. For a converging lens, $f > 0$. The lateral magnification produced by the lens is

$$ m = -\frac{d_i}{d_o}, $$

with $|m| < 1$ indicating a reduced image, and the negative sign confirming inversion. The f-number (or f-stop) is defined as $N = f/D$, where $D$ is the effective aperture diameter; it controls both depth of field and light-gathering capacity.

Worked Example. A camera lens has focal length $f = 50\text{ mm}$. A subject stands $d_o = 2.00\text{ m} = 2000\text{ mm}$ from the lens. Find the image distance and magnification.

Applying the thin-lens equation:

$$ \frac{1}{d_i} = \frac{1}{f} - \frac{1}{d_o} = \frac{1}{50} - \frac{1}{2000} = \frac{40 - 1}{2000} = \frac{39}{2000}. $$

Thus $d_i = 2000/39 \approx 51.3\text{ mm}$. The sensor must be placed approximately $1.3\text{ mm}$ beyond the nominal focal plane to achieve sharp focus. The magnification is

$$ m = -\frac{51.3}{2000} \approx -0.0256, $$

confirming a strongly reduced, inverted image.

Key Theorem (Lensmaker's Equation). For a lens of refractive index $n$ with radii of curvature $R_1$ and $R_2$,

$$ \frac{1}{f} = (n-1)\!\left(\frac{1}{R_1} - \frac{1}{R_2}\right). $$

This result, derived from successive application of Snell's law at each surface in the paraxial approximation, shows that focal length is determined entirely by lens geometry and material, independent of object position.

Lab Exercise. Mount a converging lens ($f \approx 100\text{ mm}$) on an optical bench. Place a luminous object at $d_o = 300\text{ mm}$ and locate the sharpest image on a screen. Measure $d_i$ and compare with the theoretical prediction. Repeat for $d_o = 150\text{ mm}$ and $d_o = 500\text{ mm}$, then plot $1/d_i$ versus $1/d_o$ to verify that the slope equals $-1$ and the intercept equals $1/f$.
