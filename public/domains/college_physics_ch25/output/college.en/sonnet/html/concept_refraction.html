🍃 Refraction

🍃 Refraction

Definition. Refraction is the change in direction of a wave as it passes from one medium into another in which it travels at a different speed. For light, the phenomenon arises because the phase speed $v = c/n$ depends on the refractive index $n$ of the medium. When a ray crosses a planar interface between two homogeneous, isotropic media with indices $n_1$ and $n_2$, the relationship between the angles of incidence $\theta_1$ and refraction $\theta_2$ (both measured from the normal to the surface) is given by Snell's Law:

$$ n_1 \sin\theta_1 = n_2 \sin\theta_2. $$

This equality follows from the requirement that the component of the wave vector parallel to the interface is conserved across the boundary—a direct consequence of phase matching.

Worked Example. A ray of light traveling through air ($n_1 = 1.00$) strikes a flat glass surface ($n_2 = 1.52$) at an angle of incidence $\theta_1 = 45.0°$. Find the angle of refraction.

Applying Snell's Law:

$$ \sin\theta_2 = \frac{n_1}{n_2}\sin\theta_1 = \frac{1.00}{1.52}\sin 45.0° = \frac{0.7071}{1.52} \approx 0.4652. $$

Therefore:

$$ \theta_2 = \arcsin(0.4652) \approx 27.7°. $$

The refracted ray bends toward the normal because the light slows down upon entering the denser medium ($n_2 > n_1$).

Key Theorem — Total Internal Reflection. When light travels from a denser medium ($n_1 > n_2$), Snell's Law requires $\sin\theta_2 = (n_1/n_2)\sin\theta_1 > \sin\theta_1$. For angles of incidence exceeding the critical angle,

$$ \theta_c = \arcsin\!\left(\frac{n_2}{n_1}\right), $$

no real solution for $\theta_2$ exists and all incident light is reflected back into the first medium. This principle underlies fiber-optic waveguides and retroreflective prisms.

Lab Exercise. Fill a rectangular glass tank with water ($n \approx 1.33$) and direct a laser beam through one side at several measured angles of incidence. Record the corresponding refraction angles and plot $\sin\theta_2$ versus $\sin\theta_1$. Determine the slope of the best-fit line and compare it to the theoretical ratio $n_1/n_2$. Then tilt the laser from inside the water outward and identify the critical angle experimentally; compare your result with the value predicted by $\theta_c = \arcsin(1/1.33)$.
