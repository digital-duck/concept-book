🌸 Entrainment

🌸 Entrainment

Definition. Entrainment is the phenomenon in which a driven oscillating system is forced to synchronize its frequency with that of an external periodic driving force, even when the driving frequency differs from the system's natural resonant frequency. When a damped oscillator is subjected to a periodic external force of angular frequency $\omega_d$, the steady-state response oscillates at $\omega_d$ rather than at the system's natural frequency $\omega_0 = \sqrt{k/m}$. This frequency capture occurs because damping continuously dissipates energy stored in free oscillations, leaving only the driven response at $\omega_d$ to persist in steady state.

The amplitude of the entrained oscillation is given by:

$$ A = \frac{F_0/m}{\sqrt{(\omega_0^2 - \omega_d^2)^2 + (b\omega_d/m)^2}} $$

where $F_0$ is the magnitude of the driving force, $b$ is the damping coefficient, and $m$ is the mass. As $\omega_d \to \omega_0$, the denominator reaches its minimum, and $A$ reaches its maximum — the condition of resonance. Entrainment persists for all driving frequencies, but maximum energy transfer occurs at resonance.

Worked Example. A mass-spring system has $m = 0.50\,\text{kg}$, $k = 50\,\text{N/m}$, and $b = 1.0\,\text{kg/s}$. It is driven by $F(t) = 4.0\cos(8.0t)\,\text{N}$, so $\omega_d = 8.0\,\text{rad/s}$. The natural frequency is $\omega_0 = \sqrt{50/0.50} = 10.0\,\text{rad/s}$. Substituting:

$$ A = \frac{4.0/0.50}{\sqrt{(100 - 64)^2 + (1.0 \cdot 8.0/0.50)^2}} = \frac{8.0}{\sqrt{1296 + 256}} = \frac{8.0}{\sqrt{1552}} \approx 0.203\,\text{m} $$

The system oscillates at exactly $8.0\,\text{rad/s}$, entrained to the driver, not at its natural $10.0\,\text{rad/s}$.

Key Theorem. In any linearly damped driven oscillator, the transient (homogeneous) solution decays exponentially as $e^{-bt/2m}$, leaving only the particular solution oscillating at $\omega_d$. Entrainment is therefore mathematically guaranteed for all $t \operatorname{gg} 2m/b$.

Lab Exercise. Mount a spring-mass system on a motorized shaker with adjustable frequency. Starting well below $\omega_0$, slowly increase $\omega_d$ while recording steady-state amplitude and phase lag. Plot $A$ versus $\omega_d$ and identify the resonance peak. Measure the phase difference between driving force and displacement; confirm it approaches $\pi/2$ exactly at resonance and $\pi$ for $\omega_d \operatorname{gg} \omega_0$.
