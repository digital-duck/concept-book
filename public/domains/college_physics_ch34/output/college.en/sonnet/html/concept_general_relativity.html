🍃 General Relativity

Definition. General relativity (GR), formulated by Albert Einstein in 1915, is a geometric theory of gravitation that supersedes Newtonian gravity. Its central premise is the equivalence principle: the effects of gravitational acceleration are locally indistinguishable from those of inertial acceleration. GR encodes gravity not as a force but as the curvature of four-dimensional spacetime caused by the presence of mass and energy. The governing equations are the Einstein field equations:

$$ G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4} T_{\mu\nu} $$

where $G_{\mu\nu} = R_{\mu\nu} - \frac{1}{2}Rg_{\mu\nu}$ is the Einstein tensor (encoding spacetime curvature via the Ricci tensor $R_{\mu\nu}$ and Ricci scalar $R$), $\Lambda$ is the cosmological constant, $g_{\mu\nu}$ is the metric tensor, $T_{\mu\nu}$ is the stress-energy tensor representing matter and energy content, and $G$ is Newton's gravitational constant.

Worked Example: Gravitational Time Dilation. A clock on Earth's surface (gravitational potential $\phi_1 = -GM_\oplus/R_\oplus$) and a GPS satellite at altitude $h$ (potential $\phi_2 = -GM_\oplus/(R_\oplus + h)$) run at different rates. The Schwarzschild metric predicts the fractional time difference:

$$ \frac{\Delta t_{\text{surface}}}{\Delta t_{\text{satellite}}} \approx 1 - \frac{\Delta\phi}{c^2} $$

For $h = 20{,}200\ \text{km}$, $\Delta\phi/c^2 \approx 5.3 \times 10^{-10}$, giving a rate discrepancy of roughly $45\ \mu\text{s/day}$. Without GR corrections, GPS positional errors would accumulate at approximately $11\ \text{km/day}$.

Key Theorem: Geodesic Equation. In curved spacetime, freely falling particles (no non-gravitational forces) follow geodesics — the generalization of straight lines. The geodesic equation is:

$$ \frac{d^2 x^\mu}{d\tau^2} + \Gamma^\mu_{\alpha\beta}\frac{dx^\alpha}{d\tau}\frac{dx^\beta}{d\tau} = 0 $$

where $\Gamma^\mu_{\alpha\beta}$ are the Christoffel symbols derived from the metric, and $\tau$ is proper time. In the weak-field, slow-motion limit, this reduces exactly to Newton's second law under gravitational acceleration.

Lab Exercise. Using the Schwarzschild radius formula $r_s = 2GM/c^2$, calculate the Schwarzschild radius of (a) Earth ($M_\oplus = 5.97 \times 10^{24}\ \text{kg}$) and (b) the Sun ($M_\odot = 1.99 \times 10^{30}\ \text{kg}$). Compare each result to the object's actual radius and discuss what it would mean physically if a body were compressed to within $r_s$.
