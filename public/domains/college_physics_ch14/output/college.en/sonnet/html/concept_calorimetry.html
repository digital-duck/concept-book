🌸 Calorimetry

Definition. Calorimetry is the experimental science of measuring heat transfer between a system and its surroundings. The foundational principle is conservation of energy: in a thermally isolated system, the total heat gained by cooler objects equals the total heat lost by warmer objects. For a substance of mass $m$, specific heat capacity $c$, undergoing a temperature change $\Delta T$, the heat exchanged is

$$ Q = mc\Delta T. $$

When a phase transition occurs at constant temperature, the relevant quantity is instead the latent heat $L$, so that $Q = mL$. A calorimeter enforces thermal isolation so that the calorimeter contents form a closed system, giving the governing equation

$$ \sum_i Q_i = 0, $$

where each $Q_i$ carries a sign: positive for heat absorbed, negative for heat released.

Worked Example. A 0.250 kg aluminum block ($c_\text{Al} = 900\ \text{J}\cdot\text{kg}^{-1}\cdot\text{K}^{-1}$) is heated to $150.0\ ^\circ\text{C}$ and dropped into a calorimeter containing 0.500 kg of water ($c_w = 4186\ \text{J}\cdot\text{kg}^{-1}\cdot\text{K}^{-1}$) initially at $20.0\ ^\circ\text{C}$. Assuming the calorimeter cup has negligible heat capacity, find the equilibrium temperature $T_f$.

Setting $Q_\text{Al} + Q_w = 0$:

$$ m_\text{Al}\,c_\text{Al}(T_f - 150.0) + m_w\,c_w(T_f - 20.0) = 0. $$

Substituting values:

$$ (0.250)(900)(T_f - 150.0) + (0.500)(4186)(T_f - 20.0) = 0 $$ $$ 225\,T_f - 33750 + 2093\,T_f - 41860 = 0 $$ $$ 2318\,T_f = 75610 $$ $$ T_f \approx 32.6\ ^\circ\text{C}. $$

The large heat capacity of water dominates, pulling the final temperature close to the initial water temperature.

Key Theorem. The method of mixtures is valid only when (1) no heat escapes to the environment, (2) no phase change occurs unexpectedly, and (3) the system reaches a single uniform equilibrium temperature. Violation of any condition introduces systematic error; in practice, a correction term $Q_\text{cal} = C_\text{cal}\,\Delta T$ accounts for the finite heat capacity $C_\text{cal}$ of the calorimeter itself.

Lab Exercise. Determine the specific heat of an unknown metal sample ($\approx 0.100\ \text{kg}$). Heat the sample to $100.0\ ^\circ\text{C}$ in boiling water, transfer it rapidly to a calorimeter containing 0.200 kg of water at a measured initial temperature, and record the equilibrium temperature. Use $\sum Q_i = 0$, including a calorimeter correction, to solve for $c_\text{metal}$. Repeat three times and report the mean with standard deviation.
