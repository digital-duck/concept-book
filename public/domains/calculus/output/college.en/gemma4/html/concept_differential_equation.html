🍃 Differential Equation

A differential equation (DE) is a fundamental mathematical statement that relates an unknown function to one or more of its derivatives. Unlike algebraic equations, which equate variables directly (e.g., $x^2 + 2 = 6$), DEs are used when the relationship between variables is governed by rates of change; they describe how a quantity evolves over time or space. The solution to a DE is not a single number but rather a function—the specific curve that satisfies the equation. DEs can be classified based on whether the highest derivative present is with respect to a single independent variable (forming an Ordinary Differential Equation, ODE) or multiple variables (forming a Partial Differential Equation, PDE). For foundational study, we primarily focus on ODEs of the form:

$$ F(x, y, \frac{dy}{dx}, \dots, \frac{d^n y}{dx^n}) = 0 $$

Solving such an equation involves finding the family of functions $y(x)$ that make the statement true.

Worked Example: Consider the simple first-order linear DE: $\frac{dy}{dx} = 2x$. This equation states that the rate of change of $y$ with respect to $x$ is always twice the value of $x$. To find the function $y(x)$, we integrate both sides with respect to $x$:

$$ y(x) = \int 2x \, dx = x^2 + C $$

Here, $C$ is the constant of integration, representing a family of solutions (the parabola $y=x^2+C$). If an initial condition were provided, say $y(0)=5$, we would solve for $C$: $5 = 0^2 + C$, yielding $C=5$. The unique solution is then $y(x) = x^2 + 5$.

Key Theorem (Existence and Uniqueness): For a first-order ODE of the form $\frac{dy}{dx} = f(x, y)$, if both $f(x, y)$ and its partial derivative with respect to $y$, $\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}$, are continuous in some rectangular region $R$ containing the point $(x_0, y_0)$, then there exists a unique solution passing through that point. This theorem is critical because it guarantees that our initial value problem (IVP) has exactly one valid answer.

Lab Exercise: Solve the following Initial Value Problem (IVP):

$$ \frac{dy}{dx} + 3xy = x^2 $$

Given the initial condition $y(0)=1$. Identify which standard method (e.g., separation of variables, integrating factor) is required and apply it to find the specific function $y(x)$ that satisfies both the differential equation and the given boundary condition.
