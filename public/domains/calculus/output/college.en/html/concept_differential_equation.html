Differential Equation

Definition. A differential equation (DE) is an equation that relates an unknown function to one or more of its derivatives. If the unknown depends on a single independent variable, the equation is called an ordinary differential equation (ODE); if it involves partial derivatives with respect to multiple variables, it is a partial differential equation (PDE). The order of a DE is the highest derivative that appears. A first-order ODE has the general form

$$F\!\left(x,\, y,\, \frac{dy}{dx}\right) = 0,$$

while a second-order ODE includes $\dfrac{d^2y}{dx^2}$. A DE is linear if $y$ and all its derivatives appear to the first power with no products among them; otherwise it is nonlinear.

Worked Example. Consider the first-order linear ODE

$$\frac{dy}{dx} - 2y = 0, \quad y(0) = 3.$$

Separating variables gives $\dfrac{dy}{y} = 2\,dx$. Integrating both sides yields $\ln|y| = 2x + C$, so $y = Ae^{2x}$ for an arbitrary constant $A$. Applying the initial condition $y(0) = 3$ forces $A = 3$, giving the unique solution

$$y(x) = 3e^{2x}.$$

One can verify by substitution: $y' = 6e^{2x} = 2 \cdot 3e^{2x} = 2y$. $\checkmark$

Key Theorem (Existence and Uniqueness — Picard–Lindelöf). Let $f(x, y)$ be continuous on an open rectangle $R$ containing $(x_0, y_0)$, and suppose $\partial f/\partial y$ is also continuous on $R$. Then the initial-value problem

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = f(x, y), \quad y(x_0) = y_0$$

has a unique solution on some open interval containing $x_0$.

This theorem guarantees that under mild smoothness conditions, specifying the state of a system at one moment completely determines its future and past — a cornerstone of deterministic modeling in physics and engineering.

Lab Exercise. Solve the initial-value problem

$$\frac{dy}{dx} + \frac{y}{x} = x^2, \quad y(1) = 2, \quad x > 0$$

using an integrating factor $\mu(x) = e^{\int (1/x)\,dx}$. Find $y(x)$ explicitly, evaluate $y(2)$ to four decimal places, and plot the solution on $[1, 3]$. Identify the particular and homogeneous components of your solution.
