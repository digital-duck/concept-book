🍃 Vapor Pressure

🍃 Vapor Pressure

Definition. Vapor pressure is the equilibrium pressure exerted by the vapor phase of a substance above its liquid (or solid) surface in a closed system at a given temperature. At equilibrium, the rate at which molecules escape the liquid surface equals the rate at which vapor molecules return to it. The vapor pressure $P_v$ depends solely on temperature and the identity of the substance; it is independent of the amount of liquid or vapor present, provided both phases coexist.

The relationship between vapor pressure and temperature is governed by the Clausius–Clapeyron equation:

$$ \frac{dP_v}{dT} = \frac{L\, P_v}{R T^2} $$

where $L$ is the molar latent heat of vaporization and $R = 8.314\ \mathrm{J\,mol^{-1}\,K^{-1}}$ is the universal gas constant. Integrating between two temperatures $T_1$ and $T_2$ yields the integrated form:

$$ \ln\!\left(\frac{P_2}{P_1}\right) = -\frac{L}{R}\left(\frac{1}{T_2} - \frac{1}{T_1}\right). $$

Worked Example. The vapor pressure of water at $T_1 = 373\ \mathrm{K}$ (normal boiling point) is $P_1 = 1.013 \times 10^5\ \mathrm{Pa}$. Given $L = 4.07 \times 10^4\ \mathrm{J\,mol^{-1}}$, find the vapor pressure at $T_2 = 353\ \mathrm{K}$.

$$ \ln\!\left(\frac{P_2}{P_1}\right) = -\frac{4.07\times10^4}{8.314}\left(\frac{1}{353} - \frac{1}{373}\right) = -4895\times(2.834\times10^{-4} - 2.681\times10^{-4}) $$ $$ = -4895 \times 1.527\times10^{-4} \approx -0.747 $$ $$ P_2 = 1.013\times10^5 \times e^{-0.747} \approx 4.74\times10^4\ \mathrm{Pa}. $$

This result confirms that lowering temperature significantly reduces vapor pressure, consistent with everyday observation.

Key Theorem. A liquid boils when its vapor pressure equals the ambient pressure. At standard atmospheric pressure ($1.013\times10^5\ \mathrm{Pa}$), water boils at exactly $373\ \mathrm{K}$. At reduced pressure — for instance, at high altitude — boiling occurs below $373\ \mathrm{K}$, since the vapor pressure reaches the lower ambient value at a lower temperature.

Lab Exercise. Using a sealed syringe containing a small volume of ethanol and a pressure sensor, record the equilibrium pressure at five temperatures between $20\ \mathrm{°C}$ and $60\ \mathrm{°C}$. Plot $\ln P_v$ versus $1/T$ and determine the molar latent heat $L$ from the slope.
