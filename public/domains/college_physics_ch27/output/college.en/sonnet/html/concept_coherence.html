🍃 Coherence

🍃 Coherence

Definition. Coherence is the property of a wave source (or pair of sources) that maintains a fixed, well-defined phase relationship over time and space. Two sources are mutually coherent if the phase difference $\Delta\phi$ between their emissions remains constant throughout the observation period. Formally, the temporal coherence time $\tau_c$ characterizes how long a wave train maintains predictable phase; equivalently, the coherence length $\ell_c = c\,\tau_c$ is the spatial extent over which interference can be observed. A perfectly monochromatic source has infinite coherence length, while a broadband source has $\ell_c \approx \lambda^2/\Delta\lambda$, where $\Delta\lambda$ is the spectral bandwidth.

Spatial coherence, by contrast, describes the phase correlation between different points on a wavefront at the same instant. A point source produces a spatially coherent wavefront; an extended thermal source does not, because emission from spatially separated atoms is statistically independent.

Worked Example. Sodium light ($\lambda = 589\,\text{nm}$) has a spectral linewidth $\Delta\lambda \approx 0.6\,\text{nm}$. Estimate its coherence length.

$$ \ell_c = \frac{\lambda^2}{\Delta\lambda} = \frac{(589 \times 10^{-9})^2}{0.6 \times 10^{-9}} \approx 5.8 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{m} \approx 0.58\,\text{mm} $$

This means path-length differences exceeding $\sim 0.58\,\text{mm}$ in a Young's double-slit experiment with a sodium lamp will wash out interference fringes, because the overlapping wave trains no longer originate from the same phase epoch.

Key Theorem (Wiener–Khinchin). The degree of temporal coherence is quantified by the normalized autocorrelation of the electric field:

$$ \gamma(\tau) = \frac{\langle E^*(t)\,E(t+\tau)\rangle}{\langle |E(t)|^2 \rangle} $$

Fringe visibility $\mathcal{V} = |\gamma(\tau)|$, so observable contrast directly measures coherence. Perfect coherence gives $\mathcal{V} = 1$; incoherent superposition gives $\mathcal{V} = 0$.

Lab Exercise. Set up a Michelson interferometer with a white-light source. Slowly increase the path-length difference between the two arms and record fringe visibility at intervals of 0.1 mm. Plot $\mathcal{V}$ versus path difference, identify the path length at which fringes vanish, and compare your measured coherence length to the value predicted by $\ell_c = \lambda^2/\Delta\lambda$ using the source's specified spectral bandwidth.
