🌸 Spectroscopic Dispersion

🌸 Spectroscopic Dispersion

Definition. Spectroscopic dispersion is the angular or spatial separation of light into its constituent wavelengths produced by a dispersive optical element such as a prism or diffraction grating. For a diffraction grating, the condition for constructive interference at order $m$ is the grating equation:

$$ d \sin\theta = m\lambda $$

where $d$ is the slit spacing, $\theta$ is the diffraction angle, and $\lambda$ is the wavelength. Because $\theta$ depends on $\lambda$, different wavelengths emerge at different angles, spreading the spectrum across the detector plane. The angular dispersion $D$ quantifies how rapidly the angle changes with wavelength:

$$ D = \frac{d\theta}{d\lambda} = \frac{m}{d\cos\theta} $$

A related quantity, resolving power $R = \lambda/\Delta\lambda = mN$, gives the minimum wavelength difference $\Delta\lambda$ that the grating can distinguish, where $N$ is the total number of illuminated slits.

Worked Example. A diffraction grating has $5000$ lines/cm ($d = 2.00\times10^{-4}$ cm $= 2.00\times10^{-6}$ m). White light is incident normally. Find the angular separation between the sodium doublet lines $\lambda_1 = 589.0$ nm and $\lambda_2 = 589.6$ nm in first order.

Using $\sin\theta = m\lambda/d$ with $m = 1$:

$$ \sin\theta_1 = \frac{589.0\times10^{-9}}{2.00\times10^{-6}} = 0.2945 \implies \theta_1 = 17.12° $$ $$ \sin\theta_2 = \frac{589.6\times10^{-9}}{2.00\times10^{-6}} = 0.2948 \implies \theta_2 = 17.14° $$

Angular separation: $\Delta\theta \approx 0.02°$. This tiny separation demands high resolving power $R = \lambda/\Delta\lambda = 589.0/0.6 \approx 982$, achievable with at least $N = 982$ illuminated lines.

Key Theorem. The Rayleigh criterion for a grating states that two wavelengths are just resolved when the principal maximum of one coincides with the first minimum of the other, yielding $R = mN$. Increasing either the diffraction order or the number of slits proportionally improves resolution.

Lab Exercise. Illuminate a 600 lines/mm grating with a mercury lamp. Measure $\theta$ for the green line ($\lambda = 546.1$ nm) in orders $m = 1, 2, 3$. Compute $D$ at each order and verify that angular dispersion scales linearly with $m$. Record whether the second-order sodium doublet ($\Delta\lambda = 0.6$ nm) is resolved, and compare your observation against the predicted resolving power $R = mN$.
