🍃 Shear Deformation

🍃 Shear Deformation

Definition. Shear deformation occurs when equal and opposite forces act parallel to opposite faces of an object, causing successive layers to slide relative to one another without a change in volume. Quantitatively, the shear strain $\phi$ is defined as the lateral displacement $\Delta x$ of one face relative to the opposite face, divided by the separation $L$ between those faces:

$$ \phi = \frac{\Delta x}{L} $$

The corresponding shear stress $\tau$ is the tangential force $F_\parallel$ applied per unit area $A$ of the face:

$$ \tau = \frac{F_\parallel}{A} $$

For small deformations in a linear elastic material, stress and strain are proportional through the shear modulus (or modulus of rigidity) $S$:

$$ \tau = S\,\phi \quad \Longrightarrow \quad \frac{F_\parallel}{A} = S\,\frac{\Delta x}{L} $$

The SI unit of $S$ is the pascal (Pa). Typical values range from $\sim 10^{9}$ Pa for soft biological tissue composites to $\sim 80 \times 10^{9}$ Pa for structural steel.

Worked Example. A steel plate of thickness $L = 2.0\,\text{cm}$ and face area $A = 0.50\,\text{m}^2$ is subjected to a tangential force $F_\parallel = 4.0 \times 10^6\,\text{N}$. Given $S_{\text{steel}} = 8.0 \times 10^{10}\,\text{Pa}$, find the lateral displacement $\Delta x$.

First, compute the shear stress:

$$ \tau = \frac{F_\parallel}{A} = \frac{4.0 \times 10^6}{0.50} = 8.0 \times 10^6\,\text{Pa} $$

Then apply Hooke's law for shear:

$$ \Delta x = \frac{\tau\, L}{S} = \frac{(8.0 \times 10^6)(0.020)}{8.0 \times 10^{10}} = 2.0 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{m} = 2.0\,\mu\text{m} $$

Key Theorem. In the linear elastic regime, the shear modulus $S$ is related to Young's modulus $E$ and Poisson's ratio $\nu$ by:

$$ S = \frac{E}{2(1 + \nu)} $$

This identity reflects that shear and tensile deformations are not independent material responses; they arise from the same interatomic bonding forces and are fully characterized by any two of the three elastic constants $\{E,\,\nu,\,S\}$.

Lab Exercise. Obtain a block of modeling clay with known dimensions. Apply a measured horizontal force using a calibrated spring scale to the top face while the bottom face is fixed. Measure the lateral displacement $\Delta x$ with a ruler. Compute the experimental shear modulus $S_{\text{exp}}$ and compare it to published values. Discuss sources of systematic error, including non-uniform force distribution and boundary-layer effects near the edges.
