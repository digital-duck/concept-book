🌱 Momentum

🌱 Momentum

Definition. The linear momentum of a particle is a vector quantity defined as the product of its mass and velocity:

$$ \vec{p} = m\vec{v} $$

where $m$ is the invariant mass (kg) and $\vec{v}$ is the instantaneous velocity (m/s). The SI unit of momentum is kg·m/s, equivalent to N·s. Momentum is an extensive property: for a system of $N$ particles, the total momentum is the vector sum $\vec{P} = \sum_{i=1}^{N} m_i \vec{v}_i$. Newton's second law is most generally stated in terms of momentum:

$$ \vec{F}_{\text{net}} = \frac{d\vec{p}}{dt} $$

This form reduces to $\vec{F} = m\vec{a}$ only when mass is constant, making the momentum formulation fundamental to relativistic and variable-mass systems.

Worked Example. A 0.145 kg baseball traveling at $\vec{v}_i = 40.0\ \hat{x}\ \text{m/s}$ is struck by a bat and leaves at $\vec{v}_f = -55.0\ \hat{x}\ \text{m/s}$ (reversing direction). Compute the change in momentum and the average force if contact lasts $\Delta t = 1.20 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{s}$.

$$ \Delta \vec{p} = m(\vec{v}_f - \vec{v}_i) = (0.145)(-55.0 - 40.0)\,\hat{x} = -13.8\ \hat{x}\ \text{kg·m/s} $$ $$ \vec{F}_{\text{avg}} = \frac{\Delta \vec{p}}{\Delta t} = \frac{-13.8}{1.20\times10^{-3}}\,\hat{x} \approx -1.15\times10^4\ \hat{x}\ \text{N} $$

The magnitude $|\vec{F}_{\text{avg}}| \approx 11.5\ \text{kN}$, directed opposite to the initial motion.

Key Theorem — Conservation of Linear Momentum. For an isolated system (no net external force), $\frac{d\vec{P}}{dt} = 0$, hence $\vec{P} = \text{const}$. This follows directly from Newton's third law: internal forces occur in equal and opposite pairs, so their contributions to $d\vec{P}/dt$ cancel identically.

Lab Exercise. Using a linear air track, collide two gliders of measured masses $m_1$ and $m_2$ equipped with photogates. Record velocities before and after both an elastic collision (spring bumpers) and an inelastic collision (Velcro pads). Compute $\vec{P}_{\text{before}}$ and $\vec{P}_{\text{after}}$ for each trial. Quantify the percentage discrepancy and identify sources of momentum non-conservation (friction, air resistance) by comparing the two collision types.
