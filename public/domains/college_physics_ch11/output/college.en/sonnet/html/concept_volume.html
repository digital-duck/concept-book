🌱 Volume

🌱 Volume

Definition. Volume is the measure of the three-dimensional extent of a region in space. For a continuous body, it quantifies the amount of space the object occupies and is a scalar quantity. In SI units, volume is measured in cubic meters ($\text{m}^3$), though liters ($\text{L}$, where $1\ \text{L} = 10^{-3}\ \text{m}^3$) are common in laboratory contexts. For a fluid or gas contained within a vessel, the volume $V$ is determined by the geometry of the container. For a compressible substance, volume is a thermodynamic state variable related to pressure and temperature through an equation of state.

For simple geometric solids, volume is computed analytically. A rectangular prism with dimensions $\ell$, $w$, and $h$ has volume $V = \ell w h$. A sphere of radius $r$ has volume

$$ V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3. $$

A cylinder of base radius $r$ and height $h$ has volume $V = \pi r^2 h$.

Worked Example. A spherical water tank has an inner diameter of $2.00\ \text{m}$. Determine the volume of water it can hold.

Solution. The radius is $r = 1.00\ \text{m}$. Applying the sphere formula:

$$ V = \frac{4}{3}\pi (1.00\ \text{m})^3 = \frac{4\pi}{3}\ \text{m}^3 \approx 4.19\ \text{m}^3. $$

Since $1\ \text{m}^3 = 1000\ \text{L}$, the tank holds approximately $4190\ \text{L}$ of water.

Key Theorem (Cavalieri's Principle). If two solids have equal cross-sectional areas at every height, they have equal volumes. Formally, if $A_1(z) = A_2(z)$ for all $z \in [0, h]$, then

$$ V_1 = \int_0^h A_1(z)\, dz = \int_0^h A_2(z)\, dz = V_2. $$

This principle underlies the integral definition of volume and justifies computing volumes of irregular solids by slicing.

Lab Exercise. Using a graduated cylinder, measure the volume of an irregularly shaped metal object by water displacement: record the initial water level $V_i$, submerge the object, and record the final level $V_f$. The object's volume is $V = V_f - V_i$. Compare this result with a geometric estimate based on caliper measurements of the object's approximate dimensions and compute the percent discrepancy.
