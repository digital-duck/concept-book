🍃 Density

🍃 Density

Definition. The density of a substance is defined as its mass per unit volume. For a homogeneous sample of mass $m$ occupying volume $V$, the density is

$$ \rho = \frac{m}{V} $$

with SI units of kilograms per cubic meter ($\text{kg/m}^3$). For non-uniform materials, density is defined locally as the limit

$$ \rho = \lim_{\Delta V \to 0} \frac{\Delta m}{\Delta V} $$

ensuring the definition applies even when composition varies spatially. Density depends on the material and its thermodynamic state (temperature and pressure), not on the size of the sample.

Worked Example. A rectangular aluminum block measures $5.00 \text{ cm} \times 3.00 \text{ cm} \times 2.00 \text{ cm}$ and has a mass of $81.0 \text{ g}$. Determine its density and identify whether the value is consistent with pure aluminum ($\rho_\text{Al} = 2700 \text{ kg/m}^3$).

First, compute the volume:

$$ V = (0.0500)(0.0300)(0.0200) \text{ m}^3 = 3.00 \times 10^{-5} \text{ m}^3 $$

Convert the mass: $m = 81.0 \text{ g} = 0.0810 \text{ kg}$.

Then:

$$ \rho = \frac{0.0810 \text{ kg}}{3.00 \times 10^{-5} \text{ m}^3} = 2700 \text{ kg/m}^3 $$

The result matches the tabulated value for aluminum, confirming the identification.

Key Theorem — Archimedes' Principle. When an object is fully or partially submerged in a fluid of density $\rho_f$, the upward force exerted by the fluid equals the weight of the displaced fluid:

$$ F_b = \rho_f \, g \, V_\text{sub} $$

where $V_\text{sub}$ is the submerged volume. This result follows directly from the definition of density: the displaced fluid has mass $m_f = \rho_f V_\text{sub}$, and its weight $m_f g$ is precisely the force the fluid exerts on the object. An object floats when $\rho_\text{object} \leq \rho_f$, since full submersion is not required to balance gravity.

Lab Exercise. Using a graduated cylinder, a balance, and tap water ($\rho_w = 1000 \text{ kg/m}^3$), determine the density of an irregularly shaped rock via water displacement. Record the initial water volume $V_1$, the final volume $V_2$ after submerging the rock, and its mass $m$. Calculate $\rho = m/(V_2 - V_1)$ and compare your result to reference values for common silicate minerals. Estimate your percent error and identify the dominant source of uncertainty.
