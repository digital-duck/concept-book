🍃 Pressure Depth Relation

🍃 Pressure Depth Relation

Definition. In a static fluid of uniform density $\rho$, the absolute pressure $P$ at a depth $h$ below a free surface where the pressure is $P_0$ is given by

$$ P = P_0 + \rho g h, $$

where $g$ is the magnitude of the gravitational acceleration. This result follows directly from the condition of hydrostatic equilibrium: for any thin horizontal fluid layer of thickness $dh$, the net upward pressure force must balance the downward weight of the layer. Formally, considering a fluid column of cross-sectional area $A$,

$$ P(h + dh)\,A - P(h)\,A = \rho g A\, dh \implies \frac{dP}{dh} = \rho g. $$

Integrating from the surface downward yields the linear pressure–depth relation. Note that pressure depends only on depth, not on the horizontal position or the shape of the container.

Worked Example. A scuba diver descends to a depth of $h = 30\,\text{m}$ in seawater ($\rho = 1025\,\text{kg/m}^3$). Atmospheric pressure at the surface is $P_0 = 1.013 \times 10^5\,\text{Pa}$. Find the absolute pressure experienced by the diver.

$$ P = P_0 + \rho g h = 1.013 \times 10^5 + (1025)(9.81)(30) $$ $$ P = 1.013 \times 10^5 + 3.017 \times 10^5 = 4.03 \times 10^5\,\text{Pa} \approx 3.98\,\text{atm}. $$

The pressure in excess of atmospheric is $\rho g h \approx 3.0 \times 10^5\,\text{Pa}$, roughly three additional atmospheres. This is why pressure equalization in the ears becomes necessary even at moderate depths.

Key Theorem (Hydrostatic Invariance). In a connected static fluid, all points at the same depth are at the same pressure, regardless of the geometry of the vessel. This follows because any two points at equal depth are connected by a horizontal path along which $dP/dh = 0$, so no pressure difference can develop along that path.

Lab Exercise. Fill a tall graduated cylinder with water and attach pressure sensors at depths of $0.10\,\text{m}$, $0.20\,\text{m}$, and $0.30\,\text{m}$. Record the pressure reading in excess of atmospheric at each depth and plot that excess pressure versus $h$. Determine the slope of the best-fit line and compare it to the theoretical value $\rho g = (1000)(9.81) = 9810\,\text{Pa/m}$. Assess whether the relationship is linear and identify possible sources of systematic error, such as temperature gradients or sensor calibration drift.
