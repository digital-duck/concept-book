🍃 Portable

🍃 Portable

Everyday example. Think about a suitcase. You can carry it from your bedroom to the car, from the car to the airport, and from the airport to a hotel across the world. The suitcase travels with you. That idea of "able to be carried" is exactly what the word portable means.

Picture this. The word portable is built from two smaller pieces:

  • port — a Latin root meaning to carry
  • -able — a suffix meaning capable of being

Put them together: carry + capable of being = capable of being carried.

You can see the same root port hiding in other English words: a porter carries your bags at a hotel; a port is a harbor where ships carry goods; to transport something means to carry it from one place to another.

Simple rule. Whenever you spot the root port in an English word, ask yourself: is something being carried here? The answer is almost always yes. The suffix -able then tells you the carrying is possible.

$$ \underbrace{\text{port}}_{\text{carry}} + \underbrace{\text{-able}}_{\text{capable of}} = \text{portable} $$

Try it yourself. Look at the word import. The prefix im- means into. Using what you just learned about port, what do you think import means? Write your answer in one sentence, then check a dictionary to see how close you were.
