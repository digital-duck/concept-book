🍃 Predict

Have you ever looked at a cloud pattern and said, "It looks like it's going to rain later"? That moment is prediction in action! Predicting simply means using what we already know (our past observations) combined with our understanding of how things work (patterns) to make an educated guess about something that has not happened yet. It’s not magic; it’s thoughtful reasoning.

Picture this: Imagine a conveyor belt at the local grocery store. You see three items—apples, bananas, apples. If you are asked what item comes next, your brain immediately suggests 'bananas,' because you have identified a repeating pattern (A-B-A... B). This ability to spot patterns is the core of prediction. We don't know for sure that the sequence must repeat, but based on evidence, it is our best guess.

The simple rule for making predictions is: Observe the past $\rightarrow$ Identify the pattern $\rightarrow$ Extrapolate (meaning you extend or project that pattern forward in time). When we predict, we are essentially creating a temporary model of what might happen next, using only evidence from before.

Try it yourself! Look out your window right now. Describe something natural—like how far down the street cars usually travel, or how long it takes for the shadow of that tree to move across the pavement. Based on your observations, make a prediction about where that shadow will be in five minutes.
