🍃 Pre-

🍃 Pre-

Have you ever noticed that some words feel like they have little pieces attached to them? These extra pieces are called prefixes. A prefix is simply a small group of letters—like a tiny time machine dial—that gets stuck onto the beginning of a main word, or what we call the *root*. Today, we are looking at the prefix $\text{pre-}$. In plain words, $\text{pre-}$ always means "before." For instance, if you know what 'dict' means (to say), then the word predict literally means to "say before" something happens.

Picture this: Think of time moving from left to right on a line. When we add $\text{pre-}$, we are mentally stepping backward along that line—we are talking about an event or state that exists *prior* to the main action. It’s like looking at a photograph taken moments before a big party starts, capturing what was there beforehand.

The simple rule to remember is this: Whenever you see $\text{pre-}$ attached to any root word, take a deep breath and think "before." This prefix helps us build complex meanings without needing new words—it just shifts the timing! For example, if 'view' means to look at something, then preview means to look at it *before* it is actually shown.
