🍃 Re-

🍃 Re-

Have you ever heard someone say they need to read a chapter, but realize they have to go back and re-read it? That little prefix, re-, is what gives us that extra layer of meaning. It doesn't change the core word; instead, it simply tells us to do that action again. Think about baking: if you need to bake bread twice, you are going to re-bake it. We use this powerful little marker whenever we want to signal a return or repetition.

Picture this prefix as an arrow pointing backward in time. When you see re-, imagine drawing an arrow that loops back to the beginning of the action. If your initial action is 'write,' and you need to correct it, you are going to re-write it—you are starting the writing process over again. It’s a signal for renewal or repetition, like hitting the "redo" button on a computer program.

The simple rule to remember is this: When re- attaches to a base word (like view), it means you must perform that action once more (review). The concept of 'doing something again' is always linked directly by this prefix. It’s one of the most common prefixes in English, helping us understand how we often revisit information!

Now, let’s try it together! Think about the word write. If you had to perform that action a second time because your first attempt was messy, what would the new word be? Knowing this small prefix unlocks dozens of other words and helps you understand concepts like re-cycle or re-view.
