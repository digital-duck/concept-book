🌱 Newtons Third Law

🌱 Newton's Third Law

Definition. Newton's Third Law of Motion states that for every action force exerted by object A on object B, there exists a reaction force of equal magnitude and opposite direction exerted by object B on object A. Formally, if $\vec{F}_{AB}$ denotes the force that A exerts on B, then

$$ \vec{F}_{AB} = -\vec{F}_{BA} $$

These two forces are called an action-reaction pair. Several properties are essential to understand correctly: the paired forces act on different objects simultaneously, they are always equal in magnitude regardless of the masses or accelerations of the objects involved, and they cannot cancel each other because cancellation requires forces acting on the same object.

Worked Example. A sprinter of mass $m = 70\,\text{kg}$ pushes backward against the starting blocks with a horizontal force of $F = 420\,\text{N}$. Identify the action-reaction pair and determine the sprinter's initial acceleration.

By Newton's Third Law, if the sprinter exerts $F_{\text{sprinter on blocks}} = 420\,\text{N}$ backward on the blocks, the blocks exert $F_{\text{blocks on sprinter}} = 420\,\text{N}$ forward on the sprinter. Applying Newton's Second Law to the sprinter alone (the only force relevant to their acceleration is the reaction force acting on them):

$$ a = \frac{F_{\text{blocks on sprinter}}}{m} = \frac{420\,\text{N}}{70\,\text{kg}} = 6.0\,\text{m/s}^2 $$

The sprinter accelerates forward at $6.0\,\text{m/s}^2$, while the Earth-block system experiences a negligible rearward acceleration due to its vastly greater mass.

Key Theorem. Newton's Third Law is a direct consequence of the conservation of linear momentum in an isolated two-body system. For a system comprising only A and B, the internal forces satisfy $\vec{F}_{AB} + \vec{F}_{BA} = \vec{0}$, so the total momentum $\vec{p}_{\text{total}} = \vec{p}_A + \vec{p}_B$ remains constant. This makes the Third Law a cornerstone connecting kinematics, dynamics, and conservation principles.

Lab Exercise. Mount two force sensors on low-friction carts of unequal mass ($m_1 = 0.50\,\text{kg}$, $m_2 = 1.00\,\text{kg}$) on a level track. Push the carts together briefly and record both force-versus-time traces simultaneously. Verify that the magnitudes are equal at every instant and that the impulses $\int F\,dt$ delivered to each cart satisfy $|\Delta \vec{p}_1| = |\Delta \vec{p}_2|$, confirming the Third Law quantitatively.
