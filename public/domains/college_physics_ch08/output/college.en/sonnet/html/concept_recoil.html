🌸 Recoil

🌸 Recoil

Definition. Recoil is the backward motion experienced by an object when it expels mass in the forward direction, a direct consequence of the conservation of linear momentum. For an isolated system (net external force equal to zero), total momentum is conserved. If two objects are initially at rest, the total initial momentum is zero, so after one object expels mass, the remaining system must acquire an equal and opposite momentum so that the vector sum remains zero:

$$ m_1 \vec{v}_1 + m_2 \vec{v}_2 = 0 $$

More precisely, if object 1 has mass $m_1$ and recoils with velocity $\vec{v}_1$, while the expelled mass $m_2$ moves with velocity $\vec{v}_2$, then:

$$ m_1 \vec{v}_1 = -m_2 \vec{v}_2 \implies \vec{v}_1 = -\frac{m_2}{m_1}\vec{v}_2 $$

Worked Example. A rifle of mass $M = 4.0\ \text{kg}$ fires a bullet of mass $m = 0.010\ \text{kg}$ at a muzzle velocity of $v_b = 600\ \text{m/s}$ horizontally. Find the recoil speed of the rifle.

The system (rifle + bullet) is initially at rest, so $p_i = 0$. Applying conservation of momentum:

$$ 0 = M v_r + m v_b $$ $$ v_r = -\frac{m v_b}{M} = -\frac{(0.010)(600)}{4.0} = -1.5\ \text{m/s} $$

The rifle recoils at $1.5\ \text{m/s}$ opposite to the bullet's direction. The negative sign confirms the backward motion.

Key Theorem. The internal forces that propel the bullet forward act equally and oppositely on the rifle, by Newton's third law. Over the brief firing interval $\Delta t$, these internal forces contribute no net impulse to the system as a whole, so total momentum is preserved. Formally, since $\sum \vec{F}_{\text{ext}} = 0$ for the isolated system, $\Delta \vec{p}_{\text{total}} = 0$, regardless of the magnitude or time profile of the internal forces. This makes recoil independent of the details of the propulsion mechanism.

Lab Exercise. Place a dynamics cart on a frictionless track. Load it with a spring-launched projectile. Release the spring and measure the cart's recoil velocity with a motion sensor. Compare the measured recoil speed to the prediction from $m_1 v_1 = -m_2 v_2$, and calculate the percentage discrepancy. Identify at least two sources of systematic error in your experimental setup.
